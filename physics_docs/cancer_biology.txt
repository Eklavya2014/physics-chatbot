1. WHAT IS CANCER?
-------------------
Cancer is a group of diseases characterised by uncontrolled cell growth, invasion
of surrounding tissues, and potential spread to distant organs (metastasis).
~18 million new cancer cases/year globally; ~10 million deaths/year.
Most common: Lung (~2.2M), breast (~2.3M), colorectal (~1.9M), prostate (~1.4M).

Key Concepts:
- Neoplasm    : Abnormal mass of tissue from uncontrolled cell growth.
- Benign      : Non-invasive; encapsulated; does not metastasise; usually curable.
- Malignant   : Invasive; can metastasise; may be fatal if untreated.
- Carcinoma   : Cancer of epithelial cells (~80% of all cancers).
- Sarcoma     : Cancer of connective tissue (bone, muscle, fat).
- Leukaemia   : Blood cell cancer; liquid tumour.
- Lymphoma    : Cancer of lymphoid tissue (Hodgkin vs Non-Hodgkin).
- Metastasis  : Spread of cancer from primary site to distant organs.
  Most common metastatic sites: Liver, lungs, brain, bone.

Cancer Causes (Aetiology):
- Tobacco          : ~22% of all cancer deaths. Lung, throat, bladder, kidney.
- Infection        : ~13% (HPV → cervical; H. pylori → gastric; HBV/HCV → liver).
- UV radiation     : Skin cancers (melanoma, BCC, SCC).
- Ionising radiation: Leukaemia, thyroid cancer.
- Chemical carcinogens: Asbestos (mesothelioma); benzene (leukaemia); aflatoxin (liver).
- Hereditary       : 5–10% of cancers; BRCA1/2, Lynch syndrome, Li-Fraumeni.
- Obesity/diet     : Colorectal, endometrial, oesophageal, kidney cancers.
- Alcohol          : Liver, colorectal, breast, oropharyngeal cancers.

2. HALLMARKS OF CANCER (Hanahan & Weinberg)
---------------------------------------------
Original 6 Hallmarks (2000):
  1. Sustaining proliferative signalling (self-sufficient growth signals)
  2. Evading growth suppressors (p53, Rb pathway bypass)
  3. Resisting cell death (anti-apoptotic)
  4. Enabling replicative immortality (telomerase reactivation)
  5. Inducing angiogenesis (VEGF production)
  6. Activating invasion and metastasis (EMT, matrix metalloproteinases)

Additional Hallmarks (2011):
  7. Deregulating cellular energetics (Warburg effect)
  8. Avoiding immune destruction (PD-L1 upregulation)

Emerging Hallmarks (2022):
  9. Unlocking phenotypic plasticity (lineage switching, dedifferentiation)
  10. Non-mutational epigenetic reprogramming
  11. Polymorphic microbiomes
  12. Senescent cells in TME

Enabling Characteristics:
  Genome instability and mutation; Tumour-promoting inflammation.

3. ONCOGENES & TUMOUR SUPPRESSORS
------------------------------------
Proto-oncogenes → Oncogenes (gain-of-function mutations; dominant):
  Mechanism: Point mutation (KRAS G12D/V), amplification (HER2, MYC),
  translocation (BCR-ABL in CML; c-MYC in Burkitt; PML-RARalpha in APL).
  Common oncogenes:
    RAS (KRAS, NRAS, HRAS): GTPase; mutated in ~30% all cancers; activates MAPK, PI3K.
    MYC             : Transcription factor; activates cell cycle, ribosome biogenesis.
    HER2 (ERBB2)    : RTK; amplified in ~20% breast cancer; target of trastuzumab.
    EGFR            : RTK; mutations in lung adenocarcinoma; target of erlotinib.
    BCR-ABL         : Constitutively active tyrosine kinase; CML; target of imatinib.
    BRAF V600E      : Kinase; ~50% melanomas, ~10% CRC; target of vemurafenib.
    CDK4/6          : Cell cycle kinase; amplified in some cancers; target of palbociclib.
    PI3K (PIH3CA)   : Lipid kinase; mutations in ~30% breast cancer.

Tumour Suppressor Genes (loss-of-function; Knudson's two-hit hypothesis):
    TP53            : "Guardian of the genome"; mutated in >50% all cancers.
                      Cell cycle arrest, apoptosis, senescence, DNA repair.
    RB1 (Retinoblastoma): G1/S checkpoint; E2F transcription factor regulation.
    BRCA1/BRCA2     : DNA repair (HDR); germline mutation → hereditary breast/ovarian.
    APC             : Wnt pathway; destruction complex component; CRC.
    PTEN            : Lipid phosphatase; opposes PI3K/Akt; PTEN hamartoma syndromes.
    VHL             : HIF degradation; mutated in renal cell carcinoma → VEGF ↑.
    CDKN2A (p16/p14ARF): CDK inhibitor + p53 activator; melanoma, lung.

4. METASTASIS CASCADE
----------------------
Steps of metastasis:
  1. Local invasion: EMT; loss of E-cadherin; gain of vimentin, N-cadherin; MMP-mediated
     ECM degradation; invadopodia formation.
  2. Intravasation: Enter blood or lymphatic vessels. Circulating tumour cells (CTCs).
  3. Survival in circulation: Platelet cloaking; immune evasion; resistance to anoikis.
  4. Arrest: Physical trapping or receptor-mediated adhesion to endothelium.
  5. Extravasation: Exit the vessel at distant site.
  6. Colonisation: Adaptation to foreign microenvironment; establish metastatic niche.
     Organotropism: Breast → bone (CXCR4/CXCL12 axis), lung, liver, brain.
     Seed and soil hypothesis (Paget, 1889): Tumour cells (seeds) spread randomly but
     only grow where microenvironment (soil) is permissive.

Liquid Biopsy:
  Detection of CTCs, circulating tumour DNA (ctDNA), or exosomes from blood.
  ctDNA: Detects mutations (KRAS, BRAF, EGFR, TP53) in blood → early detection,
  treatment monitoring, resistance detection. Sensitivity improving with ddPCR and NGS.

5. CANCER GENOMICS
-------------------
Mutational Signatures:
  Distinct patterns of somatic mutations arise from specific DNA-damaging processes.
  COSMIC database: >60 validated signatures.
  Signature 4: APOBEC deaminases (C>T in TCA context) → breast, bladder, lung.
  Signature 7: UV damage (C>T at dipyrimidines) → melanoma, skin cancers.
  Signature 4: Tobacco carcinogens → C>A transversions → lung cancer.
  Signature 3: BRCA1/2 dysfunction (HRD) → ovarian, breast; predicts PARP inhibitor response.
  Microsatellite instability (MSI-H/dMMR): MMR deficiency → hypermutation →
  immunogenic tumours → responds to pembrolizumab (pan-tumour approval 2017).
  Tumour mutational burden (TMB): High TMB predicts immune checkpoint inhibitor response.

Cancer Evolution (Clonal Evolution):
  Somatic mutations accumulate in normal cells (field cancerisation).
  Initiating driver mutation → clonal expansion → further driver mutations →
  subclonal diversification → selection of fit clones.
  Intratumour heterogeneity (ITH): Different cells within same tumour have different
  mutations → drug resistance; parallel evolution of resistance.
  Phylogenetic trees from multi-region sampling reconstruct tumour evolution (TRACERx study).

6. CANCER METABOLISM
---------------------
Warburg Effect:
  Cancer cells preferentially use aerobic glycolysis (glucose → lactate) even with O2.
  Advantages: Rapid ATP; biosynthetic precursors (nucleotides, amino acids, lipids);
  acidic microenvironment inhibits immune cells.
  Oncometabolites: IDH1/2 mutations → 2-hydroxyglutarate (2-HG) → inhibits alpha-KG-
  dependent dioxygenases → blocks differentiation → glioma, AML. Enasidenib/ivosidenib
  target mutant IDH2/IDH1.

Glutamine Addiction:
  Many cancers require glutamine as nitrogen and carbon source.
  Glutaminolysis: Glutamine → glutamate → alpha-KG → TCA (anaplerosis).
  MYC-amplified cancers particularly glutamine-addicted.

7. TARGETED THERAPY & RESISTANCE
----------------------------------
Targeted Therapies (selected):
Drug              | Target           | Indication          | Mechanism
------------------|------------------|---------------------|------------------
Imatinib          | BCR-ABL          | CML                 | ATP-competitive TKI
Gefitinib/Erlotinib| EGFR            | NSCLC (EGFR-mut)    | EGFR TKI (1st gen)
Osimertinib       | EGFR T790M       | NSCLC               | 3rd gen EGFR TKI
Vemurafenib       | BRAF V600E       | Melanoma            | BRAF inhibitor
Trastuzumab       | HER2             | HER2+ breast        | Monoclonal Ab
Palbociclib       | CDK4/6           | ER+ breast cancer   | Cell cycle arrest
Olaparib          | PARP             | BRCA-mut ovarian    | Synthetic lethality
Venetoclax        | BCL-2            | CLL, AML            | Pro-apoptotic
Pembrolizumab     | PD-1             | Multiple cancers    | Immune checkpoint

Resistance Mechanisms:
  On-target: Secondary mutation in drug target (EGFR T790M → C797S).
  Off-target (bypass): Activation of alternative pathway (MET amplification bypasses
  EGFR inhibition; RAS amplification bypasses BRAF inhibition).
  Downstream activation: Amplification of downstream effectors (PI3K, AKT mutations).
  Phenotypic plasticity: EMT; small cell transformation in EGFR-mut NSCLC on osimertinib.
  Tumour heterogeneity: Pre-existing resistant clones selected by therapy.

[PRECISION ONCOLOGY] Molecular tumour boards review NGS data to match patients
with targeted therapies regardless of tumour histology (basket/umbrella trials).
NTRK fusions: Rare; occur across many tumour types → larotrectinib/entrectinib
(TRK inhibitors) → ~75% response rate pan-tumour. First truly histology-agnostic approval (2018).
