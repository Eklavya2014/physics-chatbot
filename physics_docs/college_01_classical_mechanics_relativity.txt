CHAPTER: ADVANCED CLASSICAL MECHANICS

LAGRANGIAN MECHANICS:
The Lagrangian: L = T - V (T = kinetic energy, V = potential energy)
Euler-Lagrange equations: d/dt(∂L/∂q̇ᵢ) - ∂L/∂qᵢ = 0
qᵢ = generalized coordinates, q̇ᵢ = generalized velocities
Generalized momentum: pᵢ = ∂L/∂q̇ᵢ
Generalized force: Qᵢ = ∂L/∂qᵢ
Advantage: works in any coordinate system; constraints easily handled.

HAMILTONIAN MECHANICS:
Hamiltonian: H = Σpᵢq̇ᵢ - L = T + V (total energy, in terms of p and q)
Hamilton's equations: q̇ᵢ = ∂H/∂pᵢ, ṗᵢ = -∂H/∂qᵢ
Poisson bracket: {A,B} = Σ(∂A/∂qᵢ·∂B/∂pᵢ - ∂A/∂pᵢ·∂B/∂qᵢ)
Conservation: if ∂H/∂t = 0, then H (energy) is conserved.

CENTRAL FORCE PROBLEM:
Force directed toward/away from fixed point.
Angular momentum L = mr²θ̇ = constant (conserved)
Effective potential: U_eff = V(r) + L²/(2mr²)
Orbit equation: d²u/dθ² + u = -m/(L²u²) F(1/u) where u = 1/r

KEPLERS LAWS FROM NEWTON'S LAW:
For F = -GMm/r²:
Orbit: r = L²/(mk) × 1/(1 + e cos θ) [conic section]
k = GMm, e = eccentricity.
e = 0: circle, 0 < e < 1: ellipse, e = 1: parabola, e > 1: hyperbola.
T² = 4π²a³/(GM) naturally derived.

RIGID BODY DYNAMICS:
Inertia tensor (Iᵢⱼ) generalizes moment of inertia.
Euler's equations: I₁ω̇₁ - (I₂-I₃)ω₂ω₃ = τ₁ [and cyclic]
Principal axes: axes along which inertia tensor is diagonal.
Angular momentum: L⃗ = I·ω⃗ (matrix multiplication)
L⃗ not necessarily parallel to ω⃗ unless ω along principal axis.

SMALL OSCILLATIONS:
Near stable equilibrium, V(q) ≈ V(q₀) + ½k(q-q₀)²
Normal modes: independent oscillations of coupled system.
Normal mode frequencies from secular equation: det(K - ω²M) = 0

CHAOS AND NONLINEAR DYNAMICS:
- Sensitive dependence on initial conditions.
- Lyapunov exponent measures rate of divergence.
- Strange attractors in phase space.
- Period doubling route to chaos.
- Examples: double pendulum, three-body problem.

CHAPTER: SPECIAL THEORY OF RELATIVITY

POSTULATES OF SPECIAL RELATIVITY (Einstein, 1905):
1. Laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames of reference.
2. Speed of light in vacuum is c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s, the same for all observers regardless of motion of source or observer.

CONSEQUENCES:

Lorentz Factor: γ = 1/√(1 - v²/c²) ≥ 1

TIME DILATION:
Moving clocks run slow.
Δt = γΔt₀ (Δt₀ = proper time in rest frame)
"Twin paradox": traveling twin ages less than stationary twin.

LENGTH CONTRACTION:
Moving objects appear shorter in direction of motion.
L = L₀/γ (L₀ = proper length in rest frame)
Transverse dimensions unchanged.

RELATIVITY OF SIMULTANEITY:
Events simultaneous in one frame are not simultaneous in another moving frame.

LORENTZ TRANSFORMATION:
x' = γ(x - vt)
t' = γ(t - vx/c²)
y' = y, z' = z

RELATIVISTIC VELOCITY ADDITION:
u' = (u - v)/(1 - uv/c²)
(Never exceeds c for u, v < c)

RELATIVISTIC MOMENTUM:
p = γmv = mv/√(1-v²/c²)
For v << c: p ≈ mv (classical result)

RELATIVISTIC ENERGY:
Total energy: E = γmc²
Rest energy: E₀ = mc² (most famous equation in physics!)
Kinetic energy: K = (γ-1)mc²
Energy-momentum relation: E² = (pc)² + (mc²)²
For massless particles (photon): E = pc, v = c.

MASS-ENERGY EQUIVALENCE:
E = mc² means mass and energy are interchangeable.
Nuclear reactions release energy due to mass defect.
1 u = 931.5 MeV/c²

DOPPLER EFFECT (RELATIVISTIC):
f_observed = f_source × √((1-β)/(1+β)) [for recession]
f_observed = f_source × √((1+β)/(1+β)) [for approach]
β = v/c
Redshift: z = Δλ/λ = (f_source/f_observed) - 1

SPACETIME AND MINKOWSKI SPACE:
Spacetime interval: s² = c²t² - x² - y² - z² (invariant)
Four-vector: (ct, x, y, z)
Timelike interval: s² > 0 (causally connected events)
Spacelike interval: s² < 0 (causally unconnected events)
Lightlike (null) interval: s² = 0 (connected by light signal)

GENERAL RELATIVITY (brief overview):
Einstein's 1915 theory of gravity.
- Gravity is curvature of spacetime caused by mass-energy.
- Test particle follows geodesic (shortest path in curved spacetime).
- Einstein field equations: G_μν = 8πG/c⁴ × T_μν
Predictions:
- Gravitational time dilation: clocks run slower in stronger gravity.
- Light bending near massive objects. (Confirmed 1919)
- Gravitational waves. (Confirmed by LIGO 2015)
- Black holes: regions where escape velocity > c.
- Expanding universe.
- GPS satellites must correct for both special and general relativistic time dilation.

IMPORTANT FORMULAS:
- γ = 1/√(1-v²/c²)
- t' = γt₀ (time dilation)
- L = L₀/γ (length contraction)
- E = γmc² = √((pc)² + (mc²)²)
- E₀ = mc²
- K = (γ-1)mc²
- p = γmv
- u' = (u-v)/(1-uv/c²) (velocity addition)
