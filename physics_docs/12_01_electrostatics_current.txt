CLASS 12 PHYSICS - ELECTROSTATICS AND CURRENT ELECTRICITY
==========================================================

CHAPTER: ELECTRIC CHARGES AND FIELDS

ELECTRIC CHARGE:
- Two types: positive and negative.
- Like charges repel; unlike charges attract.
- Charge is quantized: q = ne (n = integer, e = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C)
- Charge is conserved: total charge of isolated system remains constant.
- Charge is invariant: does not depend on frame of reference.

COULOMB'S LAW:
F = kq₁q₂/r² = q₁q₂/(4πε₀r²)
k = 9 × 10⁹ N·m²/C² = 1/(4πε₀)
ε₀ = 8.85 × 10⁻¹² C²/N·m² (permittivity of free space)
In medium: F = kq₁q₂/(εr²), ε = ε₀ × ε_r (ε_r = relative permittivity/dielectric constant)

ELECTRIC FIELD:
E⃗ = F⃗/q₀ (force per unit positive test charge)
Due to point charge: E = kq/r²
SI unit: N/C or V/m

ELECTRIC FIELD LINES:
- Start from positive, end at negative charges.
- Never intersect.
- Density indicates field strength.
- Perpendicular to equipotential surfaces.

ELECTRIC DIPOLE:
Two equal and opposite charges separated by distance 2a.
- Dipole moment: p⃗ = q(2a) (direction: -q to +q)
- Field on axial line: E = 2kp/r³ (for r >> a)
- Field on equatorial line: E = kp/r³ (for r >> a)
- Torque on dipole in field: τ = pE sin θ
- Potential energy: U = -pE cos θ = -p⃗·E⃗

GAUSS'S LAW:
Φ = ∮E⃗·dA⃗ = q_enclosed/ε₀
Electric flux through a closed surface = enclosed charge/ε₀.

Applications:
- Infinite line charge: E = λ/(2πε₀r) [λ = linear charge density]
- Infinite plane sheet: E = σ/(2ε₀) [σ = surface charge density]
- Uniformly charged sphere:
  Outside (r > R): E = kQ/r² (like point charge)
  Inside (r < R): E = kQr/R³ (increases with r)
  On surface: E = kQ/R² = σ/ε₀

CHAPTER: ELECTROSTATIC POTENTIAL AND CAPACITANCE

ELECTRIC POTENTIAL:
V = W/q₀ (work done per unit positive charge from infinity)
Due to point charge: V = kq/r
Potential is scalar; add algebraically for multiple charges.
SI unit: Volt (V) = J/C

POTENTIAL DIFFERENCE:
V_AB = V_A - V_B = work done per unit charge moving from B to A.
Relation: E = -dV/dr (E is in direction of decreasing V)

EQUIPOTENTIAL SURFACES:
Surfaces of constant potential.
- No work done moving charge along equipotential.
- E⃗ is always perpendicular to equipotential surfaces.
- For point charge: concentric spheres.
- For uniform field: parallel planes.

POTENTIAL ENERGY:
System of two charges: U = kq₁q₂/r
System of charges: sum of all pairwise interactions.

CONDUCTORS IN ELECTROSTATIC FIELD:
- E = 0 inside conductor.
- All charges reside on surface.
- Surface is equipotential.
- E just outside = σ/ε₀ (perpendicular to surface).
- Charges concentrate at sharp points (lightning conductor principle).

CAPACITANCE:
C = Q/V
SI unit: Farad (F)
1 F = 1 C/V

Types of capacitors:
- Parallel plate: C = ε₀A/d (A = plate area, d = separation)
- With dielectric: C = Kε₀A/d (K = dielectric constant)
- Spherical: C = 4πε₀R
- Cylindrical: C = 2πε₀L/ln(b/a)

COMBINATION OF CAPACITORS:
Series: 1/C_eff = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + 1/C₃ (charge same, voltage divides)
Parallel: C_eff = C₁ + C₂ + C₃ (voltage same, charge divides)

ENERGY STORED IN CAPACITOR:
U = Q²/2C = CV²/2 = QV/2
Energy density: u = ½ε₀E²

DIELECTRICS:
- Polar dielectric: molecules have permanent dipole moment (water).
- Non-polar dielectric: no permanent dipole; induced when E applied (glass).
- Dielectric constant K = C/C₀ (always > 1)
- Dielectric strength: maximum E field before breakdown.

EFFECT OF DIELECTRIC ON CAPACITOR:
With dielectric inserted (isolated capacitor):
- Charge Q remains same.
- Capacitance increases: C' = KC.
- Voltage decreases: V' = V/K.
- Energy decreases: U' = U/K.

CHAPTER: CURRENT ELECTRICITY (CLASS 12 EXTENDED)

DRIFT VELOCITY:
Average velocity of electrons due to applied E field.
v_d = eEτ/m (τ = relaxation time)
Current: I = neAv_d (n = electron density, A = cross-section area)
Current density: J = nev_d = σE

CONDUCTANCE AND RESISTIVITY:
Conductance: G = 1/R (Siemens, S)
Resistivity: ρ = RA/L
Conductivity: σ = 1/ρ = J/E

Temperature dependence of resistance:
R_T = R₀(1 + αT)
α = temperature coefficient of resistance (positive for metals, negative for semiconductors)

EMF AND INTERNAL RESISTANCE:
Terminal voltage: V = ε - Ir (during discharge)
V = ε + Ir (during charging)
ε = EMF of cell, r = internal resistance, I = current

KIRCHHOFF'S LAWS:
1. Junction rule (KCL): Sum of currents at junction = 0. (Conservation of charge)
2. Loop rule (KVL): Sum of EMFs = sum of voltage drops in any closed loop. (Conservation of energy)

WHEATSTONE BRIDGE:
Balanced condition: P/Q = R/S (no current through galvanometer)
Used to find unknown resistance accurately.

METRE BRIDGE:
l/(100-l) = R/S
Used to find unknown resistance using principle of Wheatstone bridge.

POTENTIOMETER:
- Used to compare EMFs, find internal resistance.
- Works on null deflection principle (no current drawn from cell being measured).
- More accurate than voltmeter.
- Comparing EMFs: ε₁/ε₂ = l₁/l₂
- Internal resistance: r = R(l₁-l₂)/l₂

POWER IN CIRCUITS:
P = VI = I²R = V²/R
Maximum power transfer: when external resistance = internal resistance.

CELLS IN COMBINATION:
Series: ε_total = ε₁ + ε₂ + ..., r_total = r₁ + r₂ + ...
Best for high resistance external circuit.
Parallel (identical cells): ε_total = ε, r_total = r/n
Best for low resistance external circuit.

IMPORTANT FORMULAS:
- F = kq₁q₂/r² (Coulomb's law)
- E = kq/r² (point charge field)
- V = kq/r (point charge potential)
- C = Q/V, C = ε₀A/d
- U = CV²/2 (energy in capacitor)
- V = ε - Ir (terminal voltage)
- Wheatstone bridge: P/Q = R/S
