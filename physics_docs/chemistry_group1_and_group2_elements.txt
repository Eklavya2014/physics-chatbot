GROUP 1 — ALKALI METALS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROUP 1 OVERVIEW
-----------------
General Properties:
  Valence electrons: 1 (ns¹ configuration).
  Common oxidation state: +1 only (very stable — achieves noble gas configuration).
  Valency: 1 (always form +1 ions).
  Highly reactive metals; reactivity INCREASES down the group.
  Soft, low-density metals. All have low melting and boiling points.
  Silver-white lustre when freshly cut; tarnish rapidly in air.
  Stored in oil (Li, Na) or kerosene (K, Rb, Cs) to prevent reaction with air/moisture.
  React vigorously with water → metal hydroxide + H₂ (flammable/explosive).
  React with O₂: Li→Li₂O (oxide); Na→Na₂O₂ (peroxide); K,Rb,Cs→MO₂ (superoxide).
  All compounds are ionic, soluble in water (exceptions: Li₂CO₃, LiF, LiOH have low solubility).
  Flame colours: Li=red; Na=yellow/orange; K=violet; Rb=red/violet; Cs=blue.

ELEMENT 1: HYDROGEN (H)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  Atomic Number    : 1
  Atomic Mass      : 1.008 amu
  Electron Config  : 1s¹
  Valence e-       : 1
  Valency          : 1
  Common Oxidation : +1 (most compounds); -1 (metal hydrides e.g. NaH)
  Group/Period     : Group 1 / Period 1 (unique — fits both Group 1 and 17)
  State at STP     : Gas (H₂, diatomic)
  Colour           : Colourless
  Melting Point    : -259.14°C (14.01 K)
  Boiling Point    : -252.87°C (20.28 K)
  Density          : 0.0899 g/L (gas)
  Electronegativity: 2.20 (Pauling)

  Key Properties:
  - Most abundant element in the universe (~75% by mass).
  - Lightest element; diatomic gas (H₂) at standard conditions.
  - Three isotopes: Protium (¹H, 99.98%), Deuterium (²H, D, 0.015%), Tritium (³H, T, radioactive, t½=12.3 yr).
  - Nonmetal despite being in Group 1; not a true alkali metal.
  - Bond with itself (H-H): Bond enthalpy 436 kJ/mol; bond length 74 pm.
  - Can act as oxidant (with metals → H₂ + metal oxide) or reductant (with halogens → HX).
  - Hydrogen bonding: Strong intermolecular force with N, O, F (lone pairs) → key in water, DNA, proteins.

  Important Compounds & Reactions:
    2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O                          (combustion; fuel cells)
    H₂ + Cl₂ → 2HCl                          (halogen reaction)
    H₂ + N₂ → 2NH₃  (400°C, 200 atm, Fe cat) (Haber-Bosch process)
    H₂O + CO (steam reforming of methane)     (industrial H₂ production)
    NaH + H₂O → NaOH + H₂                    (hydride + water)
    H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O                           (neutralisation)

  Uses: Rocket fuel (liquid H₂); fuel cells; hydrogenation (margarine, nylon); ammonia synthesis; petroleum refining (hydrocracking); potential future energy carrier.

  Hydrogen Economy: H₂ as clean fuel. Green hydrogen (electrolysis by renewables); Blue hydrogen (steam reforming + CCS); Grey hydrogen (steam reforming, no CCS). Fuel cells: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O + electricity (Gibbs free energy directly to work).

ELEMENT 3: LITHIUM (Li)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  Atomic Number    : 3
  Atomic Mass      : 6.941 amu
  Electron Config  : [He] 2s¹
  Valence e-       : 1
  Valency          : 1
  Oxidation State  : +1 only
  Group/Period     : Group 1 / Period 2
  State at STP     : Solid (metal)
  Colour           : Silver-white
  Melting Point    : 180.5°C
  Boiling Point    : 1342°C
  Density          : 0.534 g/cm³ (lightest metal; less dense than water)
  Electronegativity: 0.98

  Key Properties:
  - Lightest metal; floats on water AND oil (density 0.534 g/cm³ < 1.0 g/cm³).
  - Hardest alkali metal; highest melting point in Group 1.
  - Reacts with water (gently compared to Na): 2Li + 2H₂O → 2LiOH + H₂.
  - Reacts with O₂: 4Li + O₂ → 2Li₂O (only oxide, unlike Na/K).
  - Only alkali metal to react with N₂: 6Li + N₂ → 2Li₃N (lithium nitride).
  - Anomalous properties: Similar to Mg (diagonal relationship) — both form normal oxides; carbonate decomposes on heating; nitride stable; salts often insoluble.
  - Lithium-ion batteries: Anode=graphite (Li⁺ intercalated); cathode=LiCoO₂; high energy density; rechargeable.

  Important Compounds:
    LiOH    : Strong base; used in CO₂ scrubbers (spacecraft, submarines).
    LiCl    : Lithium chloride; hygroscopic; air drying.
    Li₂CO₃ : Lithium carbonate; psychiatric drug (bipolar disorder); glass ceramics.
    LiAlH₄ : Strong reducing agent in organic chemistry.
    Li₃N    : Lithium nitride; ionic conductor; hydrogen storage.
    BuLi    : n-Butyllithium; very strong base/nucleophile in organic synthesis.

  Uses: Lithium-ion batteries (EVs, smartphones); psychiatric medication (bipolar disorder); glass and ceramics; alloys (with Al, Mg — aerospace); nuclear fusion reactor (breeding tritium from Li-6).

ELEMENT 11: SODIUM (Na)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  Valence e-       : 1

  Oxidation State  : +1 only
  Group/Period     : Group 1 / Period 3
  State at STP     : Solid (metal)
  Colour           : Silver
  Melting Point    : 97.79°C
  Boiling Point    : 882.8°C
  Density          : 0.971 g/cm³
  Electronegativity: 0.93

  Key Properties:
  - 6th most abundant element in Earth's crust.
  - Reacts vigorously with water: 2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂ (fizzes; melts; moves on water).
  - Reacts with O₂: 2Na + O₂ → Na₂O₂ (sodium peroxide; not Na₂O like Li).
  - Burns in chlorine: 2Na + Cl₂ → 2NaCl (bright yellow flame).
  - Bright yellow flame in flame tests (most sensitive Group 1 flame colour).
  - Na⁺ is essential biological ion (action potentials, osmotic balance, Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase).
  - Liquid sodium: Used as coolant in fast breeder nuclear reactors (high thermal conductivity; low viscosity).

  Important Compounds:
    NaCl    : Table salt; essential; ionic lattice; FCC structure; NaCl mined as halite.
    NaOH   : Caustic soda; strong base; soap making; pH adjustment; produced by chloralkali process.
    Na₂CO₃ : Washing soda/soda ash; glass making; Solvay process.
    NaHCO₃ : Baking soda; antacid; leavening agent.
    Na₂SO₄ : Glauber's salt; paper making; detergents.
    Na₂O₂  : Sodium peroxide; powerful oxidant; CO₂ absorber.
    NaH    : Sodium hydride; strong base in organic synthesis; releases H₂ with water.
    NaOCl  : Sodium hypochlorite; bleach; disinfectant.

  Chloralkali Process: 2NaCl + 2H₂O → Cl₂ + H₂ + 2NaOH (electrolysis).

    KHCO₃  : Potassium bicarbonate; antacid; baking.
    KO₂    : Potassium superoxide; reacts with H₂O → KOH + O₂ (self-contained breathing apparatus).

GROUP 2 — ALKALINE EARTH METALS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GROUP 2 OVERVIEW
-----------------
  Valence electrons: 2 (ns² configuration).
  Common oxidation state: +2 only (loses both valence electrons → noble gas config).
  Valency: 2 always.
  Less reactive than Group 1; reactivity increases down group.
  Higher melting points + densities than Group 1 (charge density of M²⁺ higher).
  Harder than alkali metals.
  Flame colours: Ca=brick red; Sr=crimson red; Ba=apple green.
  React with water (Mg slowly; Ca vigorously; Ba explosively).
  Hydroxides: Be(OH)₂ = amphoteric; Mg(OH)₂ = weakly basic; Ca/Sr/Ba(OH)₂ = strong bases.
  Oxides: BeO and MgO have very high melting points (ionic lattice with high charge density).
  Anomalous relationship: Be resembles Al (diagonal relationship).

ELEMENT 4: BERYLLIUM (Be)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  Atomic Number    : 4
  Atomic Mass      : 9.012 amu
  Electron Config  : [He] 2s²
  Valence e-       : 2
  Valency          : 2
  Oxidation State  : +2 only
  Group/Period     : Group 2 / Period 2
  State at STP     : Solid (metal)
  Colour           : Steel-grey
  Melting Point    : 1287°C
  Boiling Point    : 2469°C
  Density          : 1.848 g/cm³
  Electronegativity: 1.57

  Key Properties:
  - Lightest alkaline earth metal; very high melting point for Group 2.
  - Amphoteric: Reacts with both acids AND alkalis: Be + 2NaOH → Na₂BeO₂ + H₂.
  - Resistant to air oxidation at room temp (protective BeO oxide layer).
  - Extremely toxic; all compounds toxic — causes berylliosis (chronic lung disease).
  - Anomalous (like Li): Covalent bonding tendency; forms BeF₂ (4-coordinated covalent); BeO and Be(OH)₂ amphoteric; diagonal resemblance to Al.
  - High X-ray transparency (BeO window in X-ray tubes).
  - Nuclear properties: Good moderator + reflector (low neutron absorption cross-section).
  - Be-Cu alloys (2%Be): Very strong, non-sparking, non-magnetic — specialist tools.

  Important Compounds:
    BeO     : Beryllia; very high mp (2572°C); excellent thermal conductor; electrical insulator; specialist ceramics.
    BeCl₂   : Covalent dimer (bridging Cl); Lewis acid; used as catalyst.
    Be(OH)₂ : Amphoteric hydroxide — reacts with acid and base.
    Beryl   : Be₃Al₂Si₆O₁₈; gemstone; source of Be. Varieties: Emerald (Cr³⁺), Aquamarine (Fe²⁺/³⁺).

  Uses: Aerospace structures; X-ray windows; nuclear reactors; gyroscopes; mirrors in telescopes (JWST beryllium mirrors); Be-Cu alloys.

  - Brick-red flame test colour.
  - Essential for bones, teeth, cell signalling, muscle contraction, blood clotting.
  - Ca²⁺ second messenger: Calmodulin binding → enzyme activation; IP3 pathway; excitation-contraction coupling.

  Important Compounds:
    CaCO₃  : Limestone, chalk, marble; most common natural carbonate.
    CaO    : Quicklime; made by roasting CaCO₃; reacts violently with water.

    CaC₂   : Calcium carbide; reacts with water → acetylene (C₂H₂) + Ca(OH)₂.
    CaCN₂  : Calcium cyanamide; fertiliser; nitrogenous compounds synthesis.

  Cement Chemistry:
    Portland cement: CaO + SiO₂ + Al₂O₃ + Fe₂O₃ (from limestone + clay + iron ore, calcined 1450°C) → calcium silicates + aluminates. Mix with water → hydration reactions → calcium silicate hydrate (CSH gel) → hardening.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
  Atomic Number    : 88
  Atomic Mass      : (226) amu
  Electron Config  : [Rn] 7s²
  Valence e-       : 2
  Valency          : 2
  Oxidation State  : +2 only
  Group/Period     : Group 2 / Period 7
  State at STP     : Solid (metal); radioactive
  Melting Point    : 700°C
  Density          : 5.5 g/cm³
  Electronegativity: 0.9

  Key Properties:
  - Discovered by Marie and Pierre Curie (1898) from pitchblende.
  - All isotopes radioactive. Most stable: ²²⁶Ra (t½ = 1,600 years; α + γ emitter).
  - Luminescent: Glows faintly blue-green in the dark (radioluminescence).
  - Historically used in luminous paint ("Radium girls" — occupational hazard; dial painters; jawbone cancer). Now banned; replaced by tritium/promethium.
  - Ra²⁺ mimics Ca²⁺ biologically → concentrates in bone → bone cancer + leukaemia.
  - Medical: ²²³Ra (Xofigo, radium-223 dichloride): Alpha emitter; treats bone metastases of prostate cancer (targets bone; short range of alpha particles). FDA approved 2013.
  - Decays: ²²⁶Ra → ²²²Rn (radon gas) + α.
  - Historical: Showed radiation could ionise air; disproved vitalism; led to nuclear physics.
