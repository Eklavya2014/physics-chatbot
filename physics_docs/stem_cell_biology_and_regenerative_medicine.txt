1. STEM CELL FUNDAMENTALS
--------------------------
Stem cells are undifferentiated cells with two defining properties:
  1. Self-renewal: Ability to divide and produce more stem cells indefinitely.
  2. Potency: Ability to give rise to specialised cell types.

Potency Hierarchy (recap):
  Totipotent  : Zygote + first 2–4 cells → can form entire organism including placenta.
  Pluripotent : ESCs, iPSCs → can form all embryonic germ layers but not placenta.
  Multipotent : HSCs, MSCs → limited lineage potential.
  Bipotent    : Two cell types (e.g., common lymphoid progenitor → B or T cells).
  Unipotent   : Single lineage only (spermatogonial stem cells → sperm).

Types of Stem Cells:
  Embryonic stem cells (ESCs): Derived from inner cell mass of blastocyst (day 5–6). Pluripotent. Ethical controversy → animal ESCs widely used; human ESC research regulated.
  Induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs): Adult cells reprogrammed by Yamanaka factors (OCT4, SOX2, KLF4, c-MYC). Nobel Prize 2012. Same pluripotency as ESCs without embryo destruction.
  Adult (somatic) stem cells: Tissue-resident; maintain homeostasis + repair. More restricted potency.
    Haematopoietic stem cells (HSCs): Long-term (LT-HSC) → all blood lineages.
    Mesenchymal stem/stromal cells (MSCs): Bone marrow, adipose, cord blood → bone, cartilage, fat, muscle.
    Neural stem cells (NSCs): SVZ + SGZ → neurons + glia (limited in adult human).
    Intestinal stem cells (ISC): LGR5+ CBC cells (covered in gut biology doc).
    Skin epidermal stem cells: Bulge region of hair follicle + basal layer.
    Cardiac progenitor cells: Very limited; negligible regenerative capacity in adult human.

1.1 STEM CELL MARKERS
-----------------------
Key surface markers used for isolation:
  ESC/iPSC: OCT4, SOX2, NANOG (pluripotency TFs); surface: SSEA-4 (human), TRA-1-60, TRA-1-81, CD9.
  HSC (human): CD34+, CD38-, CD90+, CD45RA-, CD49f+. Lin- (negative for lineage markers).
  MSC: CD73+, CD90+, CD105+, CD34-, CD45-, CD14-, CD19-. Negative for haematopoietic/endothelial markers.
  Neural stem cell: Nestin+, Sox2+, GFAP+, CD133 (Prominin-1)+.
  Cancer stem cells: Vary by tumour type. Breast: CD44+CD24-/low; CRC: CD133+; AML: CD34+CD38-.

2. PLURIPOTENCY NETWORKS
--------------------------
Core pluripotency transcription factors form a self-reinforcing circuit:
  OCT4 (POU5F1): Master regulator; activates pluripotency genes; represses differentiation.
  SOX2: HMG-box TF; forms OCT4-SOX2 heterodimer on many pluripotency gene enhancers.
  NANOG: Homeobox; maintains ground state pluripotency; fluctuates → primes differentiation.
  KLF4: Activates OCT4; represses differentiation genes.

Super-enhancers at pluripotency genes: Extremely large enhancer clusters drive high OCT4/SOX2/NANOG expression via transcriptional condensates (BRD4/Mediator).

Epigenetic landscape:
  Active: H3K4me3 + H3K27ac at pluripotency gene promoters/enhancers.
  Bivalent domains: H3K4me3 + H3K27me3 co-occur at developmental regulator genes (Hox, Pax) → poised for activation on differentiation.
  ESC has globally open, hyperactive chromatin → progressively restricted on differentiation.

Naive vs Primed Pluripotency:
  Naive (ground state, ESC from ICM): Two active X chromosomes; X-inactivation not yet occurred; clonogenic; independent of MEK/ERK and FGF signalling.
    Maintained by: 2i media (MEK inhibitor PD0325901 + GSK3 inhibitor CHIR99021) + LIF.
  Primed (EpiSC, epiblast): One active X; dependent on FGF/Activin; less clonogenic; more similar to human ESC.
  Human ESC: More similar to primed state. Research ongoing to establish truly naïve human pluripotency.

iPSC Reprogramming:
  Original Yamanaka 2006 method: Retroviral vectors (MMLV) → murine fibroblasts → iPSC colonies after ~3 weeks.
  Safer delivery methods: Non-integrating episomal vectors, mRNA transfection, Sendai virus (non-integrating RNA virus), small molecule cocktail (for partial reprogramming without transgenes).
  Reprogramming barriers: DNA damage response (p53 activation → apoptosis), senescence, incomplete epigenetic reprogramming.
  Transgene-free iPSCs: Achieved by multiple methods; required for clinical applications.
  Disease modeling: ALS (SOD1, FUS), PD (SNCA, LRRK2), schizophrenia (DISC1), DMD, SMA, cystic fibrosis, retinal dystrophies — "disease in a dish."

3. HAEMATOPOIETIC STEM CELL BIOLOGY
--------------------------------------
HSC Hierarchy:
  LT-HSC (long-term) → ST-HSC (short-term, ~5–8 week repopulation) → MPP (multipotent progenitor) →
  CMP (common myeloid) or CLP (common lymphoid) → further differentiation.
  LT-HSC characteristics: Mostly quiescent (G0); low metabolic rate; ROS-low; glycolysis-dependent; high autophagy.

HSC Niche (detailed):
  Endosteal niche: CXCL12-abundant reticular (CAR) cells + osteoblasts + sympathetic nerves → maintain quiescence.
  Perivascular niche: Sinusoidal endothelium + CXCL12 CAR cells + megakaryocytes → activate HSC.
  CXCL12/CXCR4 axis: Essential for HSC retention in marrow. AMD3100 (plerixafor, CXCR4 antagonist) + G-CSF → mobilise HSCs for collection.
  Thrombopoietin (TPO)/MPL: HSC self-renewal signal. Romiplostim (TPO receptor agonist) used in ITP.

Clonal Haematopoiesis of Indeterminate Potential (CHIP):
  Somatic mutations in HSCs (DNMT3A, TET2, ASXL1, JAK2 V617F) → clonal expansion.
  Prevalence: ~10% at age 70; ~20% at age 80. Associated with: CV disease (2× risk MI, 4× risk stroke), haematological malignancy (10% 10-year risk), overall mortality.
  TET2 CHIP → macrophage IL-6/IL-1beta → atherosclerosis (mouse model; IL-1beta blockade partially reverses).

4. iPSC-DERIVED CELL THERAPIES
---------------------------------
Approved/Advanced iPSC Products:
  OCU400 (Ocugen): Photoreceptor precursors from iPSCs for retinal dystrophies. Phase III.
  RPE from iPSCs (Masayo Takahashi): AMD treatment. Japanese Phase I/II — transplanted autologous iPSC-derived RPE sheet. Safety demonstrated.
  iPSC-NK cells (Fate Therapeutics, FT516/FT538/FT576): Off-the-shelf NK cells for cancer. Phase I/II.
  iPSC-T cells: Engineered with TCR or CAR; allogeneic. Early phase.
  iPSC-Cardiomyocytes: Remuscularisation post-MI (covered in CV biology doc).
  iPSC-Islets (beta cells): Type 1 diabetes → subcapsular transplantation + device encapsulation to avoid immunosuppression. Vertex (VX-880/VX-264): ESC-derived islets → functional insulin response in T1DM.

Challenges:
  Tumorigenicity: Undifferentiated iPSC contamination → teratoma risk. Stringent safety testing required.
  Immunogenicity: Allogeneic cells → HLA mismatch → rejection. Solutions: HLA homozygous "super-donors," HLA knockout + HLA-E expression, off-the-shelf approaches.
  Functional maturity: iPSC-CMs and iPSC-hepatocytes show fetal-like characteristics → need maturation protocols.
  Manufacturing scale: GMP-grade production of consistent cells at therapeutic scale is challenging and costly.

5. ORGANOIDS — BIOLOGY & APPLICATIONS
----------------------------------------
3D self-organising structures derived from stem cells that recapitulate organ architecture.
Pioneer work: Clevers (intestinal, 2009), Lancaster (cerebral, 2013), Huch (liver/pancreas, 2013), Karthaus (prostate, 2014).

Types and Applications:
  Brain (cerebral) organoids: From ESC/iPSC → neuroectoderm → 3D structures with cortical layers.
    Contain: Neuroepithelial stem cells, IPC, cortical neurons (deep and superficial), astrocytes.
    Uses: Model cortical development; microcephaly (ASPM, CDK5RAP2, MCPH1 mutations → reduced lobe formation); SARS-CoV-2 neurotropism; Zika virus (causes microcephaly — cortical organoids showed reduced progenitor proliferation → mechanism).
  Liver organoids: Ductal (cholangiocyte-derived) or hepatocyte organoids. Drug toxicity screening; disease modelling (NASH, Wilson's, AAT deficiency).
  Kidney organoids (Phipson, Little, Bhatt): Recapitulate nephron segments. Drug nephrotoxicity; PKD modelling; Wilms tumour.
  Lung organoids: Model SARS-CoV-2 infection; CF; IPF. Airway vs alveolar subtypes.
  Cardiac organoids: Chamber-like structures; cardiomyocytes + endothelium + cardiac fibroblasts. Drug cardiotoxicity (replace HERG assay).
  Tumour organoids: Patient-derived (PDOs) → drug sensitivity testing; biobank correlations with clinical response.

Assembloids:
  Two or more organoids fused together to model tissue-tissue interactions.
  Cortical-thalamic assembloids: Model thalamocortical projection development.
  Forebrain-spinal cord assembloids: Study corticospinal tract formation.
  Brain-gut assembloids: Model enteric nervous system.
  Vascularised organoids: Co-culture with endothelial cells → partial vascularisation → improved survival and maturation.

Organ-on-a-Chip:
  Microfluidic devices housing living cells in 3D environments with fluid flow.
  Gut-on-a-chip (Ingber lab): Caco-2 cells + peristaltic flow + gut microbiome → model intestinal physiology.
  Lung-on-a-chip: Alveolar + endothelial cells + air-liquid interface + cyclic stretch → breathing motion.
  Multi-organ: Body-on-a-chip → liver + gut + kidney + heart chips linked → systemic drug toxicity/ADME.
  Advantages over organoids: Defined fluid flow, mechanical forces, compartmentalisation.

6. CANCER STEM CELLS (CSCs)
-----------------------------
Hypothesis: Tumours contain a subpopulation of cells with stem cell properties → responsible for tumour initiation, maintenance, metastasis, and recurrence.

Evidence:
  Al-Hajj (2003): CD44+CD24- breast cancer cells → 100× more tumorigenic in xenograft than CD44-CD24+ cells.
  Dick (1994–1997): AML xenograft transplantation → only CD34+CD38- cells initiated AML.
  Hierarchical organisation in some tumours (CRC, AML, glioblastoma) → supports CSC model.
  Lineage tracing in mouse models: LGR5+ CRC cells → drive metastasis; LGR5- cells can revert to LGR5+ state → plasticity.

CSC Properties:
  Self-renewal (Wnt, Notch, Hedgehog signalling).
  Drug resistance: Slow cycling (evade S-phase targeting drugs); ABC transporters; efficient DNA repair; anti-apoptotic proteins (BCL-2 high); ROS-low (protect from oxidative damage of many drugs).
  EMT (Epithelial-Mesenchymal Transition): CSC properties overlap with EMT state → invasive + stem-like simultaneously.
  Symmetric vs asymmetric division: CSC can self-renew or produce more differentiated progeny.

Targeting CSCs:
  BMP pathway: BMP4 promotes CSC differentiation → clinical trials (AML, CRC, GBM).
  LGR5 targeting: Antibody-drug conjugate (ADC) against LGR5+ CRC cells.
  Hedgehog inhibitors: Vismodegib (Smo inhibitor) → basal cell carcinoma (skin CSC → approved); medulloblastoma (PTCH1-mutant).
  Wnt inhibitors: Porcupine inhibitors (WNT-974, LGK-974) → multiple trials in KRAS-mutant cancers.
  Notch inhibitors: Gamma-secretase inhibitors (GSIs) — trial toxicity (GI goblet cell metaplasia); selective NOTCH1/2 antibodies in trials.
  Targeting CSC metabolism: CSCs often depend on OXPHOS (vs glycolysis in bulk tumour) — metformin, IACS-010759 (ETC complex I) in trials.

[ETHICAL CONTEXT] Stem cell research ethics:
  Human embryonic stem cells: Controversy over blastocyst destruction. 14-day rule: Most countries limit embryo research to 14 days (before primitive streak). 2021: International Society for Stem Cell Research relaxed 14-day rule → allows extending under strict oversight.
  Gastruloids/synthetic embryos: Stem cells → self-organise into embryo-like structures (gastruloids, EPSC-derived blastoids). Regulatory grey area; profound ethical questions about moral status.
  Germline editing (inherited): CRISPR editing of human embryos (He Jiankui, 2018 — twins Lulu/Nana, CCR5 deletion) → universal condemnation → scientific moratorium on heritable germline editing pending ethical framework.
