ATMOSPHERIC PHYSICS AND WEATHER DYNAMICS
=======================================

ATMOSPHERE:
Earth's atmosphere is a layered fluid envelope dominated by nitrogen and oxygen.

LAYERS:
- Troposphere
- Stratosphere
- Mesosphere
- Thermosphere
- Exosphere

Most weather occurs in the troposphere.

PRESSURE:
Atmospheric pressure is due to the weight of air above a point.
Pressure generally decreases with height.

TEMPERATURE CHANGE WITH HEIGHT:
The average lapse rate in the troposphere is about:
6.5 degrees C per km

WATER VAPOR:
Water vapor strongly influences:
- humidity
- cloud formation
- rainfall
- latent heat transport

HUMIDITY TERMS:
- Absolute humidity
- Relative humidity
- Dew point

If air cools to the dew point, condensation can begin.

CLOUD FORMATION:
Clouds form when moist air rises, expands, cools, and condenses on nuclei.

WAYS AIR RISES:
- Convection
- Frontal lifting
- Orographic lifting over mountains
- Convergence

LATENT HEAT:
When water vapor condenses, latent heat is released.
This powers storms and atmospheric circulation.

WINDS:
Air moves from high pressure toward low pressure, but Earth's rotation changes the path.

CORIOLIS EFFECT:
- deflects motion to the right in the Northern Hemisphere
- deflects motion to the left in the Southern Hemisphere

PRESSURE GRADIENT FORCE:
Drives air from regions of higher pressure to lower pressure.

GLOBAL CIRCULATION:
Important circulation cells:
- Hadley cell
- Ferrel cell
- Polar cell

JET STREAMS:
Fast winds high in the atmosphere.
They influence storm tracks and large-scale weather.

FRONTS:
Boundary between air masses.
Types:
- Cold front
- Warm front
- Stationary front
- Occluded front

THUNDERSTORMS:
Require:
- moisture
- instability
- lifting

SEVERE WEATHER:
- thunderstorms
- tornadoes
- cyclones
- heat waves
- blizzards

GREENHOUSE EFFECT:
Certain gases absorb and re-radiate infrared energy.
Major greenhouse gases:
- water vapor
- carbon dioxide
- methane
- nitrous oxide

CLIMATE VS WEATHER:
Weather:
- short-term atmospheric conditions

Climate:
- long-term average patterns

ENERGY BALANCE:
Incoming solar radiation must be balanced by outgoing infrared radiation over long timescales.

ALBEDO:
Fraction of incoming radiation reflected by a surface.
Ice and clouds usually have high albedo.

EXAM FOCUS:
- pressure and wind
- humidity and dew point
- cloud formation
- fronts
- Coriolis effect
- greenhouse effect
