COLLEGE PHYSICS - NUCLEAR, PARTICLE PHYSICS AND ASTROPHYSICS
=============================================================

CHAPTER: ADVANCED NUCLEAR PHYSICS

NUCLEAR FORCE:
- Short range (~2 fm), strongest known force.
- Charge independent (same between p-p, p-n, n-n).
- Saturates: each nucleon interacts only with neighbors.
- Spin dependent: stronger when spins are parallel.
- Meson exchange: Yukawa proposed π mesons as carriers.

SEMI-EMPIRICAL MASS FORMULA (Bethe-Weizsäcker):
BE = aᵥA - aₛA^(2/3) - a_c Z(Z-1)/A^(1/3) - a_a(A-2Z)²/A ± δ
- Volume term (aᵥA): all nucleons bound by neighbors.
- Surface term (aₛA^(2/3)): surface nucleons less bound.
- Coulomb term: proton-proton repulsion.
- Asymmetry term: energetically favorable when N = Z.
- Pairing term (δ): even-even nuclei most stable.

NUCLEAR REACTIONS:
Q value: Q = (m_reactants - m_products)c²
Q > 0: exothermic (energy released)
Q < 0: endothermic (energy absorbed, threshold reaction)

Cross section (σ): measure of reaction probability. Units: barn (1 b = 10⁻²⁸ m²)

FISSION:
Heavy nucleus (A > 200) splits into two medium nuclei.
U-235 + n → Ba-141 + Kr-92 + 3n + 200 MeV
Chain reaction: each fission produces 2-3 neutrons which can cause further fissions.
Critical mass: minimum mass for self-sustaining chain reaction.
Moderation: slow neutrons more effective (water, heavy water, graphite).

NUCLEAR REACTOR:
- Fuel: enriched U-235 (3-5% in U-238).
- Moderator: slows neutrons (H₂O, D₂O, graphite).
- Control rods: absorb neutrons (Cd, B) to control reaction rate.
- Coolant: removes heat (H₂O, D₂O, CO₂, liquid Na).
- Reflector: reduces neutron leakage.
Types: PWR, BWR, CANDU, fast breeder reactor.

FUSION:
Light nuclei combine at extremely high temperature (10⁷-10⁸ K).
D + T → He-4 + n + 17.6 MeV
Advantages over fission: more energy per nucleon, no long-lived radioactive waste, fuel abundant.
Challenges: plasma confinement (Tokamak - magnetic confinement, ICF - laser compression).

RADIOACTIVE SERIES:
Natural decay chains:
- Uranium series: U-238 → ... → Pb-206
- Actinium series: U-235 → ... → Pb-207
- Thorium series: Th-232 → ... → Pb-208

NUCLEAR MODELS:
- Liquid drop model: explains fission and binding energy.
- Shell model: explains magic numbers (2,8,20,28,50,82,126) — especially stable nuclei.
- Collective model: combines both; explains nuclear deformation.

CHAPTER: PARTICLE PHYSICS

FUNDAMENTAL PARTICLES:

QUARKS (6 flavors):
Up (u), Down (d), Strange (s), Charm (c), Bottom (b), Top (t)
- Fractional charges: u, c, t have +2/3 e; d, s, b have -1/3 e.
- Confined inside hadrons (never observed alone).
- Held by gluons (strong force).

Baryons (3 quarks): proton (uud), neutron (udd), etc.
Mesons (quark-antiquark): pion (ud̄), kaon, etc.

LEPTONS (6 types):
Electron (e⁻), muon (μ⁻), tau (τ⁻) and their neutrinos (νₑ, ν_μ, ν_τ).
No internal structure. Not subject to strong force.
Electron: lightest, stable. Muon: 207× heavier than e, decays. Tau: 3477× heavier.

FUNDAMENTAL FORCES AND GAUGE BOSONS:
1. Strong force: gluons (g). Holds quarks in nucleons. Range ~ 1 fm.
2. Electromagnetic: photons (γ). Infinite range. Couples to charge.
3. Weak force: W±, Z⁰ bosons. Responsible for beta decay. Range ~ 10⁻¹⁸ m.
4. Gravity: graviton (hypothetical). Infinite range. Not yet quantized.

STANDARD MODEL:
Quantum field theory describing strong, EM, and weak forces.
Gauge group: SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1)
- SU(3): quantum chromodynamics (QCD) for strong force.
- SU(2)×U(1): electroweak force (unification of EM and weak).

HIGGS BOSON:
- Gives mass to W, Z bosons and fermions through Higgs mechanism.
- Discovered at CERN (LHC) in 2012. Mass ≈ 125 GeV/c².

ANTIMATTER:
Every particle has an antiparticle (opposite charge, other quantum numbers).
Particle-antiparticle annihilation: e⁻ + e⁺ → 2γ (E = 2m_ec²)
CP violation: slight asymmetry between matter and antimatter (explains why universe has more matter).

CONSERVATION LAWS:
Always conserved: energy, momentum, angular momentum, charge, baryon number, lepton number.
Conserved in strong and EM but not weak: parity (P), charge conjugation (C).
Violated in weak interactions: CP (slightly), T (slightly), but CPT always conserved.

FEYNMAN DIAGRAMS:
Visual representation of particle interactions.
- Lines: propagators (particle propagation).
- Vertices: interaction points.
- Each vertex contributes a factor of coupling constant.
- Virtual particles: internal lines (can violate energy-momentum conservation temporarily, due to uncertainty principle).

CHAPTER: ASTROPHYSICS AND COSMOLOGY

STELLAR STRUCTURE:
Energy source: nuclear fusion (pp chain, CNO cycle in massive stars).
Hydrostatic equilibrium: gravity balanced by pressure gradient.
dP/dr = -ρGM(r)/r²

STELLAR EVOLUTION:
1. Protostar: gas cloud collapses under gravity.
2. Main sequence: H fusion in core. Sun's lifetime: ~10¹⁰ years.
3. Red giant: H exhausted in core; He fusion begins.
4. Final stages depend on mass:
   - < 1.4 M_sun: white dwarf (Chandrasekhar limit).
   - 1.4 - 3 M_sun: neutron star (incredibly dense, ~10¹⁷ kg/m³).
   - > 3 M_sun: black hole.

NEUTRON STARS:
- Radius ~ 10 km, mass ~ 1.4-3 M_sun.
- Pulsars: rotating neutron stars emitting beams of radiation.
- Densest stable objects in universe.

BLACK HOLES:
Schwarzschild radius: r_s = 2GM/c²
For M = M_sun: r_s ≈ 3 km.
Event horizon: surface of no return.
Hawking radiation: quantum effect causing black holes to slowly evaporate.
T_H = ℏc³/(8πGMk_B) (Hawking temperature)

GRAVITATIONAL WAVES:
Ripples in spacetime caused by accelerating masses.
Produced by binary systems, merging black holes, neutron stars.
LIGO detected first direct observation (2015): GW150914, merger of two ~30 M_sun black holes.
Amplitude: strain h = ΔL/L ~ 10⁻²¹ (incredibly small).

THE UNIVERSE:
Big Bang: origin of universe ~13.8 billion years ago.
Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB): thermal radiation at 2.7 K filling universe.
Hubble's law: v = H₀d (H₀ ≈ 67-73 km/s/Mpc)
Dark matter: ~27% of energy density. Detected only gravitationally.
Dark energy: ~68% of energy density. Causes accelerating expansion.
Ordinary matter: ~5%.

COSMOLOGICAL MODELS:
Friedmann equation: H² = 8πGρ/3 - kc²/a²
a = scale factor, k = curvature (-1, 0, +1), ρ = energy density.
Flat universe (k=0): critical density ρ_c = 3H²/(8πG) ≈ 9.4 × 10⁻²⁷ kg/m³.

IMPORTANT CONSTANTS:
G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²
M_sun = 1.989 × 10³⁰ kg
R_sun = 6.96 × 10⁸ m
1 parsec = 3.086 × 10¹⁶ m = 3.26 light years
1 Astronomical Unit (AU) = 1.496 × 10¹¹ m
Age of universe: ~13.8 × 10⁹ years
H₀ ≈ 70 km/s/Mpc (Hubble constant)
