MEDICAL IMAGING AND BIOPHOTONICS
================================

OVERVIEW:
Medical imaging uses physical principles to visualize structure and function inside the body.
Biophotonics focuses on the interaction of light with biological systems.

MAJOR IMAGING MODALITIES:
- X-ray imaging
- Computed tomography (CT)
- Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)
- Ultrasound
- Positron emission tomography (PET)
- Optical imaging

X-RAYS:
X-rays are high-energy electromagnetic waves.
They are absorbed differently by bone, soft tissue, and air.
Dense materials appear brighter on radiographs.

ATTENUATION:
As X-rays pass through tissue, intensity decreases.
A simple law is:
I = I0 e^(-mu x)
where mu is attenuation coefficient and x is thickness.

CT SCAN:
CT uses many X-ray projections from multiple angles.
A computer reconstructs cross-sectional images.

MRI:
MRI is based on nuclear magnetic resonance of hydrogen nuclei.
Important ideas:
- strong magnetic field aligns spins
- radiofrequency pulses disturb alignment
- relaxation signals are measured

MRI CONTRAST:
Important time constants:
- T1 relaxation
- T2 relaxation

ULTRASOUND:
Uses high-frequency sound waves.
Useful because it is:
- non-ionizing
- real time
- relatively inexpensive

ULTRASOUND IMAGING DEPENDS ON:
- acoustic impedance differences
- reflection
- scattering
- time of flight

DOPPLER ULTRASOUND:
Measures blood flow using Doppler shift.

PET IMAGING:
Uses positron-emitting tracers.
Shows metabolic or biochemical activity.
Often combined with CT or MRI.

BIOPHOTONICS:
Light can be used for:
- microscopy
- fluorescence imaging
- spectroscopy
- laser surgery
- optical biosensing

FLUORESCENCE:
A molecule absorbs light at one wavelength and emits at a longer wavelength.
Used in tagging cells and proteins.

LASERS IN MEDICINE:
Applications:
- eye surgery
- dermatology
- cutting and coagulation
- photodynamic therapy

RESOLUTION:
Imaging systems aim for:
- high spatial resolution
- good contrast
- low noise
- safe dose

IONIZING VS NON-IONIZING:
Ionizing methods:
- X-ray
- CT
- PET

Non-ionizing methods:
- MRI
- Ultrasound
- many optical methods

IMAGE QUALITY FACTORS:
- signal-to-noise ratio
- contrast-to-noise ratio
- resolution
- artifacts

APPLICATIONS:
- diagnosis
- surgical guidance
- tumour detection
- monitoring treatment
- biological research
