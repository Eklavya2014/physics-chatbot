COLLEGE PHYSICS - QUANTUM MECHANICS
=====================================

HISTORICAL BACKGROUND:
Classical physics failed to explain:
1. Blackbody radiation (UV catastrophe).
2. Photoelectric effect.
3. Atomic stability and line spectra.
4. Compton scattering.

PLANCK'S QUANTUM HYPOTHESIS (1900):
Energy is quantized: E = nhf (n = integer)
Planck's constant: h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s = 4.136 × 10⁻¹⁵ eV·s
ℏ = h/2π = 1.055 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s

WAVE-PARTICLE DUALITY:
- Light exhibits both wave (interference, diffraction) and particle (photoelectric effect) nature.
- Matter exhibits both particle and wave nature (de Broglie, Davisson-Germer).
- λ = h/p (de Broglie wavelength)

SCHRÖDINGER EQUATION:
Foundation of quantum mechanics.
Time-dependent: iℏ ∂Ψ/∂t = ĤΨ = [-ℏ²/2m ∇² + V]Ψ
Time-independent: ĤΨ = EΨ [-ℏ²/2m ∇² + V]ψ = Eψ

WAVEFUNCTION (Ψ):
- Ψ contains all information about quantum state.
- |Ψ|² = probability density.
- Born interpretation: P(x,t)dx = |Ψ(x,t)|² dx (probability of finding particle in dx).
- Normalization: ∫|Ψ|² dτ = 1 (particle must be somewhere).
- Must be continuous, single-valued, finite, and square-integrable.

QUANTUM STATES AND OPERATORS:
Observable ↔ Hermitian operator.
Expectation value: <A> = ∫Ψ* Â Ψ dτ
Momentum operator: p̂ = -iℏ∇
Energy operator (Hamiltonian): Ĥ = -ℏ²/2m ∇² + V

PARTICLE IN A BOX (Infinite Square Well):
Boundary conditions: ψ(0) = ψ(L) = 0
Energy eigenvalues: E_n = n²π²ℏ²/(2mL²) = n²E₁ (n = 1,2,3,...)
Wavefunctions: ψ_n = √(2/L) sin(nπx/L)
Zero-point energy: E₁ = π²ℏ²/(2mL²) ≠ 0 (purely quantum effect)
Energy levels are discrete — quantized.

QUANTUM HARMONIC OSCILLATOR:
V(x) = ½mω²x²
Energy levels: E_n = (n + ½)ℏω (n = 0,1,2,...)
Zero-point energy: E₀ = ½ℏω
Equally spaced energy levels.
Creation/annihilation operators: ψ_n = (a†)ⁿ/√n! × ψ₀

HYDROGEN ATOM (QUANTUM MECHANICAL):
Full solution of Schrödinger equation in 3D.
Energy: E_n = -13.6/n² eV (same as Bohr model)
Quantum numbers:
- Principal: n = 1, 2, 3, ... (energy, size of orbital)
- Orbital angular momentum: l = 0, 1, ..., n-1 (shape: s,p,d,f)
- Magnetic: m_l = -l, ..., 0, ..., +l (orientation)
- Spin: m_s = +½ or -½ (intrinsic angular momentum)

Wavefunctions: ψ_nlm(r,θ,φ) = R_nl(r) × Y_l^m(θ,φ)
Orbitals: s (l=0, spherical), p (l=1, dumbbell), d (l=2, complex shapes).

PAULI EXCLUSION PRINCIPLE:
No two electrons in an atom can have the same set of quantum numbers (n, l, m_l, m_s).
Explains periodic table structure and chemical bonding.

UNCERTAINTY PRINCIPLE (formal):
ΔxΔp_x ≥ ℏ/2
ΔEΔt ≥ ℏ/2
ΔJₓΔJᵧ ≥ ℏ/2 |<Jz>|

SPIN:
Intrinsic angular momentum of particles.
Electron spin: s = ½ (fermion).
Spin angular momentum: S = ℏ√(s(s+1)) = ℏ√3/2
Z-component: S_z = m_s ℏ = ±ℏ/2
Bosons (integer spin): photon (s=1), graviton (s=2). Obey Bose-Einstein statistics.
Fermions (half-integer spin): electron, proton (s=½). Obey Fermi-Dirac statistics. Obey Pauli principle.

TUNNELING:
Quantum particle can pass through a potential barrier even if E < V₀.
Transmission coefficient: T ≈ e^(-2κL) where κ = √(2m(V₀-E))/ℏ
Applications: tunnel diode, scanning tunneling microscope (STM), nuclear fusion in stars, radioactive decay.

QUANTUM ENTANGLEMENT:
Two particles can be in correlated quantum states such that measuring one instantly affects the other, regardless of distance.
EPR paradox, Bell's inequality (violation confirmed experimentally).
Application: quantum cryptography, quantum computing.

PERTURBATION THEORY:
When H = H₀ + H', exact solution unknown.
First-order energy correction: E_n^(1) = <n⁰|H'|n⁰>
First-order state correction: |n^(1)> = Σ_{m≠n} <m⁰|H'|n⁰>/(E_n⁰-E_m⁰) |m⁰>

VARIATIONAL METHOD:
<E> = <ψ|H|ψ>/<ψ|ψ> ≥ E_ground
Used to estimate ground state energy.

SELECTION RULES (for hydrogen atom transitions):
Δl = ±1, Δm_l = 0, ±1 (electric dipole transitions)
Δn = any, Δs = 0

QUANTUM STATISTICS:
Fermi-Dirac distribution: f(E) = 1/(e^((E-E_F)/kT) + 1) [fermions]
Bose-Einstein distribution: n(E) = 1/(e^((E-μ)/kT) - 1) [bosons]
Classical limit (Maxwell-Boltzmann): f(E) ∝ e^(-E/kT)

Fermi energy E_F: energy below which all states filled at T=0.
For electrons: E_F = (ℏ²/2m)(3π²n)^(2/3)

IMPORTANT CONSTANTS:
h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s
ℏ = 1.055 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s
m_e = 9.109 × 10⁻³¹ kg
e = 1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
a₀ = 0.529 Å (Bohr radius)
Rydberg energy: E_R = 13.6 eV

IMPORTANT FORMULAS:
- E = hf = ℏω (photon energy)
- λ = h/p (de Broglie)
- ΔxΔp ≥ ℏ/2
- E_n (box) = n²π²ℏ²/(2mL²)
- E_n (oscillator) = (n+½)ℏω
- E_n (hydrogen) = -13.6/n² eV
- T (tunneling) ≈ e^(-2κL)
