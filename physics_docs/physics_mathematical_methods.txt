MATHEMATICAL PHYSICS - COMPLETE GUIDE
=======================================
Vectors, Tensors, Complex Analysis, Differential Equations, Group Theory, Fourier Analysis

PART 1: VECTOR CALCULUS

VECTOR OPERATORS:
Gradient: ∇f = (∂f/∂x)î + (∂f/∂y)ĵ + (∂f/∂z)k̂. Points in direction of maximum increase.
Divergence: ∇·F = ∂Fx/∂x + ∂Fy/∂y + ∂Fz/∂z. Measures source/sink of field.
Curl: ∇×F = (∂Fz/∂y - ∂Fy/∂z)î + ... Measures rotation of field.
Laplacian: ∇²f = ∂²f/∂x² + ∂²f/∂y² + ∂²f/∂z². Very important in physics.

Identities:
∇·(∇×F) = 0 (curl is divergence-free).
∇×(∇f) = 0 (gradient is curl-free).
∇²F = ∇(∇·F) - ∇×(∇×F).

FUNDAMENTAL THEOREMS:
Divergence theorem (Gauss): ∮F·dA = ∫∫∫ ∇·F dV.
Stokes theorem: ∮F·dL = ∫∫(∇×F)·dA.
Green's theorems (2D analogs).

CURVILINEAR COORDINATES:
Cylindrical (ρ, φ, z):
x = ρcos φ, y = ρsin φ, z = z.
∇f = (∂f/∂ρ)ρ̂ + (1/ρ)(∂f/∂φ)φ̂ + (∂f/∂z)ẑ.
∇²f = (1/ρ)∂/∂ρ(ρ ∂f/∂ρ) + (1/ρ²)∂²f/∂φ² + ∂²f/∂z².

Spherical (r, θ, φ):
x = r sin θ cos φ, y = r sin θ sin φ, z = r cos θ.
∇²f = (1/r²)∂/∂r(r²∂f/∂r) + (1/r²sinθ)∂/∂θ(sinθ ∂f/∂θ) + (1/r²sin²θ)∂²f/∂φ².
Volume element: dV = r² sinθ dr dθ dφ.

PART 2: TENSORS

DEFINITION:
Tensor of rank n: mathematical object with n indices that transforms in a specific way under coordinate transformation.
Rank 0: scalar. Rank 1: vector. Rank 2: matrix-like (but transforms properly).

NOTATION (Einstein summation):
Repeated index (one up, one down) implies summation: Aᵢ Bⁱ = Σᵢ AᵢBᵢ.
Upper (contravariant) index: transforms like coordinates.
Lower (covariant) index: transforms like gradient.

METRIC TENSOR:
gᵢⱼ: relates coordinate distances to actual distances.
ds² = gᵢⱼ dxⁱ dxʲ.
Flat spacetime: g = diag(1,1,1) (Euclidean) or diag(-1,1,1,1) (Minkowski).
Curved spacetime: gᵢⱼ varies with position (General Relativity).
Inverse metric: gⁱʲ gⱼₖ = δⁱₖ.
Raising/lowering indices: Aⁱ = gⁱʲ Aⱼ.

STRESS TENSOR:
σᵢⱼ: force per unit area in direction i on surface with normal j.
Diagonal: normal stresses. Off-diagonal: shear stresses.
Symmetry: σᵢⱼ = σⱼᵢ (no net torque on volume element).
Cauchy's equation: ∂σᵢⱼ/∂xⱼ + fᵢ = ρ aᵢ.

ELECTROMAGNETIC FIELD TENSOR:
Fᵘᵛ: antisymmetric rank-2 tensor containing E and B fields.
F⁰ⁱ = Eᵢ/c, Fⁱʲ = εᵢⱼₖ Bₖ.
Maxwell's equations become: ∂ᵥFᵘᵛ = μ₀Jᵘ and ∂[λFᵘᵥ] = 0.
Elegant covariant formulation of EM.

CHRISTOFFEL SYMBOLS AND COVARIANT DERIVATIVE:
Γᵅβγ = (1/2) gᵅδ(∂gβδ/∂xγ + ∂gγδ/∂xβ - ∂gβγ/∂xδ).
Covariant derivative: ∇β Vᵅ = ∂β Vᵅ + Γᵅβγ Vγ.
Generalizes derivative to curved spaces.

RIEMANN CURVATURE TENSOR:
Rᵅβγδ: measures intrinsic curvature of spacetime.
Rᵅβγδ = ∂γΓᵅδβ - ∂δΓᵅγβ + ΓᵅγλΓλδβ - ΓᵅδλΓλγβ.
Ricci tensor: Rβδ = Rᵅβᵅδ (contraction).
Ricci scalar: R = gβδRβδ.
Einstein tensor: Gβδ = Rβδ - ½gβδR.
Einstein field equations: Gβδ + Λgβδ = (8πG/c⁴)Tβδ.

PART 3: COMPLEX ANALYSIS IN PHYSICS

COMPLEX NUMBERS:
z = x + iy = r e^(iθ) (Euler form). r = |z|, θ = arg(z).
Euler's formula: e^(iθ) = cos θ + i sin θ. Most beautiful formula: e^(iπ) + 1 = 0.
Complex conjugate: z* = x - iy. |z|² = zz*.

ANALYTIC FUNCTIONS:
Complex differentiable everywhere in a region.
Cauchy-Riemann equations: ∂u/∂x = ∂v/∂y, ∂u/∂y = -∂v/∂x.
Harmonic functions: ∇²u = 0 (satisfies Laplace equation).
Conformal mappings: angle-preserving transformations. Used in 2D electrostatics, fluid flow.

CAUCHY'S INTEGRAL THEOREM:
If f is analytic inside and on contour C: ∮_C f(z) dz = 0.
Cauchy's integral formula: f(a) = (1/2πi) ∮ f(z)/(z-a) dz.
Derivatives: f^(n)(a) = (n!/2πi) ∮ f(z)/(z-a)^(n+1) dz.

RESIDUE THEOREM:
∮ f(z) dz = 2πi Σ Res[f, zₖ] (sum over poles inside contour).
Residue at simple pole: Res[f, z₀] = lim(z→z₀) (z-z₀)f(z).
Powerful tool for evaluating real integrals.
Example: ∫₋∞^∞ 1/(1+x²) dx = π (using residue at z = i).

APPLICATIONS IN PHYSICS:
Green's functions: solution of differential equations using complex analysis.
Dispersion relations: Kramers-Kronig relations connect real and imaginary parts of response functions.
Causality: Im[χ(ω)] determines absorption; Re[χ(ω)] determines dispersion. Related by Hilbert transform.
Quantum mechanics: analytic continuation of Green's functions → resonances (complex poles).
Statistical mechanics: partition function as function of complex β = 1/kT.

PART 4: DIFFERENTIAL EQUATIONS IN PHYSICS

ORDINARY DIFFERENTIAL EQUATIONS:

First order: dy/dx = f(x,y). Separable, linear, exact forms.
Second order linear: ay'' + by' + cy = f(x). Most important in physics.
Homogeneous + Particular solution. Characteristic equation for constant coefficients.
Simple harmonic oscillator: ẍ + ω₀²x = 0. Solution: x = A cos(ω₀t + φ).
Damped oscillator: ẍ + 2γẋ + ω₀²x = 0. Three cases: underdamped (γ<ω₀), critically damped (γ=ω₀), overdamped (γ>ω₀).
Driven oscillator: ẍ + 2γẋ + ω₀²x = F₀cos(ωt)/m. Steady state: x = A(ω)cos(ωt+φ).
Resonance at ω ≈ ω₀ (for weak damping).

PARTIAL DIFFERENTIAL EQUATIONS:

Wave equation: ∂²u/∂t² = v² ∇²u. D'Alembert solution: u(x,t) = f(x-vt) + g(x+vt).

Diffusion/Heat equation: ∂u/∂t = D ∇²u. Solutions involve error functions (1D) or eigenfunctions.
Gaussian packet broadens with time: σ(t) = √(2Dt).

Laplace equation: ∇²u = 0. Describes electrostatic potential, gravitational potential, steady heat flow.
Solutions: spherical harmonics (spherical symmetry), Fourier series (rectangular), Bessel functions (cylindrical).

Poisson equation: ∇²u = -ρ/ε₀. Laplace + source term.

Schrödinger equation: iℏ∂Ψ/∂t = ĤΨ. See quantum mechanics section.

SEPARATION OF VARIABLES:
Assume u(r,t) = R(r)T(t). Gets separate ODEs. Works for linear PDEs with separable geometry.
Produces eigenvalue problem. Solutions are eigenfunctions.

SPECIAL FUNCTIONS:
Legendre polynomials P_l(cos θ): angular part of spherical solutions. l = 0,1,2,...
P₀ = 1, P₁ = cos θ, P₂ = (3cos²θ-1)/2.
Spherical harmonics Y_l^m(θ,φ) = N P_l^m(cos θ) e^(imφ).
Orthonormality: ∫ Y_l^m* Y_l'^m' sinθ dθ dφ = δ_ll' δ_mm'.

Bessel functions J_n(kr): radial solutions in cylindrical coordinates.
Oscillatory behavior, like sinusoidal but with r-dependent period.
Zeros: J₀(x) = 0 at x = 2.405, 5.520, 8.654, ...

Hermite polynomials: harmonic oscillator wavefunctions.
H₀ = 1, H₁ = 2x, H₂ = 4x²-2, H₃ = 8x³-12x.

Laguerre polynomials: radial wavefunctions of hydrogen atom.

PART 5: GROUP THEORY IN PHYSICS

BASIC DEFINITIONS:
Group G: set of elements with operation * satisfying closure, associativity, identity, inverse.
Abelian group: a*b = b*a for all a,b.
Cyclic group Z_n: n elements, generated by one element.
Symmetric group S_n: all permutations of n objects. |S_n| = n!

REPRESENTATIONS:
Representation: homomorphism from group to matrices.
Faithful: one-to-one. Reducible: can be block-diagonalized. Irreducible (irrep): cannot be reduced further.
Regular representation: group acts on itself.
Character χ(g): trace of representation matrix. Same for all elements in same conjugacy class.
Character table: irreps × conjugacy classes → characters.

CONTINUOUS GROUPS (LIE GROUPS):
Group with continuous parameters. Generated by Lie algebra (infinitesimal generators).
Commutation relations: [Xᵢ, Xⱼ] = Cᵢⱼₖ Xₖ (Cᵢⱼₖ = structure constants).

Important groups:
SO(3): rotations in 3D. Generators: angular momentum operators Jₓ, Jᵧ, Jᵤ.
[Jₓ,Jᵧ] = iℏJᵤ and cyclic. Casimir: J² = Jₓ²+Jᵧ²+Jᵤ².
SU(2): 2×2 unitary matrices with det=1. Spinors. Double cover of SO(3).
SU(3): 8 generators (Gell-Mann matrices). QCD gauge group.
U(1): complex numbers of magnitude 1. QED gauge group.
Poincaré group: Lorentz transformations + translations. Symmetry group of special relativity.

APPLICATIONS IN PHYSICS:

Atomic physics: SO(3) symmetry → conservation of angular momentum → selection rules.
Representations labeled by l (orbital) and m (magnetic quantum number).
Spin: SU(2) representations. Half-integer spin from SU(2) not in SO(3).
Addition of angular momentum: Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

Particle physics: SU(3) flavor symmetry → predicts hadron multiplets (Eightfold Way, Gell-Mann 1961).
Predicted omega baryon (Ω⁻) discovered 1964 → triumph of group theory.
Standard Model gauge group: SU(3)_C × SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y.

Crystal symmetry: 32 crystallographic point groups. 230 space groups.
Group theory determines: allowed crystal structures, selection rules for optical transitions, phonon modes.

PART 6: FOURIER ANALYSIS

FOURIER SERIES (periodic functions):
f(x) = a₀/2 + Σ [aₙcos(nπx/L) + bₙsin(nπx/L)] for x in [-L, L].
aₙ = (1/L)∫f(x)cos(nπx/L)dx. bₙ = (1/L)∫f(x)sin(nπx/L)dx.
Complex form: f(x) = Σ cₙ e^(inπx/L). cₙ = (1/2L)∫f(x)e^(-inπx/L)dx.
Parseval's theorem: Σ|cₙ|² = (1/2L)∫|f(x)|²dx. Conservation of energy.

FOURIER TRANSFORM (aperiodic functions):
F(k) = (1/√2π) ∫ f(x) e^(-ikx) dx.
f(x) = (1/√2π) ∫ F(k) e^(ikx) dk.
Uncertainty principle: ΔxΔk ≥ 1/2. (Bandwidth theorem).
Convolution theorem: F[f*g] = F[f] × F[g]. Convolution in real space = multiplication in frequency space.
Important pairs: Gaussian ↔ Gaussian. Rectangle ↔ sinc. Delta function ↔ constant.

APPLICATIONS:
Signal processing: filter design, spectral analysis.
Quantum mechanics: wavefunction in position ↔ momentum space.
Diffraction: Fraunhofer diffraction pattern = Fourier transform of aperture function.
NMR/MRI: raw data = Fourier transform of image. Reconstruction by inverse FT.
X-ray crystallography: diffraction pattern = Fourier transform of electron density.
Solving differential equations: converts PDEs to algebraic equations.

LAPLACE TRANSFORM:
F(s) = ∫₀^∞ f(t) e^(-st) dt.
Useful for solving ODEs with initial conditions, circuit analysis.
Transforms: 1 → 1/s. e^(at) → 1/(s-a). sin(ωt) → ω/(s²+ω²). δ(t) → 1.
Convolution theorem: L[f*g] = F(s)G(s).
Final value theorem: lim(t→∞) f(t) = lim(s→0) sF(s).
Inverse: Bromwich integral (complex contour integral).

GREEN'S FUNCTIONS:
Solution of L[G(x,x')] = δ(x-x') where L is linear differential operator.
Particular solution: y(x) = ∫G(x,x')f(x')dx'.
Physically: response at x to impulse at x'.
Retarded Green's function: satisfies causality (effect after cause).
Applications: electrostatics, quantum scattering, wave propagation.

IMPORTANT CONSTANTS AND IDENTITIES:
Cauchy integral: ∮ z^n dz = 2πi δ_{n,-1}.
Parseval: ∫|f|²dx = ∫|F|²dk.
Convolution: (f*g)(x) = ∫f(y)g(x-y)dy.
Dirac delta: ∫f(x)δ(x-a)dx = f(a). δ(g(x)) = Σ δ(x-xᵢ)/|g'(xᵢ)| (xᵢ = zeros of g).
Step function: θ(x) = 0 (x<0), 1 (x>0). dθ/dx = δ(x).
