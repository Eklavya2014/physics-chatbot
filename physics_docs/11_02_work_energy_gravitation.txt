CLASS 11 PHYSICS - WORK, ENERGY, POWER AND GRAVITATION
=======================================================

CHAPTER: WORK, ENERGY AND POWER

WORK:
Work is done when a force displaces a body.
W = F⃗·s⃗ = Fs cos θ
Where θ = angle between force and displacement.
- Work is a scalar quantity.
- SI unit: Joule (J) = N·m
- Work is positive when force and displacement are in same direction (θ < 90°).
- Work is negative when force opposes displacement (θ > 90°).
- Zero work: force ⊥ displacement (θ = 90°), e.g., centripetal force.

KINETIC ENERGY (KE):
Energy possessed by a body due to its motion.
KE = ½mv²
Work-Energy Theorem: W_net = ΔKE = ½mv² - ½mu²

POTENTIAL ENERGY (PE):
Energy stored in a body due to its position or configuration.
- Gravitational PE: U = mgh (near earth's surface)
- Elastic PE (spring): U = ½kx² (k = spring constant, x = compression/extension)

CONSERVATIVE AND NON-CONSERVATIVE FORCES:
- Conservative force: work done is path-independent (gravity, spring force, electrostatic force).
  Work done in a closed path = 0.
- Non-conservative force: work done depends on path (friction, air resistance).

CONSERVATION OF MECHANICAL ENERGY:
For conservative forces: KE + PE = constant
½mv² + mgh = constant
(Only valid when no non-conservative forces act)

POWER:
Rate of doing work.
P = W/t = F·v = F·v cos θ
SI unit: Watt (W) = J/s
1 horsepower (hp) = 746 W
1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J

COLLISIONS:
- Elastic collision: both KE and momentum conserved.
- Inelastic collision: momentum conserved, KE not conserved.
- Perfectly inelastic: objects stick together after collision.

For elastic collision (1D) between masses m₁ and m₂:
v₁' = (m₁-m₂)u₁/(m₁+m₂) + 2m₂u₂/(m₁+m₂)
v₂' = 2m₁u₁/(m₁+m₂) + (m₂-m₁)u₂/(m₁+m₂)

Special cases:
- Equal masses: velocities exchange.
- m₁ >> m₂: heavy body barely slows; light body bounces back at ~2u₁.
- m₂ >> m₁: light body bounces back; heavy body barely moves.

Coefficient of restitution: e = relative velocity of separation / relative velocity of approach
- Elastic: e = 1
- Perfectly inelastic: e = 0
- Inelastic: 0 < e < 1

CHAPTER: SYSTEM OF PARTICLES AND ROTATIONAL MOTION

CENTRE OF MASS:
x_cm = (m₁x₁ + m₂x₂ + ...)/( m₁ + m₂ + ...)
For uniform bodies: CM is at geometric centre.

Newton's 2nd law for system: F_ext = Ma_cm

TORQUE (τ):
τ⃗ = r⃗ × F⃗, |τ| = rF sin θ
Torque causes rotational acceleration.
SI unit: N·m

MOMENT OF INERTIA (I):
Rotational analogue of mass.
I = Σmᵢrᵢ² (for discrete masses)
I = ∫r² dm (for continuous bodies)

Moments of inertia of common bodies (about axis of symmetry):
- Thin rod (about centre): I = ML²/12
- Thin rod (about end): I = ML²/3
- Solid sphere: I = 2MR²/5
- Hollow sphere: I = 2MR²/3
- Solid cylinder/disk: I = MR²/2
- Hollow cylinder/ring: I = MR²
- Rectangular plate: I = M(a²+b²)/12

Parallel axis theorem: I = I_cm + Md²
Perpendicular axis theorem (for planar bodies): I_z = I_x + I_y

ANGULAR MOMENTUM (L):
L⃗ = r⃗ × p⃗ = Iω
τ = dL/dt
Conservation of angular momentum: if τ_ext = 0, L = Iω = constant.
Example: Figure skater spinning faster when arms pulled in (I decreases → ω increases).

ROTATIONAL EQUATIONS (analogous to linear):
- Linear → Rotational
- x → θ
- v → ω
- a → α
- F → τ
- m → I
- p → L
- F=ma → τ=Iα
- KE=½mv² → KE=½Iω²

Rolling motion (without slipping):
v_cm = Rω (condition for pure rolling)
KE_total = ½mv² + ½Iω² = ½mv²(1 + I/mR²)

CHAPTER: GRAVITATION

NEWTON'S LAW OF UNIVERSAL GRAVITATION:
F = Gm₁m₂/r²
G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg² (Universal gravitational constant)
Force is always attractive, acts along the line joining centres.

GRAVITATIONAL FIELD INTENSITY:
g = F/m = GM/r²
Near earth surface: g = 9.8 m/s²

VARIATION OF g:
1. With altitude (h): g_h = g(1 - 2h/R) for h << R
   Exact: g_h = gR²/(R+h)²
2. With depth (d): g_d = g(1 - d/R)
   At centre of earth: g = 0
3. With latitude (φ): g_φ = g - ω²R cos²φ
   g is maximum at poles, minimum at equator.
4. Due to shape: earth is oblate spheroid, so g is slightly more at poles.

GRAVITATIONAL POTENTIAL ENERGY:
U = -GMm/r (negative because gravity is attractive)
At earth's surface: U = -GMm/R

ESCAPE VELOCITY:
Minimum velocity needed to escape earth's gravitational field.
v_e = √(2GM/R) = √(2gR) ≈ 11.2 km/s

ORBITAL VELOCITY:
For satellite in circular orbit at height h:
v_o = √(GM/(R+h)) ≈ √(gR²/(R+h))
For near-earth orbit (h << R): v_o = √(gR) ≈ 7.9 km/s

NOTE: v_e = √2 × v_o (for near earth orbit)

KEPLER'S LAWS OF PLANETARY MOTION:
1. Law of Orbits: All planets move in elliptical orbits with the sun at one focus.
2. Law of Areas: A line joining a planet to the sun sweeps equal areas in equal times.
   (Consequence: planet moves faster when closer to sun - conservation of angular momentum)
3. Law of Periods: T² ∝ r³ (T² = 4π²r³/GM)
   T = orbital period, r = semi-major axis.

GEOSTATIONARY SATELLITES:
- Orbit in equatorial plane from west to east.
- Time period = 24 hours (same as earth's rotation).
- Height ≈ 36,000 km above earth's surface.
- Appear stationary from earth's surface.
- Used for communication and weather observation.

GRAVITATIONAL POTENTIAL:
V = -GM/r (energy per unit mass)
Relationship: g = -dV/dr

BINDING ENERGY OF SATELLITE:
BE = GMm/(2r) = ½|Total energy|
Total energy = KE + PE = -GMm/(2r) [negative, bound system]

IMPORTANT FORMULAS:
- F = Gm₁m₂/r²
- g = GM/R²
- v_escape = √(2gR) = 11.2 km/s
- v_orbital = √(gR) ≈ 7.9 km/s
- T² = 4π²r³/GM (Kepler's 3rd law)
- U = -GMm/r
- g_h = g(R/(R+h))²
- g_d = g(1-d/R)
