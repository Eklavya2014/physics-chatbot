1. BIOLOGICAL STRESS
---------------------
Biological stress: Any internal or external condition that disrupts homeostasis.
Stressors can be physical, chemical, biological, or psychological.

Types of Environmental Stress:
  Abiotic   : Temperature extremes, drought, salinity, UV radiation, hypoxia, heavy metals, pH.
  Biotic    : Pathogens, herbivores, competitors, predators.
  Cellular  : Oxidative stress, ER stress, osmotic stress, DNA damage, heat shock.
  Systemic  : Psychological stress (HPA axis); nutritional stress; exercise.

General Adaptation Syndrome (Selye, 1936):
  Three stages of physiological response to stress:
  1. Alarm reaction: Initial stress → fight-or-flight; cortisol + adrenaline surge.
  2. Resistance (adaptation): Body adjusts; coping mechanisms activated.
  3. Exhaustion: Prolonged stress → resource depletion → breakdown; disease.

1.1 THE STRESS RESPONSE — OVERVIEW
-------------------------------------
Acute stress response:
  Hypothalamus → Sympathetic NS → Adrenal medulla → Adrenaline/Noradrenaline
  → ↑ HR, ↑ BP, ↑ blood glucose, bronchodilation, pupil dilation, ↑ blood flow to muscle.

Chronic stress response (HPA axis):
  Hypothalamus → CRH → Anterior pituitary → ACTH → Adrenal cortex → Cortisol.
  Cortisol effects: ↑ gluconeogenesis, ↑ lipolysis, ↑ proteolysis, anti-inflammatory,
  immunosuppressive. Negative feedback to hypothalamus/pituitary.

Chronic stress pathology:
  Sustained cortisol → hippocampal damage (CA3 neuronal atrophy); immunosuppression;
  metabolic dysfunction (visceral obesity, insulin resistance); CVD; depression/anxiety.

2. CELLULAR STRESS RESPONSES
------------------------------
Heat Shock Response (HSR):
  Heat, oxidative stress, heavy metals → protein unfolding/aggregation →
  HSF1 (Heat Shock Factor 1) released from Hsp90 → trimerises → binds HSEs (heat shock elements)
  → upregulates Hsp70, Hsp90, Hsp27, Hsp40 → chaperone induction → protein refolding.
  Hsp70 (major inducible chaperone): Prevents aggregation; assists folding; targets
  misfolded proteins to proteasome. ATPase activity drives chaperone cycle.
  Small HSPs (Hsp27): Form oligomers; holdase chaperones; protect actin cytoskeleton.
  Hsp90: Stabilises kinases, steroid receptors, HIF-1alpha. Inhibition (geldanamycin,
  17-AAG) → cancer drug target.

Unfolded Protein Response (UPR) — ER stress:
  Misfolded proteins accumulate in ER → 3 sensors activated:
  1. IRE1alpha: Splices XBP1 mRNA → active transcription factor → chaperones + ERAD genes.
  2. PERK: Phosphorylates eIF2alpha → global translation inhibition + ATF4 translation
     → stress-adaptive genes (CHOP, GADD34).
  3. ATF6: Translocates to Golgi → cleaved → active TF → chaperone expression.
  Adaptive UPR: Increases ER folding capacity; reduces load. Chronic UPR → CHOP → apoptosis.
  Disease relevance: Diabetes (beta cell ER stress); Parkinson's (alpha-synuclein ER stress);
  atherosclerosis (macrophage ER stress → foam cell apoptosis).

Oxidative Stress:
  ROS (Reactive Oxygen Species): Superoxide (O2•-), hydrogen peroxide (H2O2), hydroxyl radical (•OH).
  Sources: Mitochondrial ETC leakage (~1–3% electrons → O2•-); NADPH oxidase (NOX); XO; inflammation.
  Damage targets: Lipid peroxidation (PUFA in membranes → MDA, 4-HNE); protein carbonylation;
  DNA oxidation (8-oxoguanine → G:C→T:A transversions).
  Antioxidant defences:
    Enzymatic: SOD (O2•- → H2O2), Catalase (H2O2 → H2O + O2), GPx (H2O2 → H2O using GSH),
    Peroxiredoxins (Prx), Thioredoxin system.
    Non-enzymatic: Glutathione (GSH — most abundant; regenerated by GR using NADPH),
    Vitamins C + E, beta-carotene, uric acid.
  Nrf2-Keap1 pathway: Master antioxidant regulator.
    Keap1: Cysteine-rich sensor; keeps Nrf2 cytoplasmic + targeted for degradation (Cul3 E3 ligase).
    Oxidative/electrophilic stress → Keap1 Cys modification → Nrf2 escapes → nucleus →
    Maf heterodimer → ARE (Antioxidant Response Element) → HO-1, NQO1, GCL, GPx → defence.
  Hormesis: Low levels of ROS/stress → adaptive response → stronger than baseline. Concept
  that mild stressors (exercise, CR, phytochemicals) extend healthspan via hormetic mechanisms.

3. HYPOXIA RESPONSE
--------------------
Hypoxia: O2 levels below normal (~5% O2 vs atmospheric ~21%).
HIF (Hypoxia-Inducible Factor) pathway: Master regulator.

Normoxia: HIF-1alpha is hydroxylated by PHD enzymes (prolyl hydroxylases — O2-dependent) →
  recognised by VHL E3 ligase → ubiquitinated → proteasomal degradation.

Hypoxia: PHDs inactive (no O2 substrate) → HIF-1alpha stable → dimerises with ARNT/HIF-1beta →
  binds HRE (hypoxia response elements) in promoters →
  Target genes:
    Glycolysis: LDHA, PDK1, GLUT1/3, BNIP3 → switch to anaerobic.
    Angiogenesis: VEGF-A → endothelial proliferation → new vessels.
    Erythropoiesis: EPO → red cell production → O2 carrying capacity ↑.
    Cell survival: BNIP3L (mitophagy), Bcl-2.
    Metastasis: LOX (collagen crosslinking → stiff ECM), EMT genes.

High-altitude adaptation:
  Tibetans: EPAS1 (HIF-2alpha) variant from Denisovans → lower Hb response to hypoxia
  → reduced blood viscosity → better cerebral perfusion. Andeans: Different mechanism
  (↑ Hb; 2,3-BPG adjustment). Ethiopian highlanders: Yet another mechanism.

4. PLANT STRESS RESPONSES
---------------------------
Drought/Water Stress:
  ABA (abscisic acid) produced in drying roots → transported to leaves →
  guards cells: H2O2 → activates plasma membrane RBOH (NADPH oxidase) → O2•- → H2O2 →
  OST1/SnRK2 → SLAC1 anion channel → Cl-/malate efflux → turgor loss → stomata close.
  ABA signalling: PYR/PYL/RCAR receptors bind ABA → inhibit PP2C phosphatases →
  SnRK2 kinases active → stomatal closure + stress gene expression.

Heat Stress:
  Plants: HSF family (HsfA1a master regulator) → Hsp90, Hsp70, sHSPs → thermotolerance.
  Memory: Priming — brief heat stress → epigenetic memory (H3K4me3 at stress genes) →
  enhanced response to subsequent stress (heat stress memory).

Cold Acclimation:
  Chilling → calcium signal → CBF/DREB transcription factors → COR (cold-regulated) genes →
  cryoprotectant accumulation (compatible solutes: proline, sucrose, raffinose, betaine).
  Membrane fluidity maintained by ↑ unsaturated fatty acid proportion (FAD desaturases).
  Freezing tolerance: Ice crystal formation → cell dehydration → proteins protect.
  Antifreeze proteins: Some plants produce AFP-like proteins; inhibit ice recrystallisation.

5. EPIGENETIC STRESS MEMORY
-----------------------------
Priming and Stress Memory:
  Organisms retain "memory" of prior stress → faster/stronger response to repeat stress.
  Molecular basis: Histone modifications (H3K4me3 at stress gene promoters);
  DNA methylation changes; chromatin looping; transcriptional memory (paused RNAPII).
  Transgenerational stress memory: Some stress responses transmitted to offspring.
    Plants: Heat-primed Arabidopsis → offspring show stress-specific methylation changes.
    Nematodes: Starvation memory transmitted for multiple generations via small RNA pathway.
    Mammals: Paternal psychological stress → sperm miRNA changes → offspring anxiety-like phenotype.
    Fetal programming (Barker hypothesis): Poor maternal nutrition → epigenetic changes in
    foetus → predisposition to obesity, CVD, T2DM in adult life.

6. DESICCATION & EXTREME STRESS TOLERANCE
-------------------------------------------
Tardigrade Desiccation Tolerance:
  Anhydrobiosis: Near-complete loss of body water → metabolic halt.
  Mechanisms: Trehalose replaces water around membranes/proteins (vitrification).
  Tardigrade-specific proteins: CAHS (cytoplasmic abundant heat-soluble) proteins →
  form protective gels on drying → protect cellular structures.
  DSUP (Damage Suppressor protein): Binds chromatin → reduces X-ray-induced DNA strand breaks.
  Life history: Remain viable for decades in dehydrated state; survive in space.

Resurrection Plants:
  Selaginella lepidophylla, Myrothamnus, Craterostigma plantagineum: Lose >95% water →
  appear dead → fully revive with rehydration within hours/days.
  Mechanisms: Folded proteins (LEA proteins — Late Embryogenesis Abundant); sucrose vitrification;
  antioxidant systems preserved; cell wall folding (no cell collapse); reversible membrane reorganisation.
  Comparative genomics: Resurrection plants have large expansions in stress-response gene families.

Deinococcus radiodurans:
  Most radiation-resistant organism known. Survives 5,000 Gy (x-ray); 15,000 Gy (charged particles).
  Humans: lethal dose ~5 Gy. Genome shattered into hundreds of fragments → reassembled perfectly within hours.
  Mechanisms: Extremely efficient DSB repair (extended synthesis-dependent strand annealing);
  high Mn2+:Fe3+ ratio → prevents protein oxidation; highly efficient antioxidant systems;
  genome redundancy (multiple copies of chromosome in cell).

[BIOTECHNOLOGY] Stress biology → crop improvement:
  Engineering DREB/CBF overexpression → drought + cold tolerance in wheat/soybean.
  CRISPR knockout of OsMAPK5 → broad-spectrum disease resistance in rice.
  LEA protein overexpression → desiccation tolerance in crop seeds.
  Engineering Nrf2 pathway → antioxidant protection for ischaemia-reperfusion injury.
  Understanding heat shock mechanisms → guide breeding for climate-resilient crops.
