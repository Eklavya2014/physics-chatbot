1. VIRUS DIVERSITY & CLASSIFICATION
-------------------------------------
Viruses infect all cellular life. ~6,800 formally classified species but estimated
millions exist. Smallest: ~17 nm (circovirus); largest: ~1,500 nm (pandoravirus).

Baltimore Classification (7 classes based on genome type and replication strategy):
  Class I  : dsDNA → mRNA. (Herpesviridae, Adenoviridae, Poxviridae, Papillomaviridae)
  Class II : ssDNA → dsDNA → mRNA. (Parvoviridae, Anelloviridae)
  Class III: dsRNA → mRNA. (Reoviridae — Rotavirus, Reovirus)
  Class IV : +ssRNA = mRNA → directly translated. (Picornaviridae, Flaviviridae, Coronaviridae)
  Class V  : -ssRNA → mRNA (via RdRp). (Orthomyxoviridae, Paramyxoviridae, Filoviridae, Rhabdoviridae)
  Class VI : +ssRNA → DNA (via reverse transcriptase) → mRNA. (Retroviridae — HIV, HTLV)
  Class VII: dsDNA with ssDNA intermediate. (Hepadnaviridae — Hepatitis B)

2. VIRAL ENTRY & HOST CELL INTERACTIONS
-----------------------------------------
Receptor-Mediated Entry:
  Virus → binds specific cell surface receptor (tropism determinant) → entry:
  Endocytosis: Clathrin-mediated or macropinocytosis → endosome → low pH → conformational
    change → membrane fusion or capsid escape.
  Direct membrane fusion (enveloped viruses): Fusion protein activated at surface or in endosome.
  Non-enveloped virus entry: Capsid disrupts membrane; pore formation; genome injection.

Key Receptor Examples:
  SARS-CoV-2  : ACE2 (Spike RBD); co-factor: TMPRSS2 (priming). Lung type II cells, gut epithelium.
  HIV-1       : CD4 + CCR5/CXCR4 (co-receptors). CD4+ T cells, macrophages, DCs.
  Influenza   : Sialic acid (alpha-2,3 linked: birds; alpha-2,6: humans). Respiratory epithelium.
  Rhinovirus  : ICAM-1 (major group) or LDL-R. Upper respiratory epithelium.
  EBV         : CD21 (CR2) on B cells; HLA class II. Also epithelial cells (gH/gL/gp42).
  Rabies      : nAChR, NCAM, p75NTR. Neuromuscular junction → retrograde axonal transport → brain.
  Poliovirus  : CD155 (PVR). Motor neuron tropism explains paralysis.
  Measles     : CD46 (most cell types); SLAMF1 (immune cells). Airborne; highly infectious.

Viral Replication Compartments:
  DNA viruses: Replicate in nucleus (exception: Poxviruses replicate in cytoplasm).
  RNA viruses: Replicate in cytoplasm (exception: Influenza, Bornavirus — replicate in nucleus).
  Positive-sense RNA viruses: Replication on membrane-associated organelles (DMVs — double-membrane vesicles)
    formed by hijacking ER/Golgi. NSP3/4/6 in SARS-CoV-2 remodel membranes.

3. SPECIFIC VIRUS BIOLOGY
---------------------------
Influenza Virology:
  Genome: 8 segments of -ssRNA → allows reassortment.
  HA (Haemagglutinin): Binds sialic acid. 18 subtypes (H1–H18).
  NA (Neuraminidase): Cleaves sialic acid → virus release + spread. 11 subtypes (N1–N11).
  H1N1, H3N2: Currently circulating in humans.
  H5N1 (avian): Binds alpha-2,3 sialic acid → lower respiratory tract; high CFR (~60%).
  M2 ion channel: Allows H+ into virion → HA conformational change. Target of amantadine.
  NA inhibitors: Oseltamivir (Tamiflu), zanamivir (Relenza). Prevent virion release.
  Baloxavir: PA endonuclease inhibitor → cap-snatching inhibition → all influenza segments affected.

Herpesvirus Latency:
  All 8 human herpesviruses establish lifelong latency.
  HSV-1/2: Latency in sensory ganglia neurons. LAT (Latency-Associated Transcript) non-coding
    RNA maintains latency; suppresses lytic gene expression.
    Reactivation: Stress, UV, immunosuppression → viral IE genes expressed → lytic cycle.
  EBV: Latency in memory B cells. 3 latency programs:
    Latency 0: Silent; memory B cells; no viral protein.
    Latency I: EBNA1 only; Burkitt lymphoma.
    Latency II: EBNA1 + LMP1 + LMP2; NPC, Hodgkin lymphoma.
    Latency III: All EBNAs + LMPs; immunoblastic lymphoma (immunocompromised).
  HCMV: Latency in CD34+ myeloid progenitors; reactivation on differentiation to macrophages/DCs.
    Major cause of morbidity in transplant patients.
  VZV: Primary infection = chickenpox. Latency in dorsal root ganglia. Reactivation = shingles (zoster).
    Shingrix vaccine (recombinant gE antigen + AS01B adjuvant): >90% protective against zoster.

5. INNATE IMMUNE EVASION BY VIRUSES
-------------------------------------
Pattern Recognition of Viral PAMPs:
  dsRNA (replication intermediate) → TLR3 (endosome), MDA5/RIG-I (cytoplasm).
  ssRNA → TLR7/8 (endosome).
  DNA (cytoplasmic) → cGAS → 2'3'-cGAMP → STING → TBK1 → IRF3 → IFN-beta.

Viral IFN Antagonism:
  SARS-CoV-2: NSP1 (blocks mRNA translation); NSP3 (deISGylase; removes ISG15); NSP12
    (RdRp — rapid replication outpaces IFN response); ORF6 (blocks nuclear import of STAT1).
  Influenza NS1: Binds dsRNA (hides from MDA5/RIG-I); binds TRIM25 (prevents RIG-I ubiquitination);
    binds p85beta (PI3K → cell survival); binds PABPII (nuclear export block).
  HIV Vif: Degrades APOBEC3G (restriction factor that mutates viral DNA).
  HIV Vpx (HIV-2, SIV): Degrades SAMHD1 (dNTPase depletes dNTP pool → restricts reverse transcription in macrophages).
  EBV EBNA1: GAr repeat domain prevents proteasomal degradation + MHC class I presentation.
  HSV ICP34.5: Dephosphorylates eIF2alpha (reverses PKR-mediated translational shutdown).

6. EMERGING VIRUSES & PANDEMIC BIOLOGY
-----------------------------------------
Spillover and Emergence:
  Ecological drivers: Deforestation, live animal markets, bushmeat hunting, climate change,
  urbanisation, globalisation → increased human-wildlife contact → spillover events.
  Most EIDs are zoonotic. Reservoir hosts often show no disease (co-evolved tolerance).
  Bat reservoirs: SARS-CoV-2 (closest relative in Rhinolophus bats), MERS-CoV (camels/bats),
    Ebola (Rousettus bats), Nipah (Pteropus bats), Rabies, Hendra.

Pandemic Preparedness:
  WHO PHEIC (Public Health Emergency of International Concern): Highest alert level.
    COVID-19 (2020–2023), Mpox (2022, 2024), SARS (2003), H1N1 (2009), polio, Ebola.
  100 Days Mission: WHO/CEPI goal to have vaccines for novel pathogen within 100 days.
  mRNA platform technology: Enabled rapid COVID-19 vaccine design (Moderna: ~2 days to design;
    Pfizer-BioNTech: vaccine candidate selected from design in 4 hours).
  Broad-spectrum antivirals: Remdesivir (RdRp), molnupiravir (mutagenesis), favipiravir.
  Universal influenza vaccine: Targeting conserved HA stalk region (vs variable head).
    BioNTech mRNA-1010 (Moderna): Targeting 4 influenza strains; clinical trials.

Giant Viruses:
  Mimivirus (discovered 2003): 750 nm; 1.2 Mb genome; larger than many bacteria.
  Pandoravirus: 1.5 µm; 2.7 Mb genome.
  Pithovirus: 1.5 µm long; found in 30,000-year-old Siberian permafrost; still infectious.
  Megavirus, Tupanvirus: Other giant virus groups.
  Encode aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases, DNA repair genes — blur boundary between virus and cell.
  Virophages: Sputnik virophage parasitises Mimivirus — "virus of a virus."

[HISTORICAL MILESTONES] Major virus discoveries:
  1892: Tobacco Mosaic Virus (Ivanovsky) — first virus.
  1898: Foot-and-mouth disease virus (Loeffler & Frosch) — first animal virus.
  1901: Yellow fever virus (Reed) — first human virus.
  1908: Poliomyelitis virus (Landsteiner & Popper).
  1911: Rous sarcoma virus (Rous, Nobel 1966) — first tumour virus.
  1935: TMV crystallised (Stanley, Nobel 1946) — viruses as chemicals.
  1953: Phage DNA injection confirmed (Hershey-Chase).
  1970: Reverse transcriptase discovered (Baltimore, Temin, Nobel 1975).
  1983: HIV discovered (Barré-Sinoussi, Montagnier, Nobel 2008).
  2003: SARS-CoV identified in 4 months — unprecedented speed.
  2020: SARS-CoV-2 genome sequenced within 2 weeks; vaccines approved within 11 months.
