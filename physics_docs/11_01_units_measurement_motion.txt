CHAPTER: UNITS AND MEASUREMENT

PHYSICAL QUANTITIES
A physical quantity is anything that can be measured. It has two parts: magnitude and unit.
- Fundamental (Base) quantities: Cannot be expressed in terms of other quantities.
- Derived quantities: Expressed in terms of fundamental quantities.

SI BASE UNITS (7 Fundamental Units):
1. Length → metre (m)
2. Mass → kilogram (kg)
3. Time → second (s)
4. Electric current → Ampere (A)
5. Temperature → Kelvin (K)
6. Luminous intensity → Candela (cd)
7. Amount of substance → Mole (mol)

SUPPLEMENTARY UNITS:
- Plane angle → radian (rad)
- Solid angle → steradian (sr)

SIGNIFICANT FIGURES RULES:
1. All non-zero digits are significant.
2. Zeros between non-zero digits are significant.
3. Leading zeros are NOT significant.
4. Trailing zeros after decimal are significant.
5. Trailing zeros without decimal may or may not be significant.

ERRORS IN MEASUREMENT:
- Absolute error: |measured - true|
- Mean absolute error: average of all absolute errors
- Relative error: mean absolute error / true value
- Percentage error: relative error × 100%

Error propagation:
- Addition/Subtraction: Absolute errors add: ΔZ = ΔA + ΔB
- Multiplication/Division: Relative errors add: ΔZ/Z = ΔA/A + ΔB/B
- Power: ΔZ/Z = n × ΔA/A (for Z = Aⁿ)

DIMENSIONAL ANALYSIS:
Dimensions of base quantities:
[M] = mass, [L] = length, [T] = time, [A] = current, [K] = temperature

Common dimensional formulas:
- Velocity: [LT⁻¹]
- Acceleration: [LT⁻²]
- Force: [MLT⁻²]
- Energy/Work: [ML²T⁻²]
- Power: [ML²T⁻³]
- Pressure: [ML⁻¹T⁻²]
- Momentum: [MLT⁻¹]
- Angular momentum: [ML²T⁻¹]
- Gravitational constant G: [M⁻¹L³T⁻²]
- Planck's constant h: [ML²T⁻¹]
- Boltzmann constant k: [ML²T⁻²K⁻¹]

Applications of dimensional analysis:
1. Check correctness of equations.
2. Derive relationships between quantities.
3. Convert units from one system to another.

Limitations: Cannot find dimensionless constants; fails if equation involves trigonometric/logarithmic functions.

CHAPTER: MOTION IN A STRAIGHT LINE (KINEMATICS)

BASIC DEFINITIONS:
- Distance: Total path length (scalar).
- Displacement: Shortest distance from initial to final position (vector).
- Speed: Distance/time (scalar).
- Velocity: Displacement/time (vector).
- Acceleration: Rate of change of velocity (vector).

EQUATIONS OF UNIFORMLY ACCELERATED MOTION:
1. v = u + at
2. s = ut + ½at²
3. v² = u² + 2as
4. s_n = u + a(2n-1)/2  [distance in nth second]

Where: u = initial velocity, v = final velocity, a = acceleration, s = displacement, t = time.

GRAPHS:
- Displacement-time graph: slope = velocity
- Velocity-time graph: slope = acceleration, area under curve = displacement
- Acceleration-time graph: area under curve = change in velocity

FREE FALL:
- Acceleration = g = 9.8 m/s² downward
- All objects fall with same acceleration in vacuum regardless of mass.
- For object dropped from rest: v = gt, h = ½gt², v² = 2gh

RELATIVE VELOCITY:
Velocity of A relative to B: v_AB = v_A - v_B

CHAPTER: MOTION IN A PLANE (2D MOTION)

VECTORS:
- Scalar: magnitude only (mass, speed, temperature)
- Vector: magnitude + direction (displacement, velocity, force)

Vector addition:
- Triangle law: Place vectors head to tail; resultant is closing side.
- Parallelogram law: R² = A² + B² + 2AB cos θ
- tan α = B sin θ / (A + B cos θ)

Unit vector: â = A⃗/|A| (magnitude = 1, gives direction only)

Dot product (scalar product): A⃗·B⃗ = AB cos θ
- Result is scalar.
- A⃗·A⃗ = A² (î·î = ĵ·ĵ = k̂·k̂ = 1)
- î·ĵ = ĵ·k̂ = k̂·î = 0

Cross product (vector product): |A⃗×B⃗| = AB sin θ
- Result is vector, perpendicular to both A and B.
- î×ĵ = k̂, ĵ×k̂ = î, k̂×î = ĵ
- A⃗×A⃗ = 0

PROJECTILE MOTION:
A projectile is an object thrown at an angle to the horizontal.
Horizontal: uniform velocity (no acceleration)
Vertical: uniformly accelerated (acceleration = g downward)

Key equations (θ = angle of projection, u = initial speed):
- Horizontal velocity: uₓ = u cos θ (constant)
- Vertical velocity: uᵧ = u sin θ - gt
- Horizontal displacement: x = u cos θ · t
- Vertical displacement: y = u sin θ · t - ½gt²
- Time of flight: T = 2u sin θ / g
- Maximum height: H = u² sin²θ / 2g
- Range: R = u² sin 2θ / g
- Maximum range: R_max = u²/g (at θ = 45°)
- Equation of trajectory: y = x tan θ - gx²/(2u² cos²θ) [parabola]

CIRCULAR MOTION:
- Angular displacement: θ (radians)
- Angular velocity: ω = dθ/dt (rad/s)
- Angular acceleration: α = dω/dt (rad/s²)
- Linear velocity: v = rω
- Centripetal acceleration: a_c = v²/r = ω²r (directed toward center)
- Centripetal force: F_c = mv²/r = mω²r
- Period: T = 2π/ω = 2πr/v
- Frequency: f = 1/T

UNIFORM CIRCULAR MOTION:
Speed is constant but velocity changes direction continuously.
Centripetal acceleration is always perpendicular to velocity.

CHAPTER: LAWS OF MOTION

NEWTON'S FIRST LAW (Law of Inertia):
A body continues in its state of rest or uniform motion unless acted upon by a net external force.
- Inertia: resistance to change in state of motion.
- Mass is a measure of inertia.

NEWTON'S SECOND LAW:
F⃗ = ma⃗ = dp⃗/dt
Net force = rate of change of momentum.
- 1 Newton = force that gives 1 kg mass an acceleration of 1 m/s².

NEWTON'S THIRD LAW:
For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
- Forces act on DIFFERENT bodies.
- Action-reaction forces are always equal in magnitude and opposite in direction.

MOMENTUM AND IMPULSE:
- Linear momentum: p⃗ = mv⃗ (SI unit: kg·m/s)
- Impulse: J = F·Δt = Δp (change in momentum)
- Conservation of momentum: total momentum is conserved when no external force acts.

FRICTION:
- Static friction: prevents relative motion between surfaces at rest.
  f_s ≤ μ_s N (μ_s = coefficient of static friction)
- Kinetic friction: opposes relative motion between sliding surfaces.
  f_k = μ_k N (μ_k = coefficient of kinetic friction)
- μ_k < μ_s always.
- Rolling friction: much smaller than sliding friction.
- Friction is independent of area of contact (for a given normal force).

CIRCULAR MOTION DYNAMICS:
- Banking of roads: tan θ = v²/rg (θ = banking angle)
- With friction: v_max = √(rg(tan θ + μ)/(1 - μ tan θ))
- Conical pendulum: T = 2π√(l cos θ/g)

EQUILIBRIUM:
- Translational equilibrium: ΣF = 0
- Rotational equilibrium: Στ = 0 (net torque = 0)

IMPORTANT FORMULAS SUMMARY:
- v = u + at
- s = ut + ½at²
- v² = u² + 2as
- R (projectile) = u² sin 2θ / g
- H (max height) = u² sin²θ / 2g
- F = ma
- p = mv
- f_k = μ_k N
- Centripetal force = mv²/r
