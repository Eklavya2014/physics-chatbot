CLASS 12 PHYSICS - MAGNETISM, EMI, AC CIRCUITS AND EM WAVES
============================================================

CHAPTER: MOVING CHARGES AND MAGNETISM

MAGNETIC FORCE:
On a moving charge: F⃗ = q(v⃗ × B⃗), |F| = qvB sin θ
On a current-carrying conductor: F⃗ = I(L⃗ × B⃗), |F| = BIL sin θ

Lorentz force: F⃗ = q(E⃗ + v⃗ × B⃗)

MOTION OF CHARGED PARTICLE IN MAGNETIC FIELD:
- Circular motion when v ⊥ B.
- Radius: r = mv/(qB)
- Time period: T = 2πm/(qB) (independent of speed!)
- Frequency (cyclotron frequency): f = qB/(2πm)
- Helical path when v has component along B.

BIOT-SAVART LAW:
dB⃗ = (μ₀/4π) × I(dL⃗ × r̂)/r²
μ₀ = 4π × 10⁻⁷ T·m/A (permeability of free space)

Magnetic field of straight wire:
B = μ₀I/(2πr) (at distance r from infinite wire)
Direction: Right-hand rule (curl fingers in direction of B, thumb = I)

Magnetic field at centre of circular loop:
B = μ₀I/(2R) (R = radius)

AMPERE'S CIRCUITAL LAW:
∮B⃗·dL⃗ = μ₀I_enclosed
Applications:
- Infinite solenoid: B = μ₀nI (n = turns per unit length), B = 0 outside.
- Toroid: B = μ₀NI/(2πr) inside, B = 0 outside.

FORCE BETWEEN PARALLEL WIRES:
F/L = μ₀I₁I₂/(2πd) per unit length
- Parallel currents attract; antiparallel repel.
- 1 Ampere defined using this force.

MAGNETIC MOMENT:
Current loop: m = IA (A = area, direction by right-hand rule)
Torque on loop: τ = m × B = mB sin θ
PE: U = -m⃗·B⃗ = -mB cos θ

GALVANOMETER, VOLTMETER, AMMETER:
- Galvanometer: measures small current. Uses torque on current loop.
- Ammeter: galvanometer + low resistance (shunt) in parallel. Ig × G = (I - Ig) × S
- Voltmeter: galvanometer + high resistance in series. V = Ig(G + R)

CHAPTER: MAGNETISM AND MATTER

MAGNETIC MATERIALS:
- Diamagnetic: weakly repelled by magnets (Bi, Cu, water). χ < 0, μ_r < 1.
- Paramagnetic: weakly attracted (Al, Na, O₂). χ > 0, μ_r slightly > 1.
- Ferromagnetic: strongly attracted (Fe, Ni, Co). χ >> 0, μ_r >> 1.

CURIE'S LAW (paramagnetic): χ = C/T (C = Curie constant)
Above Curie temperature: ferromagnetic → paramagnetic.

HYSTERESIS LOOP:
B-H graph for ferromagnetic material.
- Retentivity: residual magnetism when H = 0.
- Coercivity: reverse H needed to demagnetize.
- Hard magnetic materials: high coercivity (permanent magnets).
- Soft magnetic materials: low coercivity (transformer cores, electromagnets).

CHAPTER: ELECTROMAGNETIC INDUCTION

FARADAY'S LAWS:
1. Whenever magnetic flux through a circuit changes, EMF is induced.
2. Induced EMF = rate of change of flux: ε = -dΦ/dt

Magnetic flux: Φ = B⃗·A⃗ = BA cos θ
SI unit: Weber (Wb)

LENZ'S LAW:
Induced current opposes the change causing it (consequence of energy conservation).

MOTIONAL EMF:
For rod moving with velocity v in field B: ε = Bvl
For rotating rod: ε = ½BωL²

SELF-INDUCTANCE:
L = NΦ/I = ε/(dI/dt)
Back EMF: ε = -L(dI/dt)
Energy stored: U = ½LI²
Solenoid: L = μ₀n²V = μ₀N²A/l

MUTUAL INDUCTANCE:
M = N₂Φ₂/I₁
ε₂ = -M(dI₁/dt)
Two coils: M = μ₀N₁N₂A/l (coaxial solenoids)
M ≤ √(L₁L₂), equality when perfectly coupled: M = k√(L₁L₂), k = coupling coefficient.

EDDY CURRENTS:
Currents induced in conducting bulk materials due to changing B field.
- Cause heating (energy loss).
- Used in induction heating, braking, speedometers.
- Minimized by laminating cores.

CHAPTER: ALTERNATING CURRENT

AC QUANTITIES:
- Instantaneous: e = E₀ sin ωt, i = I₀ sin(ωt + φ)
- Peak/Amplitude: E₀, I₀
- RMS: E_rms = E₀/√2, I_rms = I₀/√2
- Average of full cycle = 0; average of half cycle = 2I₀/π

PHASORS:
Rotating vectors that represent AC quantities. Angle = phase.

REACTANCE AND IMPEDANCE:
- Resistor: V and I in phase. Z = R.
- Inductor: V leads I by 90°. X_L = ωL.
- Capacitor: I leads V by 90°. X_C = 1/(ωC).

SERIES RLC CIRCUIT:
Z = √(R² + (X_L - X_C)²)
Phase angle: tan φ = (X_L - X_C)/R
I = V/Z

RESONANCE:
X_L = X_C → ω₀ = 1/√(LC), f₀ = 1/(2π√(LC))
At resonance: Z = R (minimum), I = V/R (maximum), power factor = 1.

Quality factor: Q = ω₀L/R = 1/(ω₀CR) = (1/R)√(L/C)

POWER IN AC CIRCUITS:
P = V_rms × I_rms × cos φ
Power factor: cos φ = R/Z
- Purely resistive: cos φ = 1 (P = V_rms × I_rms)
- Purely reactive: cos φ = 0 (P = 0, wattless current)

TRANSFORMER:
V_s/V_p = N_s/N_p = I_p/I_s (for ideal transformer)
Step-up: N_s > N_p (increases V, decreases I)
Step-down: N_s < N_p (decreases V, increases I)
Efficiency: η = P_out/P_in × 100%
Losses: copper loss (I²R), iron loss (eddy currents + hysteresis)

CHAPTER: ELECTROMAGNETIC WAVES

MAXWELL'S EQUATIONS (qualitative):
1. Gauss's law for E: ∮E⃗·dA⃗ = q/ε₀
2. Gauss's law for B: ∮B⃗·dA⃗ = 0 (no magnetic monopoles)
3. Faraday's law: ∮E⃗·dL⃗ = -dΦ_B/dt
4. Ampere-Maxwell law: ∮B⃗·dL⃗ = μ₀I + μ₀ε₀(dΦ_E/dt)
Displacement current: I_d = ε₀(dΦ_E/dt)

ELECTROMAGNETIC WAVES:
- Produced by accelerating charges.
- E and B are perpendicular to each other and to direction of propagation.
- Speed: c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) = 3 × 10⁸ m/s in vacuum.
- In medium: v = c/n (n = refractive index).
- E₀/B₀ = c.
- Transverse waves; carry energy and momentum.
- Intensity: I = ½cε₀E₀² = P/A.

ELECTROMAGNETIC SPECTRUM (increasing wavelength / decreasing frequency):
1. Gamma rays: λ < 0.01 nm. Nuclear transitions. Most energetic.
2. X-rays: 0.01 nm - 10 nm. Inner electron transitions. Medical imaging.
3. Ultraviolet: 10 nm - 400 nm. Electron transitions. Causes sunburn.
4. Visible light: 400 nm - 700 nm. Violet to Red. Seen by human eye.
5. Infrared: 700 nm - 1 mm. Molecular vibrations. Heat radiation.
6. Microwaves: 1 mm - 10 cm. Radar, microwave oven, WiFi.
7. Radio waves: > 10 cm. Communication. Longest wavelength.

Key wavelengths of visible light:
Violet: 380-450 nm, Blue: 450-495 nm, Green: 495-570 nm
Yellow: 570-590 nm, Orange: 590-620 nm, Red: 620-750 nm

IMPORTANT FORMULAS:
- ε = -dΦ/dt (Faraday's law)
- X_L = ωL, X_C = 1/(ωC)
- Z = √(R² + (X_L - X_C)²)
- ω₀ = 1/√(LC) (resonance)
- P = V_rms I_rms cos φ
- V_s/V_p = N_s/N_p (transformer)
- c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) = 3 × 10⁸ m/s
