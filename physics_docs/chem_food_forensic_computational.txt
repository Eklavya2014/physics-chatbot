FOOD CHEMISTRY, FORENSIC CHEMISTRY AND COMPUTATIONAL CHEMISTRY
================================================================

PART 1: FOOD CHEMISTRY

WATER IN FOOD:
Water activity (aw): aw = P/P₀ = partial pressure of water / vapor pressure of pure water.
aw determines microbial growth, chemical reactions, shelf life.
aw < 0.6: no microbial growth. aw < 0.3: very stable.
Methods of reducing aw: drying, adding sugar/salt (osmotic), freezing.
Maillard reaction requires some water; very dry food doesn't brown as well.

CARBOHYDRATES IN FOOD:
Sugars: sucrose (table sugar), glucose, fructose, lactose, maltose.
Sweetness relative to sucrose (=100): fructose (173), glucose (74), sucrose (100), maltose (32), lactose (16).
Invert sugar: sucrose hydrolyzed to glucose + fructose. Sweeter, hygroscopic.
Caramelization: sugar heated above 160°C → dehydration, isomerization, polymerization → brown color, caramel flavor.
Maillard reaction: reducing sugar + amino acid → melanoidins (brown), complex flavors. Requires heat, low aw.
Key reactions: Amadori rearrangement, Strecker degradation → aroma compounds.
Maillard at: bread crust, roasted coffee, grilled meat, chocolate.
No Maillard in pure fat frying (no amino acids or sugars).

PROTEINS IN FOOD:
Denaturation: unfolding by heat, acid, base, mechanical action.
Coagulation: denatured proteins aggregate (egg white, cheese, tofu).
Gelation: protein network traps water (gelatin, egg custard).
Hydrolysis: enzymatic (cheese ripening, meat tenderizing) or acid/base → shorter peptides → flavor.
Meat tenderizers: papain (papaya), bromelain (pineapple), actinidin (kiwi) → hydrolyze muscle proteins.
Maillard reaction with Lys, Arg reduces nutritive value (loss of essential amino acids).
Functional properties: emulsification (lecithin, egg yolk), foaming (egg white), gelling, water-binding.

LIPIDS IN FOOD:
Fats and oils: saturated fats solid (butter, coconut oil), unsaturated liquid (vegetable oils).
Smoke point: temperature at which oil starts to smoke → breakdown begins → unhealthy compounds.
Hydrogenation: adds H₂ across double bonds. Partial hydrogenation → trans fats (unhealthy).
Oxidative rancidity: autoxidation of unsaturated fatty acids. Free radical chain reaction.
Initiation: R-H + O₂ → R• (heat, light, metals catalyze). Propagation: R• + O₂ → ROO•. ROO• + R-H → ROOH + R•.
Hydrolytic rancidity: enzymes or moisture hydrolyze ester bonds → free fatty acids → off-flavors.
Antioxidants: BHA, BHT (synthetic), vitamin E (natural), vitamin C (water-soluble, prevents initiation), rosemary extract.

EMULSIONS:
Oil-in-water (o/w): oil droplets in water. Milk, mayonnaise, vinaigrette (w/o).
Water-in-oil (w/o): water droplets in oil. Butter, margarine.
Emulsifiers: amphiphilic molecules at interface. Lecithin (egg yolk), milk proteins, mono- and diglycerides.
HLB value (Hydrophilic-Lipophilic Balance): 1-6 for w/o, 8-18 for o/w.
Homogenization: reduce droplet size → more stable emulsion.
Stability: affected by creaming/sedimentation, flocculation, coalescence, Ostwald ripening.

FOOD ADDITIVES:
Preservatives: sodium benzoate (C₆H₅COONa, acidic foods), sodium nitrite (prevents botulism in cured meats), sulfur dioxide (wine, dried fruit).
Antioxidants: BHA, BHT, TBHQ, vitamin E, ascorbic acid.
Colors: carotenoids (natural yellow-orange), anthocyanins (red-blue, pH indicator!), chlorophyll (green), curcumin (yellow), synthetic azo dyes.
Flavors: esters (fruity), aldehydes, ketones, terpenes.
Thickeners: starch, pectin, guar gum, xanthan gum, carrageenan, agar, gelatin.
Pectin: from citrus peel/apple pomace. Methylated galacturonic acid polymer. Forms gel with sugar + acid (jam). Low-methoxy pectin needs Ca²⁺.
Leavening: NaHCO₃ (baking soda) + acid → CO₂. Baking powder = NaHCO₃ + dry acid + starch.
Sweeteners: saccharin (300× sweeter), aspartame (200×), sucralose (600×), stevia (200-300×), acesulfame-K (200×).

FERMENTATION:
Alcoholic fermentation: C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₂H₅OH + 2CO₂ (Saccharomyces cerevisiae).
Beer: malted barley → mash → ferment → hops for bitterness.
Wine: grape juice → yeast → ethanol + CO₂.
Distillation: increases alcohol content (spirits).
Lactic acid fermentation: lactose → lactic acid (yogurt, cheese, sauerkraut, kimchi).
Acetic acid fermentation: ethanol → acetic acid (Acetobacter aerobically). Vinegar.
Bread: yeast fermentation → CO₂ raises dough. Gluten network traps gas.
Soy sauce: koji mold (Aspergillus) on soybeans → enzymes break down proteins and starches → complex flavors.

PART 2: FORENSIC CHEMISTRY

FORENSIC SCIENCE: application of science to legal questions.
Physical evidence: fingerprints, fibers, DNA, gunshot residue, glass, paint, documents, drugs, poisons.

DNA ANALYSIS:
STR (Short Tandem Repeat) analysis: most common in forensics.
13 CODIS loci (FBI database): probability of random match < 1 in 10¹⁸ for unrelated individuals.
PCR amplifies tiny amounts of DNA.
Mitochondrial DNA: maternally inherited, useful for hair without follicle, skeletal remains.
Y-STR: paternal lineage. Useful in sexual assault cases.
Familial DNA searching: partial match → identify relatives → narrow suspects.
Touch DNA: epithelial cells from skin contact. Very low amount → contamination concern.
DNA phenotyping: predict appearance (eye color, hair color, skin tone) from DNA.

FINGERPRINT ANALYSIS:
Three types: loops (60-65%), whorls (30-35%), arches (5%).
Ridge characteristics (minutiae): bifurcations, ridge endings, dots. >12-16 points needed for match.
Development methods:
Latent prints (not visible): aluminium powder (light surfaces), black powder (dark), fluorescent powder.
Cyanoacrylate (super glue) fuming: reacts with sweat residue → white polymer.
Ninhydrin: reacts with amino acids (purple Ruhemann's purple).
1,8-Diazafluoren-9-one (DFO): fluorescence.
Vacuum metal deposition (VMD): most sensitive, especially for plastics.
AFIS: Automated Fingerprint Identification System. Digital comparison.

DRUG ANALYSIS:
Presumptive tests: colorimetric, fast, cheap, but not definitive.
Marquis reagent (H₂SO₄/formaldehyde): heroin (purple → black), MDMA (purple), amphetamine (orange-brown).
Duquenois-Levine: cannabis (purple).
Cobalt thiocyanate: cocaine (blue).
Simon's test: secondary amines (MDMA gives blue, MDA doesn't).
Confirmatory tests: GC-MS (gold standard), HPLC-MS/MS, NMR.
GC-MS identifies: molecular weight (MS), fragmentation pattern (unique fingerprint), retention time (GC).
Illicit drug identification: cocaine, heroin, methamphetamine, MDMA, cannabis, fentanyl.

TOXICOLOGY:
LD₅₀: lethal dose for 50% of test population. Measure of toxicity.
Acute vs chronic poisoning.
Post-mortem toxicology: blood, urine, vitreous humor, hair, liver.
Blood: best for recent exposure. Post-mortem redistribution is concern.
Urine: metabolites. Detection window longer than blood.
Hair: historical record. ~1.5 cm/month growth. Can detect exposure timeline.
Vitreous humor: isolated from circulation → less redistribution. CO, ethanol.

Common poisons:
Cyanide (HCN, KCN): inhibits cytochrome c oxidase → cells can't use O₂. Cherry/bitter almond odor.
Antidote: hydroxocobalamin (binds CN⁻), sodium thiosulfate.
Carbon monoxide: binds hemoglobin (240× stronger than O₂). Cherry-red appearance.
Antidote: 100% O₂, hyperbaric O₂.
Arsenic: trioxide As₂O₃ tasteless, odorless. Inhibits cellular enzymes (thiol groups). Detected by Marsh test, AAS.
Lead: neurological damage (especially children). Inhibits ALA dehydratase (heme synthesis). Blood lead level.
Strychnine: blocks glycine receptors → spastic paralysis.
Organophosphates: irreversible AChE inhibitors. Nerve agents (sarin, VX), pesticides (malathion).

ARSON INVESTIGATION:
Accelerant identification: GC-MS of fire debris. Gasoline, kerosene, diesel have characteristic patterns.
Solid phase microextraction (SPME): extract volatiles from debris.
Comparison with reference standards in library.

DOCUMENT EXAMINATION:
Ink dating: TLC, LC-MS of ink components. Degradation products.
Paper analysis: fiber analysis (microscopy), watermarks, coating composition.
Altered documents: indented writing (ESDA — electrostatic detection apparatus), erasures (IR), additions.
Questioned signatures: comparison with known exemplars.

BALLISTICS AND GUNSHOT RESIDUE (GSR):
GSR particles: lead, antimony, barium from primer ignition. Detected by SEM-EDX.
Unique morphology: spherical solidified droplets.
Collected from: hands, face, clothes within 4-6 hours of discharge.
Primer-less/green ammunition: changing GSR composition.

GLASS ANALYSIS:
Refractive index: measured by immersion method. Very discriminating.
Elemental analysis: LA-ICP-MS. Highly discriminating.
Fracture patterns: radial and concentric cracks. Sequence of impacts.
3R rule: radial cracks form first, concentric cracks terminate at radial.

PART 3: COMPUTATIONAL CHEMISTRY

QUANTUM CHEMISTRY METHODS:
Ab initio: from first principles, no empirical parameters.
Hartree-Fock (HF): mean-field approximation. Each electron sees average field of all others.
Basis sets: mathematical functions to represent orbitals. STO-3G (minimal), 6-31G, cc-pVTZ (large).
Limitations of HF: no electron correlation. Overestimates bond lengths.
Post-HF methods include electron correlation:
MP2 (Møller-Plesset perturbation theory): adds pairwise correlation. Much better geometries and energies.
CCSD(T) (coupled cluster singles, doubles, perturbative triples): "gold standard" of quantum chemistry.
CI (Configuration Interaction): includes multiple electron configurations. FCI = exact within basis set.
Computational scaling: HF ~N⁴, MP2 ~N⁵, CCSD(T) ~N⁷. Very expensive for large molecules.

DENSITY FUNCTIONAL THEORY (DFT):
Kohn-Sham DFT: replace many-electron wavefunction with electron density ρ(r).
E = T[ρ] + E_ne[ρ] + E_ee[ρ] + E_xc[ρ].
E_xc: exchange-correlation functional. Exact form unknown → approximations.
LDA (Local Density Approximation): uses uniform electron gas xc.
GGA: Generalized Gradient Approximation. B88, PBE. Better than LDA.
Hybrid functionals: mix HF exchange with DFT. B3LYP (most popular), PBE0, HSE06.
Range-separated: CAM-B3LYP for excited states, charge transfer.
DFT scaling: ~N³. Much cheaper than post-HF for large molecules.
Applications: drug-protein interactions, reaction mechanisms, materials properties.

MOLECULAR MECHANICS (MM):
Classical force field: atoms as balls, bonds as springs. No quantum mechanics.
V = bonds + angles + torsions + electrostatics + van der Waals.
Force fields: AMBER (biomolecules), CHARMM (biomolecules), OPLS (liquids), UFF (any element).
Very fast: millions of atoms possible.
No bond breaking/forming (traditional MM).
Used for: protein structure, membrane simulations, crystal packing.

MOLECULAR DYNAMICS (MD):
Solve Newton's equations of motion for all atoms.
Forces from force field (MM-MD) or QM (AIMD — ab initio MD).
Time step: typically 1-2 fs. Total simulation: ns to μs for MM, ps for AIMD.
Applications: protein folding, drug binding, membrane transport, polymer properties.
Enhanced sampling methods: umbrella sampling, metadynamics, replica exchange MD → access rare events.

QM/MM (Hybrid):
Active site treated with QM (accurate). Rest of protein with MM (fast).
Used for: enzyme mechanisms, drug binding, photochemical processes in proteins.

MOLECULAR DOCKING:
Find binding pose and affinity of drug in protein active site.
Search algorithm: genetic algorithm, Monte Carlo, shape matching.
Scoring function: estimate binding free energy.
Programs: AutoDock, Glide (Schrödinger), GOLD, DOCK.
High-throughput virtual screening: screen millions of compounds → prioritize for synthesis/testing.

STRUCTURE PREDICTION:
Protein structure: AlphaFold2 (DeepMind, 2021) revolutionized field. Predicts structure from sequence with near-experimental accuracy.
Based on attention-based neural network (transformer architecture).
Predicted >200 million protein structures (essentially all known proteins)!
Before: experimental structure (X-ray, cryo-EM, NMR) or homology modeling.
Crystal structure prediction: predict crystal structure from molecular formula. Still challenging.
Materials design: high-throughput DFT screening → find new battery materials, catalysts, topological insulators.
Materials Project, AFLOW, AFLOWLIB: databases of calculated properties for thousands of materials.

MACHINE LEARNING IN CHEMISTRY:
Neural network potentials (NNPs): trained on DFT data, speed of MM with accuracy near DFT.
DeePMD, SchNet, PaiNN, MACE: graph neural network potentials.
Chemical property prediction: predict melting point, solubility, toxicity, NMR shifts from structure.
Drug discovery: generative models design new drug molecules. Reinforcement learning optimizes properties.
Reaction prediction: predict products, yields, conditions.
SMILES notation: text representation of molecular structure. C=CC=O (but-3-en-2-one).
Graph neural networks: atoms as nodes, bonds as edges.

QUANTUM COMPUTING FOR CHEMISTRY:
Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE): find ground state energy on quantum computer.
Quantum Phase Estimation: more accurate but needs more qubits/gate fidelity.
Potential: solve electronic structure exactly for systems where classical methods fail (strongly correlated).
Applications: N₂ binding (Haber process optimization), high-Tc superconductors, drug design.
Current limitation: noisy qubits (NISQ era). Future fault-tolerant QC needed for chemistry applications.
