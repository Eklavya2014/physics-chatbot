QUANTUM MECHANICS - COMPLETE GUIDE
====================================

HISTORICAL DEVELOPMENT - THE QUANTUM REVOLUTION

BLACKBODY RADIATION AND PLANCK (1900):
A blackbody absorbs all radiation and emits radiation based only on temperature.
Classical physics (Rayleigh-Jeans): u(ω) ∝ ω²kT → diverges at high frequency ("UV catastrophe")
Planck's solution: Energy is quantized. E = nhf = nℏω (n = 0,1,2,...)
Planck distribution: u(ω) = (ℏω³/π²c³) × 1/(e^(ℏω/kT) - 1)
This fit experiment perfectly and launched quantum mechanics.
Planck's constant: h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s (one of nature's fundamental constants)

PHOTOELECTRIC EFFECT AND EINSTEIN (1905):
When light shines on metal, electrons are emitted only if frequency exceeds threshold f₀.
Classical prediction: any frequency should work with enough intensity. Wrong!
Einstein's explanation: Light comes in quanta called photons. E_photon = hf
KE_max = hf - φ (φ = work function = minimum energy to release electron)
Stopping potential: eV₀ = hf - φ
Nobel Prize 1921. This established the particle nature of light.

COMPTON SCATTERING (1923):
X-rays scattered by electrons show increased wavelength.
Compton shift: Δλ = (h/m_e c)(1 - cosθ) = λ_C(1 - cosθ)
Compton wavelength: λ_C = h/(m_e c) = 2.426 × 10⁻¹² m
Confirmed photon has momentum: p = h/λ = E/c

DE BROGLIE HYPOTHESIS (1924):
If light (wave) has particle properties, then matter (particles) should have wave properties.
de Broglie wavelength: λ = h/p = h/(mv)
For electron accelerated through voltage V: λ = h/√(2meV) = 1.226/√V nm
Confirmed by Davisson-Germer experiment (1927): electrons diffracted by crystal.

FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES OF QUANTUM MECHANICS

WAVE FUNCTION (Ψ):
The quantum state of a particle is completely described by its wave function Ψ(x,t).
Physical meaning (Born interpretation): |Ψ(x,t)|² dx = probability of finding particle between x and x+dx.
Normalization: ∫|Ψ|²dx = 1 (particle must be somewhere)
Requirements: continuous, single-valued, finite, square-integrable.
Superposition principle: if Ψ₁ and Ψ₂ are solutions, so is c₁Ψ₁ + c₂Ψ₂.

SCHRÖDINGER EQUATION:
Time-dependent: iℏ ∂Ψ/∂t = ĤΨ = [-ℏ²/2m ∇² + V(x)]Ψ
Time-independent: [-ℏ²/2m d²ψ/dx² + V(x)]ψ = Eψ or Ĥψ = Eψ
This is the fundamental equation of quantum mechanics — analogous to F=ma in classical physics.

OPERATORS AND OBSERVABLES:
Every measurable quantity (observable) corresponds to a Hermitian operator.
Position: x̂ = x (multiply by x)
Momentum: p̂ = -iℏ d/dx
Energy (Hamiltonian): Ĥ = p̂²/2m + V = -ℏ²/2m d²/dx² + V
Angular momentum: L̂_z = -iℏ d/dφ

Expectation value: ⟨A⟩ = ∫Ψ* Â Ψ dx
Eigenvalue equation: Â ψ = a ψ (a = eigenvalue, ψ = eigenstate)
Measurement postulate: measuring observable A gives eigenvalue a with probability |⟨a|Ψ⟩|²

HEISENBERG UNCERTAINTY PRINCIPLE (1927):
Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ/2
ΔE · Δt ≥ ℏ/2
ΔL_x · ΔL_y ≥ ℏ/2 |⟨L_z⟩|
This is NOT due to measurement disturbance — it is a fundamental property of nature.
A particle simply does not have a definite position AND definite momentum simultaneously.
Zero-point energy: even in ground state, particle has minimum energy due to uncertainty.

Physical consequences:
- Atom stability: electron cannot spiral into nucleus (would require Δp → ∞)
- Nuclear size: Δx ~ 10⁻¹⁵ m → Δp ~ 10⁻²⁰ kg·m/s → minimum KE explains nuclear binding
- Quantum tunneling: particle can penetrate barriers forbidden classically
- Vacuum fluctuations: energy uncertainty allows virtual particle creation

EXACTLY SOLVABLE QUANTUM SYSTEMS

PARTICLE IN A BOX (Infinite Square Well):
V(x) = 0 for 0 < x < L, V = ∞ elsewhere.
Boundary conditions: ψ(0) = ψ(L) = 0
Solution: ψ_n(x) = √(2/L) sin(nπx/L)
Energy eigenvalues: E_n = n²π²ℏ²/(2mL²) = n²E₁
Ground state energy: E₁ = π²ℏ²/(2mL²) ≠ 0 (ZERO-POINT ENERGY - purely quantum!)
Key insight: Energy is QUANTIZED — only discrete values allowed.
As n increases, energy increases as n².

QUANTUM HARMONIC OSCILLATOR:
V(x) = ½mω²x²
Energy levels: E_n = (n + ½)ℏω, n = 0,1,2,3,...
Ground state energy: E₀ = ½ℏω (zero-point energy)
Energy levels are EQUALLY SPACED by ℏω.
Ground state wavefunction: ψ₀ ∝ e^(-mωx²/2ℏ) (Gaussian)
Ladder operators: â = √(mω/2ℏ)(x̂ + ip̂/mω), â†= √(mω/2ℏ)(x̂ - ip̂/mω)
â|n⟩ = √n|n-1⟩ (lowering), â†|n⟩ = √(n+1)|n+1⟩ (raising)
Applications: phonons, photons, molecular vibrations, field theory.

HYDROGEN ATOM:
Full 3D solution with Coulomb potential V = -e²/(4πε₀r)
Energy: E_n = -13.6 eV/n² = -m_e e⁴/(8ε₀²h²n²)
Quantum numbers:
n = 1,2,3,... (principal) — determines energy
l = 0,1,...,n-1 (orbital angular momentum) — determines shape (s,p,d,f)
m_l = -l,...,0,...,+l (magnetic) — determines orientation
m_s = ±½ (spin) — intrinsic angular momentum

Orbital shapes: s (l=0, spherical), p (l=1, dumbbell), d (l=2, complex), f (l=3, very complex)
Degeneracy of nth level: n² (without spin), 2n² (with spin)
Radial wavefunctions: involve Laguerre polynomials
Angular wavefunctions: spherical harmonics Y_l^m(θ,φ)

Selection rules for radiative transitions:
Δn = any, Δl = ±1, Δm_l = 0, ±1, Δm_s = 0

QUANTUM PHENOMENA

QUANTUM TUNNELING:
Particle penetrates a potential barrier even when E < V₀.
Classically completely forbidden!
Transmission probability: T ≈ e^(-2κL)
κ = √(2m(V₀-E))/ℏ, L = barrier width
Key: thinner barrier and smaller (V₀-E) → more tunneling.

Applications:
- Alpha decay: alpha particle tunnels out of nucleus (explains radioactive decay rates)
- Nuclear fusion in stars: protons tunnel through Coulomb barrier
- Scanning Tunneling Microscope (STM): images individual atoms
- Tunnel diode: fast switching electronics
- Flash memory: electrons tunnel through oxide layer
- Josephson junction: superconducting devices

QUANTUM ENTANGLEMENT:
Two particles prepared together can be in a correlated quantum state such that measuring one instantaneously determines the state of the other, regardless of distance.
Bell states (maximally entangled): |Φ+⟩ = (|↑↑⟩ + |↓↓⟩)/√2
Einstein called it "spooky action at a distance" and thought it showed QM was incomplete.
Bell's theorem (1964): any local hidden variable theory makes different predictions from QM.
Aspect experiment (1982): confirmed QM — entanglement is real.
Does NOT allow faster-than-light communication (no information is transferred).
Applications: quantum cryptography, quantum computing, quantum teleportation.

QUANTUM SUPERPOSITION:
A quantum system exists in a superposition of all possible states until measured.
Schrödinger's cat: superposition of alive and dead until observed.
Measurement causes "wavefunction collapse" to a definite state.
Double-slit experiment: single electron goes through BOTH slits simultaneously, creating interference pattern.
If you measure which slit: interference pattern disappears (measurement destroys superposition).

PAULI EXCLUSION PRINCIPLE:
No two identical fermions (half-integer spin) can occupy the same quantum state.
Explains: periodic table structure, stability of matter, white dwarfs, neutron stars.
Fermions (electrons, protons, neutrons): obey Fermi-Dirac statistics.
Bosons (photons, gluons): can share same state — Bose-Einstein condensate.

SPIN:
Intrinsic angular momentum of particles, no classical analogue.
Electron spin: s = ½, spin angular momentum S = ℏ√(s(s+1)) = ℏ√3/2
Z-component: S_z = m_s ℏ = ±ℏ/2 ("spin up" or "spin down")
Stern-Gerlach experiment (1922): confirmed quantization of angular momentum.
Pauli spin matrices: σ_x = [[0,1],[1,0]], σ_y = [[0,-i],[i,0]], σ_z = [[1,0],[0,-1]]

WAVE-PARTICLE DUALITY (DETAILED):
Every quantum object exhibits both wave and particle properties.
Which property manifests depends on what you measure.
Complementarity principle (Bohr): wave and particle descriptions are complementary — you can't see both at once.
This is not a limitation of our instruments — it is fundamental to nature.
The act of measurement inevitably disturbs the system (related to uncertainty principle).

INTERPRETATIONS OF QUANTUM MECHANICS

COPENHAGEN INTERPRETATION (Bohr, Heisenberg):
Most widely taught. Wavefunction is a mathematical tool, not physical reality. Measurement causes collapse. "Shut up and calculate."

MANY-WORLDS INTERPRETATION (Everett, 1957):
Wavefunction never collapses. Every measurement causes universe to branch. All outcomes happen in parallel universes. No collapse needed.

PILOT WAVE / DE BROGLIE-BOHM:
Particle always has definite position, guided by pilot wave. Deterministic but non-local.

QUANTUM DECOHERENCE:
Quantum systems become classical through interaction with environment. Explains why we don't see quantum effects macroscopically.

ADVANCED QUANTUM MECHANICS

PERTURBATION THEORY:
When exact solution unknown, treat H = H₀ + H' (H' small).
First-order energy: E_n^(1) = ⟨n⁰|H'|n⁰⟩
First-order state: |n^(1)⟩ = Σ_{k≠n} ⟨k⁰|H'|n⁰⟩/(E_n⁰-E_k⁰) |k⁰⟩
Applications: Stark effect (electric field), Zeeman effect (magnetic field), fine structure.

VARIATIONAL METHOD:
⟨E⟩ = ⟨ψ_trial|H|ψ_trial⟩ ≥ E_ground
Guess trial wavefunction with parameters, minimize energy. Gives upper bound on ground state.

WKB APPROXIMATION:
For slowly varying potentials. Used for tunneling calculations, quantization conditions.
Quantization: ∮p dx = (n + ½)h (for bound states)

IDENTICAL PARTICLES:
Symmetric wavefunction under exchange → bosons → Bose-Einstein statistics.
Antisymmetric wavefunction → fermions → Fermi-Dirac statistics.
Ψ_boson(1,2) = Ψ(1,2) + Ψ(2,1) (symmetric)
Ψ_fermion(1,2) = Ψ(1,2) - Ψ(2,1) (antisymmetric → zero if 1=2 → Pauli principle)

IMPORTANT CONSTANTS:

ℏ = 1.055 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s
m_e = 9.109 × 10⁻³¹ kg
e = 1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
a₀ = 0.529 Å (Bohr radius)
E_R = 13.6 eV (Rydberg energy)
λ_C = 2.426 pm (Compton wavelength)
