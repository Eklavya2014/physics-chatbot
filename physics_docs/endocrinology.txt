1. THE ENDOCRINE SYSTEM
------------------------
Endocrinology is the study of hormones — chemical messengers secreted directly
into the bloodstream to act on distant target cells. Hormones regulate metabolism,
growth, reproduction, sleep, mood, and homeostasis.

Types of Chemical Signalling:
- Endocrine   : Hormone released into blood → acts on distant target.
- Paracrine   : Acts on nearby cells; doesn't enter blood (prostaglandins, growth factors).
- Autocrine   : Cell signals to itself (cancer cells often have autocrine growth loops).
- Juxtacrine  : Signalling via direct cell-cell contact (Notch-Delta).
- Neurocrine  : Neurons release hormones into blood (ADH, oxytocin).

Hormone Chemistry:
  Peptide/Protein hormones: Water-soluble; cannot cross membrane; bind surface receptors.
    Examples: Insulin, glucagon, FSH, LH, TSH, GH, PTH, ADH, oxytocin, hCG.
  Steroid hormones: Derived from cholesterol; lipid-soluble; cross membrane; bind
    intracellular receptors → direct gene regulation.
    Examples: Cortisol, aldosterone, testosterone, oestradiol, progesterone, vitamin D.
  Amine hormones: Derived from amino acids.
    Tyrosine-derived: Thyroid hormones (T3/T4 — lipid-soluble, act like steroids);
                      catecholamines (epinephrine, norepinephrine, dopamine — water-soluble).
    Tryptophan-derived: Serotonin, melatonin.

2. DIABETES MELLITUS
---------------------
Type 1 Diabetes (T1DM):
  Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic beta cells → absolute insulin deficiency.
  Onset: Usually childhood; HLA-DR3/DR4 association; GAD65, IA-2 antibodies.
  Pathophysiology: No insulin → no glucose uptake → hyperglycaemia → lipolysis →
  ketogenesis → DKA (diabetic ketoacidosis) — life-threatening.
  Treatment: Exogenous insulin (basal-bolus); CSII (pump); closed-loop systems
  (artificial pancreas = CGM + insulin pump + algorithm).

Type 2 Diabetes (T2DM):
  Insulin resistance + progressive beta cell failure.
  Pathophysiology: Obesity → visceral adiposity → adipokine dysregulation →
  lipotoxicity + glucotoxicity → beta cell exhaustion.
  Genetics: Polygenic (>400 GWAS loci); TCF7L2 strongest single gene risk factor.
  Treatment progression:
    Lifestyle (diet + exercise) → Metformin (AMPK activation; first-line) →
    Add: SGLT2 inhibitor (empagliflozin — cardioprotective), GLP-1 RA
    (semaglutide — weight loss + CV benefit), DPP-4 inhibitor, sulfonylurea →
    Insulin.

Key Diabetes Drugs:
Drug Class         | Example          | Mechanism               | Key Benefit/Risk
-------------------|------------------|-------------------------|------------------
Metformin          | Metformin        | AMPK → hepatic glucose ↓ | Weight neutral; lactic acidosis (rare)
SGLT2i             | Empagliflozin    | Urinary glucose excretion| CV/renal protection
GLP-1 RA           | Semaglutide      | GLP-1 → insulin ↑, glucagon ↓ | Weight loss, CV benefit
DPP-4i             | Sitagliptin      | Blocks GLP-1 degradation | Weight neutral
Sulfonylurea       | Glibenclamide    | K-ATP channel closure → insulin | Hypoglycaemia risk
Insulin            | Various          | Exogenous insulin        | Universal; hypoglycaemia risk

Complications (both T1 and T2):
  Microvascular: Retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy (AGEs, polyol pathway, PKC).
  Macrovascular: CAD, stroke, peripheral arterial disease (atherogenesis).
  HbA1c: Glycated haemoglobin; reflects mean glucose over ~3 months. Target <48 mmol/mol (6.5%).

3. THYROID PHYSIOLOGY & DISORDERS
-----------------------------------
Thyroid Hormone Synthesis:
  Thyroglobulin (Tg) synthesised in thyroid follicular cells → secreted into
  follicular lumen → iodination of tyrosine residues by thyroid peroxidase (TPO)
  → MIT (monoiodotyrosine) + DIT (diiodotyrosine) → T3 (MIT+DIT) + T4 (DIT+DIT) →
  pinocytosis → lysosomal cleavage → T3/T4 secreted.
  T4 is main secretory product; converted to active T3 (5x more potent) in periphery
  by deiodinase (D1, D2). Reverse T3 (rT3) is inactive.
  Transport: 99.9% bound to TBG, albumin, transthyretin. Free T3/T4 = active.

Hypothyroidism:
  Primary (Hashimoto's thyroiditis): Autoimmune; anti-TPO, anti-Tg antibodies.
  High TSH + low T3/T4. Symptoms: fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, constipation,
  bradycardia, depression, myxoedema (severe). Treatment: Levothyroxine (T4).

Hyperthyroidism:
  Graves' disease: TSH receptor antibodies (TRAb) stimulate thyroid → autonomous
  T3/T4 production. Ophthalmopathy (proptosis), pretibial myxoedema, goitre.
  Low TSH + high T3/T4. Symptoms: weight loss, tachycardia, tremor, heat intolerance,
  anxiety, AF. Treatment: Carbimazole/PTU (TPO inhibitors), radioiodine (I-131),
  thyroidectomy.

4. ADRENAL CORTEX & STRESS RESPONSE
--------------------------------------
HPA Axis:
  CRH (hypothalamus) → ACTH (pituitary) → Cortisol (zona fasciculata).
  Cortisol effects: Gluconeogenesis ↑, lipolysis ↑, protein catabolism ↑,
  anti-inflammatory (inhibits PLA2, reduces cytokines), immunosuppressive.
  Negative feedback: Cortisol → hypothalamus and pituitary.
  Diurnal variation: Highest at ~8am; lowest at midnight.

Cushing's Syndrome (Hypercortisolism):
  Causes: Pituitary adenoma (ACTH-secreting) = Cushing's disease; adrenal adenoma;
  ectopic ACTH (SCLC); iatrogenic (exogenous steroids — most common).
  Features: Central obesity (buffalo hump, moon face), purple striae, diabetes,
  hypertension, osteoporosis, immunosuppression, thin skin, proximal myopathy.

Addison's Disease (Primary Adrenal Insufficiency):
  Autoimmune destruction of adrenal cortex → cortisol + aldosterone deficiency.
  Features: Fatigue, weight loss, hyperpigmentation (↑ ACTH/MSH), postural hypotension,
  hyponatraemia, hyperkalaemia, hypoglycaemia.
  Addisonian crisis: Life-threatening; triggered by stress/infection.
  Treatment: Hydrocortisone + fludrocortisone replacement.

5. INSULIN SIGNALLING IN DEPTH
--------------------------------
Insulin receptor: Heterotetrameric RTK (2 alpha + 2 beta subunits; disulphide-linked).
Insulin binds → conformational change → trans-autophosphorylation of beta-subunit
tyrosines → IRS1/IRS2 docking → PI3K recruitment → PIP2 → PIP3 → PDK1 + mTORC2
→ AKT (PKB) phosphorylation (T308 by PDK1; S473 by mTORC2) → AKT activated.

AKT substrates:
  FOXO transcription factors → phosphorylation → nuclear exclusion → suppresses
  gluconeogenic genes (PEPCK, G6Pase).
  GSK3 → phosphorylation → inhibition → glycogen synthase activated → glycogenesis.
  AS160 (TBC1D4) → phosphorylation → GLUT4 vesicle exocytosis (in muscle/fat).
  BAD → phosphorylation → anti-apoptotic (dissociates from Bcl-2).
  mTORC1 (via TSC1/2 inhibition) → S6K, 4EBP1 → protein synthesis.

Insulin resistance mechanisms:
  Lipotoxicity: DAG + ceramide activate PKC-theta → IRS1 serine phosphorylation
  (inhibitory) → PI3K recruitment impaired.
  ER stress: UPR activation → JNK → IRS1 serine phosphorylation.
  Inflammation: TNF-alpha + IL-6 (from adipose) → JNK/IKK → IRS1 phosphorylation.
  Mitochondrial dysfunction: Impaired beta-oxidation → lipid intermediates accumulate.
  Hyperinsulinaemia: Chronic insulin → insulin receptor downregulation.

6. NEUROENDOCRINOLOGY & CIRCADIAN BIOLOGY
-------------------------------------------
SCN (Suprachiasmatic Nucleus):
  Master circadian clock; ~10,000 neurons; driven by light input via retinohypothalamic
  tract (melanopsin-containing retinal ganglion cells).
  Molecular clock: CLOCK/BMAL1 heterodimer activates Per1/2/3 and Cry1/2 → PER/CRY
  accumulate → inhibit CLOCK/BMAL1 → ~24h oscillation.
  REV-ERB alpha/beta and ROR: Additional stabilising feedback loop.
  Output: Coordinates cortisol, melatonin, insulin, temperature, immune function rhythms.
  Disruption: Shift work → increased risk T2DM, obesity, CVD, cancer.

Leptin & Energy Balance:
  Leptin: Adipokine; levels proportional to fat mass → hypothalamus (ARC nucleus)
  → activates POMC neurons (anorexigenic; produce alpha-MSH → MC4R → reduced appetite).
  → inhibits AgRP/NPY neurons (orexigenic).
  Leptin resistance in obesity: High leptin but reduced receptor sensitivity.
  Leptin deficiency (Lepob/ob mice, Leptin-deficient humans): Severe obesity.

GLP-1 System (Key Drug Target):
  GLP-1 secreted by L cells in distal gut after meal.
  Actions: Insulin secretion (glucose-dependent), glucagon inhibition, gastric emptying
  slowed, satiety (hypothalamus), beta cell proliferation, cardioprotective.
  GLP-1 RAs: Semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) → ~15% body weight loss; CV benefit (SUSTAIN-6).
  Tirzepatide: Dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist → ~22% weight loss (SURMOUNT-1); cardiovascular.
  Mechanism of weight loss: ARC + brainstem (AP, NTS) neurons → reduced food intake.

[CLINICAL LINK] Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia (MEN):
  MEN1: Parathyroid (hyperCa2+) + pituitary adenomas + pancreatic NETs.
        MEN1 gene mutation (menin tumour suppressor).
  MEN2A: Medullary thyroid cancer + pheochromocytoma + parathyroid.
         RET proto-oncogene mutation.
  MEN2B: MTC + pheo + mucosal neuromas + Marfanoid habitus. RET M918T.
  Pheochromocytoma: Catecholamine-secreting adrenal medulla tumour → paroxysmal
  hypertension, headache, sweating, palpitations. "The 10% tumour." Diagnose with
  plasma/urine metanephrines. Treat: Alpha-blockade first → then beta-blockade → surgery.
