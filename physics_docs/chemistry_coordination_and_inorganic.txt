1. COORDINATION COMPOUNDS
--------------------------
Coordination compounds (complexes): Metal ion bonded to surrounding ligands by coordinate (dative) bonds.
Alfred Werner (Nobel 1913): Established coordination theory from CoВівЃє ammine complexes.

Key Terms:
  Central atom/ion: Metal (usually transition metal); Lewis acid (electron pair acceptor).
  Ligand: Molecule or ion donating lone pair(s) to metal; Lewis base.
  Coordination number (CN): Number of donor atoms directly bonded to central metal.
    Common CNs: 2 (linear; AgвЃє, AuвЃє), 4 (tetrahedral or square planar), 6 (octahedral; most common TM).
  Coordination sphere: Metal + ligands (shown in square brackets in formula).

2. NOMENCLATURE OF COORDINATION COMPOUNDS

IUPAC rules:
  1. Cation named before anion.
  2. In complex: Ligands named first (alphabetically, ignoring multiplying prefixes), then metal.
  3. Anionic ligands: -o suffix (chlorido, hydroxido, cyanido, oxalato).
  4. Neutral ligands: Normal name (ammine for NHв‚ѓ; aqua for Hв‚‚O; carbonyl for CO).

4. CRYSTAL FIELD THEORY (CFT)
--------------------------------
Explains colour, magnetism, and stability of transition metal complexes.

5. MOLECULAR ORBITAL THEORY FOR COMPLEXES (LIGAND FIELD THEORY)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
More rigorous than CFT. Includes covalent character of metal-ligand bonds.

Пѓ-only bonding: Ligand Пѓ-donor orbitals combine with metal d, s, p orbitals.
  Forms Пѓ bonding MOs (mostly ligand character) and Пѓ* antibonding MOs (mostly metal character).
  The "tв‚‚g" orbitals (dxy, dxz, dyz) are non-bonding in Пѓ-only picture.

  Exceptions: dвЃё square planar (16e; Rh, Ir, Pd, Pt, Ni complexes with strong-field ligands).

6. KINETICS OF SUBSTITUTION IN OCTAHEDRAL COMPLEXES
-----------------------------------------------------
Inert vs Labile complexes:
  Labile: Substitute quickly (sec to min). Cr(CO)в‚† slow; [Ni(Hв‚‚O)в‚†]ВІвЃє fast.

    Rate depends on entering ligand (increases with nucleophilicity). Rate = k[complex][entering ligand].

  Interchange (I): Concerted; no stable 5- or 7-coordinate intermediate.

  Square planar (PtВІвЃє): Almost always associative; 5-coordinate intermediate; trans effect important.
