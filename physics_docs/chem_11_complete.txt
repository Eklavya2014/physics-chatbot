CHAPTER 1: SOME BASIC CONCEPTS OF CHEMISTRY

LAWS OF CHEMICAL COMBINATION:
1. Law of Conservation of Mass: Mass of reactants = mass of products.
2. Law of Definite Proportions: A compound always has same elements in same mass ratio.
3. Law of Multiple Proportions: If two elements form multiple compounds, mass ratios are simple whole numbers.
4. Gay Lussac's Law of Gaseous Volumes: Gases combine in simple whole number ratios by volume.
5. Avogadro's Law: Equal volumes of all gases at same T and P contain equal number of molecules.

ATOMIC MASS UNIT: 1 amu = 1/12 mass of C-12 = 1.66 √ó 10‚ĀĽ¬≤‚Ā∑ kg
MOLE: Amount of substance containing 6.022 √ó 10¬≤¬≥ (Avogadro's number, N‚āź) entities.
Molar mass: mass of one mole of substance in grams.
n = m/M = V/22.4 L (at STP) = N/N‚āź

EMPIRICAL FORMULA: simplest whole number ratio of atoms.
MOLECULAR FORMULA: actual number of atoms in molecule. = n √ó empirical formula.

CONCENTRATION:
Molarity (M) = moles of solute / volume of solution in litres
Molality (m) = moles of solute / mass of solvent in kg
Mole fraction: X_A = n_A/(n_A + n_B)
Parts per million (ppm) = (mass of solute/mass of solution) √ó 10‚Ā∂

CHAPTER 3: CHEMICAL BONDING

IONIC BOND: Transfer of electrons from metal to non-metal.
Formed between elements of high electronegativity difference (>1.7).
Properties: high melting point, conducts electricity when molten/dissolved.
Example: NaCl (Na‚Āļ and Cl‚ĀĽ)
Lattice energy: energy released when ions come together to form crystal.

COVALENT BOND: Sharing of electrons between non-metals.
Single bond: one shared pair (H-H, H-Cl)
Double bond: two shared pairs (O=O, C=O)
Triple bond: three shared pairs (N‚Č°N, C‚Č°C)
Properties: low melting point, poor conductor.

COORDINATE/DATIVE BOND: Both electrons donated by one atom (e.g., NH‚āÉ ‚Üí BF‚āÉ, H‚āÉO‚Āļ).

VALENCE SHELL ELECTRON PAIR REPULSION (VSEPR) THEORY:
Electron pairs arrange to minimize repulsion.
Lone pair-lone pair repulsion > lone pair-bond pair > bond pair-bond pair.
Shapes:
- 2 bond pairs: linear (BeCl‚āā, CO‚āā, 180¬į)
- 3 bond pairs: trigonal planar (BF‚āÉ, 120¬į)
- 4 bond pairs: tetrahedral (CH‚āĄ, 109.5¬į)
- 3 bond + 1 lone: pyramidal (NH‚āÉ, 107¬į)
- 2 bond + 2 lone: bent/V-shape (H‚āāO, 104.5¬į)
- 5 bond pairs: trigonal bipyramidal (PCl‚āÖ, 90¬į and 120¬į)
- 6 bond pairs: octahedral (SF‚āÜ, 90¬į)

HYBRIDIZATION:
sp: linear, 180¬į (BeCl‚āā, C‚āāH‚āā)
sp¬≤: trigonal planar, 120¬į (BF‚āÉ, C‚āāH‚āĄ, benzene)
sp¬≥: tetrahedral, 109.5¬į (CH‚āĄ, NH‚āÉ, H‚āāO)
sp¬≥d: trigonal bipyramidal (PCl‚āÖ)
sp¬≥d¬≤: octahedral (SF‚āÜ)

MOLECULAR ORBITAL THEORY:
Atomic orbitals combine to form molecular orbitals.
Bonding MO: lower energy, electron density between nuclei.
Antibonding MO (*): higher energy, electron density away from nuclei.
Bond order = (bonding electrons - antibonding electrons)/2
Bond order 0: molecule doesn't exist. Higher bond order = stronger, shorter bond.

HYDROGEN BOND:
X-H---Y (X, Y = F, O, N ‚ÄĒ highly electronegative, small size)
Intermolecular: between different molecules (water, HF, NH‚āÉ)
Intramolecular: within same molecule (o-nitrophenol)
Explains: high boiling point of water, anomalous expansion of water.

VAN DER WAALS FORCES:
London dispersion: temporary dipoles in non-polar molecules. Weakest.
Dipole-dipole: between polar molecules.
Dipole-induced dipole: between polar and non-polar.

CHAPTER 6: CHEMICAL EQUILIBRIUM

EQUILIBRIUM: forward rate = reverse rate. Concentrations constant (not zero).
Law of Mass Action: K = [C]^c[D]^d / [A]^a[B]^b for aA + bB ‚áĆ cC + dD
Kc: concentration equilibrium constant.
Kp: pressure equilibrium constant. Kp = Kc(RT)^őĒn (őĒn = change in moles of gas)

Large K: equilibrium lies toward products.
Small K: equilibrium lies toward reactants.

LE CHATELIER'S PRINCIPLE:
If a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it shifts to oppose the disturbance.

Effect of changes:
Concentration: adding reactant ‚Üí shifts forward. Adding product ‚Üí shifts backward.
Pressure: increasing P ‚Üí shifts toward fewer moles of gas.
Temperature: increasing T ‚Üí shifts in endothermic direction.
Catalyst: no effect on K or position, only speeds up reaching equilibrium.
