1. HISTOLOGY — THE STUDY OF TISSUES
-------------------------------------
Histology is the microscopic study of tissue structure. Tissues are groups of
similar cells performing a specific function.

Tissue Preparation:
  Fixation (formalin) → Dehydration → Embedding (paraffin) → Sectioning (~5 µm)
  → Mounting on slide → Staining → Coverslip.

Common Stains:
  H&E (Haematoxylin & Eosin): Most common. Haematoxylin = blue/purple (nuclei,
  ribosomes). Eosin = pink/red (cytoplasm, collagen).
  PAS (Periodic Acid-Schiff): Magenta; carbohydrates, glycogen, basement membranes.
  Masson's Trichrome: Collagen (blue/green), muscle (red), nuclei (black).
  Alcian Blue: Acid mucopolysaccharides; goblet cells.
  Reticulin: Silver stain; reticular fibres.
  Immunohistochemistry (IHC): Antibodies detect specific proteins → cancer diagnosis.

1.1 THE FOUR TISSUE TYPES
---------------------------

EPITHELIAL TISSUE:
  Functions: Covers surfaces, lines cavities, forms glands.
  Characteristics: Cells tightly packed; rests on basement membrane;
  avascular; high regenerative capacity.

  Classification by layers:
    Simple     : One cell layer (rapid diffusion/transport).
    Stratified : Multiple layers (protection).
    Pseudostratified: One layer but nuclei at different heights (appears layered).

  Classification by cell shape:
    Squamous   : Flat, scale-like (alveoli, capillaries — diffusion).
    Cuboidal   : Cube-shaped (kidney tubules, thyroid — secretion/absorption).
    Columnar   : Taller than wide (GI tract, uterus — secretion/absorption).
    Transitional: Can stretch (urinary bladder — urothelium).

  Specialisations:
    Microvilli  : Brush border; increases surface area (small intestine, kidney PCT).
    Cilia       : Motile projections; move mucus (respiratory tract).
    Stereocilia : Long, non-motile microvilli (epididymis, inner ear hair cells).
    Goblet cells: Unicellular mucus glands (GI tract, respiratory epithelium).

CONNECTIVE TISSUE:
  Functions: Support, binding, protection, transport (blood), energy storage.
  Characteristics: Cells scattered in extracellular matrix (ECM).
  ECM components: Ground substance (GAGs, proteoglycans) + fibres (collagen,
  elastin, reticular fibres).

  Types:
    Loose (areolar): Most common; fibroblasts, collagen, elastic fibres; fills spaces.
    Dense regular   : Parallel collagen bundles; tendons, ligaments.
    Dense irregular : Random collagen orientation; dermis, organ capsules.
    Adipose         : Fat storage; insulation; cushioning. White vs brown adipose.
    Reticular       : Type III collagen network; liver, spleen, lymph nodes.
    Cartilage       : Hyaline (joints, nose, trachea), elastic (ear), fibrocartilage (discs).
    Bone            : Specialised; osteons (Haversian systems); compact and spongy.
    Blood           : Fluid connective tissue; RBCs, WBCs, platelets in plasma.

MUSCLE TISSUE:
    Skeletal: Striated; voluntary; multinucleate; peripherally placed nuclei.
    Cardiac : Striated; involuntary; intercalated discs; single central nucleus.
    Smooth  : Non-striated; involuntary; spindle-shaped; central nucleus.

NERVOUS TISSUE:
    Neurons (cells with axons/dendrites) + glia (support cells: astrocytes,
    oligodendrocytes, microglia, ependymal cells, Schwann cells).

2. ORGAN HISTOLOGY
-------------------
SKIN (Integument):
  Epidermis: Stratified squamous keratinised epithelium. Layers (deep to surface):
    Stratum basale (germinativum): Stem cells; keratinocyte production; melanocytes.
    Stratum spinosum: Desmosomes (spiny appearance); Langerhans cells.
    Stratum granulosum: Keratohyalin granules; lamellar bodies (lipid waterproofing).
    Stratum lucidum: Only in thick skin (palms, soles); densely packed.
    Stratum corneum: 20–30 layers dead, anucleate keratinocytes; keratin; barrier.
  Dermis: Dense irregular connective tissue. Papillary (loose CT + capillaries +
  nerve endings) + Reticular (dense CT; hair follicles, sweat glands, sebaceous glands).
  Hypodermis: Subcutaneous fat; not true skin layer.
  Melanocytes: Neural crest origin; produce melanin (eumelanin = black/brown;
  phaeomelanin = yellow/red); transferred to keratinocytes via melanosomes.

KIDNEY:
  Cortex: Glomeruli + PCT + DCT.
  Medulla: Loop of Henle (thin descending, thin ascending, thick ascending) +
           collecting ducts. Arranged in pyramids; papillae drain into calyces.
  Glomerulus: Tuft of fenestrated capillaries; podocytes (foot processes) form
  filtration slits; mesangial cells regulate GFR.
  PCT: Brush border (microvilli); abundant mitochondria; reabsorbs 65% of filtrate.
  Juxtaglomerular apparatus (JGA): Macula densa (dense nuclei in DCT) + JG cells
  (granular cells; produce renin) + extraglomerular mesangium. Regulates GFR via TGF.

LIVER:
  Hepatocytes: Polygonal cells; central nucleus; abundant SER (drug metabolism),
  RER (albumin, clotting factors), mitochondria, peroxisomes, glycogen granules.
  Classic lobule: Hexagonal; central vein at centre; portal triads at corners
  (portal vein + hepatic artery + bile duct).
  Portal lobule: Bile duct at centre; organises by bile flow direction.
  Liver acinus (Rappaport): Zone 1 (periportal; highest O2/nutrients),
  Zone 2 (mid), Zone 3 (pericentral; lowest O2 → most vulnerable to ischaemia).
  Kupffer cells: Resident macrophages in sinusoids; phagocytose bacteria, debris.
  Stellate cells (Ito cells): Vitamin A storage; activated by injury → myofibroblasts
  → collagen deposition → fibrosis → cirrhosis.

GI TRACT — GENERAL LAYERS (Deepest to Lumen):
  Serosa/Adventitia → Muscularis externa (longitudinal + circular smooth muscle;
  Auerbach's plexus between layers) → Submucosa (Meissner's plexus; glands) →
  Muscularis mucosa → Lamina propria → Epithelium (faces lumen).

  Small Intestine specialisations:
    Villi: Finger-like projections of mucosa; increase SA ~10x.
    Crypts of Lieberkühn: Invaginations; contain Paneth cells (antimicrobial peptides),
    stem cells (LGR5+), enteroendocrine cells.
    Microvilli (brush border): ~20 per enterocyte; additional ~20x SA increase.
    Peyer's patches: Lymphoid tissue in ileum (M cells).
    Duodenum: Brunner's glands in submucosa (secrete bicarbonate — protects from acid).

3. BLOOD CELLS — HAEMATOPOIESIS
---------------------------------
All blood cells derive from haematopoietic stem cells (HSCs) in bone marrow.

Cell Type         | Normal Count    | Function                    | Lifespan
------------------|-----------------|-----------------------------|-----------
RBC (erythrocyte) | 4.5–5.5 M/µL   | O2 transport (haemoglobin)  | 120 days
Neutrophil        | 1.8–7.7 K/µL   | First phagocyte; pus         | 6–12h blood
Eosinophil        | 0.1–0.4 K/µL   | Parasites; allergies          | Days-weeks
Basophil          | 0.02–0.1 K/µL  | Inflammation; heparin; IgE   | Days
Monocyte          | 0.2–0.95 K/µL  | → Macrophage in tissues      | Hours→months
Lymphocyte        | 1–4 K/µL       | B cells, T cells, NK cells   | Days–decades
Platelets         | 150–400 K/µL   | Clotting; haemostasis         | 8–12 days

Haematopoiesis:
  HSC → CMP (common myeloid progenitor) or CLP (common lymphoid progenitor)
  CMP → Megakaryocyte/Erythroid progenitor OR Granulocyte/Monocyte progenitor
  CLP → B cell or T cell or NK cell precursors.
  Key transcription factors:
    GATA-1: Erythroid and megakaryocyte fate.
    PU.1: Myeloid and lymphoid fate (high → myeloid; low → lymphoid).
    IKAROS: Lymphoid development; master regulator.
    C/EBPalpha: Neutrophil/granulocyte differentiation.

4. EXTRACELLULAR MATRIX (ECM)
------------------------------
ECM is a complex, dynamic scaffold that regulates cell behaviour, tissue
architecture, and signalling. Far more than passive "filler."

Collagen:
  Most abundant protein in mammals (~30% body protein). 28 types.
  Type I   : Bone, tendon, skin (90% of all collagen).
  Type II  : Cartilage.
  Type III : Reticular fibres; co-distributes with type I; vessels, skin.
  Type IV  : Basement membranes (sheet-forming; non-fibrillar).
  Biosynthesis: Procollagen triple helix (in ER) → secreted → propeptide cleavage
  → fibril assembly → cross-linking by lysyl oxidase (Cu-dependent).

Glycosaminoglycans (GAGs):
  Long unbranched polysaccharide chains; highly negatively charged → attract water
  → compressive resistance (joint cartilage). Examples: hyaluronate, heparan sulphate,
  chondroitin sulphate, dermatan sulphate, keratan sulphate.
  Proteoglycans: GAGs covalently attached to core proteins (aggrecan, syndecan, perlecan).

Laminin & Fibronectin:
  Laminin: Major component of basement membranes; critical for epithelial attachment.
  Fibronectin: RGD (Arg-Gly-Asp) motif binds integrins; promotes cell adhesion,
  migration; important in wound healing.

Integrins:
  Heterodimeric cell surface receptors (alpha + beta subunits); connect ECM to
  cytoskeleton (actin via talin, vinculin, paxillin → focal adhesions).
  Outside-in signalling: ECM stiffness → integrin clustering → FAK + Src activation
  → proliferation, survival, migration.
  Inside-out signalling: Internal signals (kindlin, talin) activate integrins.
  Mechanotransduction: Cell sense matrix stiffness; stiff matrix → YAP/TAZ nuclear
  localisation → gene expression → differentiation into osteoblasts/myofibroblasts.

5. STEM CELL NICHES
--------------------
A niche is the microenvironment that maintains stem cell identity and regulates
self-renewal vs differentiation.

Intestinal crypt niche:
  LGR5+ stem cells at crypt base. Paneth cells: Produce Wnt3a, EGF, Notch ligands
  (DLL1/4) → maintain LGR5+ stem cells. R-spondin (RSPO): Extends Wnt gradient.
  Crypt-villus axis: Wnt decreases up the villus → differentiation.

Haematopoietic niche:
  Endosteal niche (near bone surface): Osteoblasts; quiescent HSC maintenance.
  Vascular niche (sinusoids): Endothelial cells + CXCL12-abundant reticular (CAR)
  cells; HSC activation and mobilisation.
  CXCL12/CXCR4 axis: Anchors HSCs in bone marrow. G-CSF → mobilises HSCs to blood.

Neural stem cell niche:
  Two adult neurogenic niches: Subventricular zone (SVZ) of lateral ventricle
  (olfactory bulb pathway) and Subgranular zone (SGZ) of hippocampal dentate gyrus.
  GFAP+ radial glia-like stem cells → intermediate progenitors → newborn neurons.
  Hippocampal neurogenesis: Role in pattern separation, memory; reduced by stress,
  ageing; enhanced by exercise, enriched environment, SSRIs.

[DISEASE LINK] Fibrosis:
  Chronic injury → persistent fibroblast/myofibroblast activation → excessive
  collagen deposition → organ fibrosis. TGF-beta is master profibrotic cytokine.
  Liver cirrhosis: Activated stellate cells → fibrosis → portal hypertension.
  Pulmonary fibrosis: IPF; pirfenidone + nintedanib slow progression.
  Renal fibrosis: End-stage in all chronic kidney diseases. No approved antifibrotic.
