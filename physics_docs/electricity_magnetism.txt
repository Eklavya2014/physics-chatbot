PHYSICS: ELECTRICITY & MAGNETISM — COMPLETE GUIDE
===================================================

ELECTROSTATICS
--------------
Coulomb's law: F = kq₁q₂/r²  (k = 1/4πε₀ = 9×10⁹ N·m²/C²)
ε₀ = 8.85×10⁻¹² C²/N·m²  (permittivity of free space)
Electric field: E = F/q₀ = kQ/r²  (N/C or V/m)
  Direction: away from +charge, toward -charge
Superposition: E_total = ΣE (vector sum)
Electric field lines: Start on +, end on -, denser = stronger field

Electric dipole: p = q·d  (from -ve to +ve, C·m)
  Field at axial point: E = 2kp/r³
  Field at equatorial point: E = kp/r³

Gauss's law: ∮E·dA = Q_enclosed/ε₀
  Useful for spherical, cylindrical, planar symmetry
  Spherical shell: E = 0 inside, E = kQ/r² outside
  Infinite plane: E = σ/2ε₀  (σ = surface charge density)
  Capacitor plates: E = σ/ε₀

Electric potential: V = kQ/r  (scalar, Joules/Coulomb = Volt)
  V = -∫E·dr  (work done per unit charge against field)
  E = -dV/dr = -∇V
  Potential at infinity = 0
  Inside conductor: E=0, V=constant
  Potential of dipole: V = kp·cos θ/r²

Potential energy: U = kq₁q₂/r = q·V
Work done: W = q(V_A - V_B) = q·V_AB

CAPACITANCE
-----------
Capacitance: C = Q/V  (Farads, F)
Parallel plate: C = ε₀A/d  (with dielectric: C = κε₀A/d)
Spherical: C = 4πε₀r
Energy stored: U = Q²/2C = CV²/2 = QV/2
Series: 1/C = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + 1/C₃
Parallel: C = C₁ + C₂ + C₃
Dielectric: κ (dielectric constant) > 1, reduces E field, increases C

CURRENT ELECTRICITY
--------------------
Current: I = Q/t = nqv_d·A  (n=charge density, v_d=drift velocity)
Ohm's law: V = IR  (R in Ohms Ω)
Resistance: R = ρL/A  (ρ=resistivity)
  Temperature: ρ = ρ₀(1 + αΔT)
  Resistivity: conductor < semiconductor < insulator
  Copper: ρ ≈ 1.7×10⁻⁸ Ω·m

Series: R = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ (same current, voltages add)
Parallel: 1/R = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃ (same voltage, currents add)

Kirchhoff's laws:
  KCL (Junction rule): ΣI_in = ΣI_out (charge conservation)
  KVL (Loop rule): ΣV = 0 around closed loop (energy conservation)

EMF and internal resistance: V = ε - Ir  (terminal voltage)
  V = ε when I=0 (open circuit)
  Short circuit: I_max = ε/r

Power: P = IV = I²R = V²/R  (Watts)
Electrical energy: E = Pt = VIt  (Joules; kWh = 3.6×10⁶ J)

WHEATSTONE BRIDGE (balanced): P/Q = R/S  → R_unknown = S·P/Q

ELECTROCHEMISTRY:
  Electrolysis: Faraday's 1st law: m = ZIt = (M/nF)·It
  Faraday constant: F = 96500 C/mol
  Faraday's 2nd law: m₁/m₂ = E₁/E₂  (E=equivalent weight)

MAGNETISM
---------
Magnetic force on charge: F = qv × B  (Lorentz force)
  Magnitude: F = qvB·sin θ
  Direction: Right-hand rule (v × B)
Magnetic force on current: F = IL × B = BIL·sin θ
Magnetic field of straight wire: B = μ₀I/(2πr)
Magnetic field at centre of loop: B = μ₀I/(2R)
Magnetic field in solenoid: B = μ₀nI  (n = turns/length)
Magnetic field of toroid: B = μ₀nI

μ₀ = 4π×10⁻⁷ T·m/A  (permeability of free space)

Magnetic dipole moment: m = IA  (A = area of loop)
Torque: τ = m × B = mB·sin θ
Potential energy: U = -m·B = -mB·cos θ

Biot-Savart law: dB = (μ₀/4π)·I(dl × r̂)/r²
Ampere's law: ∮B·dl = μ₀I_enclosed

MOTION IN MAGNETIC FIELD:
  Circular motion: r = mv/(qB)  →  v = qBr/m
  Cyclotron frequency: ω = qB/m  (independent of speed!)
  Cyclotron: accelerates charged particles in spiral path

ELECTROMAGNETIC INDUCTION
--------------------------
Faraday's law: ε = -dΦ/dt  (ε = EMF, Φ = magnetic flux = B·A·cos θ)
Lenz's law: Induced current opposes the change in flux
Motional EMF: ε = BLv  (conductor moving in field)

Self-inductance: L = NΦ/I  (Henry, H)
  Solenoid: L = μ₀N²A/l = μ₀n²Al
  Energy stored: U = ½LI²
  Back EMF: ε = -L·dI/dt

Mutual inductance: M = N₂Φ₁₂/I₁
  Transformer: V₂/V₁ = N₂/N₁ = I₁/I₂ (ideal)
    Step-up: N₂>N₁, V₂>V₁; Step-down: N₂<N₁, V₂<V₁

ALTERNATING CURRENT (AC)
-------------------------
v = V₀·sin(ωt),  i = I₀·sin(ωt + φ)
RMS values: V_rms = V₀/√2,  I_rms = I₀/√2
  Mains supply: 220V, 50Hz (India/UK); 120V, 60Hz (USA)

Reactances:
  Inductive: X_L = ωL = 2πfL  (increases with frequency)
  Capacitive: X_C = 1/(ωC) = 1/(2πfC)  (decreases with frequency)
Impedance: Z = √(R² + (X_L - X_C)²)
Phase angle: tan φ = (X_L - X_C)/R
Power factor: cos φ = R/Z
Average power: P = I_rms·V_rms·cos φ = I_rms²·R

Series LCR resonance:
  X_L = X_C  →  ω₀ = 1/√(LC)  →  f₀ = 1/(2π√(LC))
  At resonance: Z = R (minimum), I = maximum
  Quality factor: Q = ω₀L/R = 1/(ω₀CR) = √(L/C)/R

Parallel LCR: Resonance at same ω₀, high impedance at resonance

ELECTROMAGNETIC WAVES
---------------------
Maxwell's equations predict electromagnetic waves
  Wave speed: c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) = 3×10⁸ m/s
  E and B are perpendicular, in phase, perpendicular to propagation
  E₀/B₀ = c
Intensity: I = c·ε₀E₀²/2 = E₀B₀/(2μ₀)
Radiation pressure: P = I/c (absorbed), P = 2I/c (reflected)
Poynting vector: S = (1/μ₀)E × B  (energy flow per unit area)

MAGNETIC PROPERTIES OF MATTER
--------------------------------
Diamagnetic: χ < 0, weakly repelled (bismuth, water, copper)
Paramagnetic: χ > 0 (small), weakly attracted (aluminium, oxygen)
Ferromagnetic: χ >> 0, strongly attracted (iron, nickel, cobalt)
  Domains, hysteresis loop, Curie temperature
Permeability: μ = μ₀μᵣ  (μᵣ = relative permeability)
Hysteresis: Retentivity = B when H=0; Coercivity = H to demagnetise
Hard magnetic: high coercivity (permanent magnets)
Soft magnetic: low coercivity (transformer cores)
