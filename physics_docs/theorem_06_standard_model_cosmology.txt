MODERN PHYSICS - STANDARD MODEL, COSMOLOGY AND BEYOND
=======================================================

THE STANDARD MODEL OF PARTICLE PHYSICS

The Standard Model (developed 1970s) is the most successful scientific theory ever — it has made predictions accurate to 1 part in 10 billion.

FUNDAMENTAL PARTICLES:

QUARKS (6 flavors, all have spin ½):
Up (u): charge +2/3, mass ~2.3 MeV/c²
Down (d): charge -1/3, mass ~4.8 MeV/c²
Strange (s): charge -1/3, mass ~95 MeV/c²
Charm (c): charge +2/3, mass ~1.28 GeV/c²
Bottom (b): charge -1/3, mass ~4.18 GeV/c²
Top (t): charge +2/3, mass ~173 GeV/c² (heaviest known particle!)
Each quark has 3 "color charges" (red, green, blue) — strong force charges.
Quarks are confined — never observed in isolation. Confinement.
Anti-quarks have opposite charge and anti-color.

LEPTONS (6 types, all spin ½):
Electron (e⁻): charge -1, mass 0.511 MeV/c², stable
Muon (μ⁻): charge -1, mass 105.7 MeV/c², decays in 2.2 μs
Tau (τ⁻): charge -1, mass 1777 MeV/c², very short-lived
Electron neutrino (νe): charge 0, very small mass, stable
Muon neutrino (νμ): charge 0, very small mass
Tau neutrino (ντ): charge 0, very small mass
Neutrinos: interact only via weak force and gravity. Pass through Earth almost undisturbed.
Neutrino oscillation: neutrinos change flavor as they travel (Nobel Prize 2015).

GAUGE BOSONS (force carriers, spin 1):
Photon (γ): mediates electromagnetic force. Massless. Range = ∞.
W⁺, W⁻, Z⁰: mediate weak force. Mass ~80-91 GeV/c². Range ~10⁻¹⁸ m.
Gluons (g): mediate strong force. Massless but carry color → self-interact.
Graviton: hypothetical, mediates gravity. Spin 2. Not yet in Standard Model.

HIGGS BOSON (spin 0):
Discovered at CERN (LHC) on July 4, 2012.
Mass = 125.09 GeV/c²
Gives mass to W, Z bosons and fermions through Higgs mechanism.
Higgs field permeates all of space. Particles acquire mass by interacting with this field.
Nobel Prize 2013 (Higgs, Englert).

FUNDAMENTAL FORCES (from strongest to weakest):
Strong nuclear: holds quarks in nucleons, nucleons in nucleus.
Range ~10⁻¹⁵ m. Relative strength: 1.
Electromagnetic: between charged particles.
Range = ∞. Relative strength: 1/137 (fine structure constant α).
Weak nuclear: radioactive beta decay, neutrino interactions.
Range ~10⁻¹⁸ m. Relative strength: 10⁻⁶.
Gravity: between all massive objects.
Range = ∞. Relative strength: 10⁻³⁸ (incredibly weak!).

ELECTROWEAK UNIFICATION:
At energies > 100 GeV, EM and weak forces merge into electroweak force.
Glashow-Weinberg-Salam theory (Nobel Prize 1979).
At low energies, Higgs mechanism breaks symmetry → W, Z become massive, photon stays massless.

QUANTUM CHROMODYNAMICS (QCD):
Theory of strong force. Quarks carry "color charge" (red, green, blue).
Gluons carry color — they interact with each other (unlike photons).
Asymptotic freedom: at very high energies, quarks behave almost freely (Nobel Prize 2004).
Color confinement: only color-neutral (white) combinations observable.
Hadrons: colorless combinations of quarks.
Baryons (qqq): proton (uud), neutron (udd), Λ, Σ, Ξ, Ω baryons.
Mesons (qq̄): π (pions), K (kaons), J/ψ, Υ mesons.

COSMOLOGY AND THE ORIGIN OF THE UNIVERSE

THE BIG BANG THEORY:
The universe began ~13.8 billion years ago from an extremely hot, dense state.
Evidence:
1. Hubble's law (1929): galaxies receding, universe expanding.
2. Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB, 1965): thermal radiation at 2.725 K.
3. Primordial nucleosynthesis: predicted H:He ratio 3:1 matches observation.
4. Large-scale structure: galaxy distribution matches predictions.

TIMELINE OF THE UNIVERSE:
t = 0: Big Bang singularity (GR breaks down, need quantum gravity)
t = 10⁻⁴³ s: Planck time — all four forces unified (quantum gravity era)
t = 10⁻³⁵ s: Inflation begins — exponential expansion by factor ~10⁶⁰
t = 10⁻³² s: Inflation ends. Universe is flat and homogeneous.
t = 10⁻¹² s: Electroweak transition — EM and weak forces separate.
t = 10⁻⁶ s: QCD transition — quarks combine into hadrons (confinement).
t = 1 s: Neutrino decoupling. n/p ratio freezes.
t = 3 min: Big Bang nucleosynthesis — H, He, Li formed.
t = 380,000 yr: Recombination — electrons combine with protons → neutral atoms. CMB emitted.
t = 200 million yr: First stars form.
t = 1 billion yr: First galaxies form.
t = 9.2 billion yr: Solar System forms.
t = 13.8 billion yr: Today.

COSMIC INFLATION (Alan Guth, 1980):
Universe expanded exponentially from ~10⁻³⁵ to ~10⁻³² seconds.
Scale factor increased by factor of 10⁶⁰ or more.
Explains:
- Flatness problem: why is universe so flat (k ≈ 0)?
- Horizon problem: why is CMB so uniform even for regions never in causal contact?
- Magnetic monopole problem: why haven't we detected monopoles?
Inflation stretched all quantum fluctuations to macroscopic scales → seeds of structure.

COSMIC MICROWAVE BACKGROUND (CMB):
Perfect blackbody spectrum at T = 2.725 K.
Tiny temperature fluctuations ΔT/T ~ 10⁻⁵ (observed by WMAP, Planck satellites).
These fluctuations are seeds that grew into galaxies and clusters.
Angular power spectrum of CMB gives precise values of cosmological parameters.
CMB polarization: B-mode polarization would be signature of gravitational waves from inflation.

CONTENTS OF THE UNIVERSE:
Ordinary (baryonic) matter: 4.9%
Dark matter: 26.8%
Dark energy: 68.3%
Total adds to 100% — universe is flat.

DARK MATTER:
Evidence: galaxy rotation curves (velocities don't drop off at large radii).
Gravitational lensing: more bending than visible matter can produce.
Galaxy clusters: hot gas held in cluster requires more mass.
CMB power spectrum: requires dark matter for structure formation.
What it is: unknown. Candidates: WIMPs (weakly interacting massive particles), axions, sterile neutrinos.
What it isn't: not black holes, not ordinary matter (BBN constraints).

DARK ENERGY:
Discovered 1998: supernovae observations showed universe expansion is ACCELERATING.
Nobel Prize 2011 (Perlmutter, Schmidt, Riess).
Acts as repulsive energy with negative pressure: w = P/ρ = -1 (cosmological constant).
Einstein's cosmological constant Λ: vacuum energy density.
Problem: theoretical prediction of vacuum energy is 10¹²⁰ times larger than observed — the worst prediction in physics!

HUBBLE'S LAW:
v = H₀d (recession velocity = Hubble constant × distance)
H₀ = 67-73 km/s/Mpc (current tension between measurements)
Hubble time: t_H = 1/H₀ ≈ 14 billion years (approximate age of universe)
Observable universe radius: ~46 billion light-years (larger than 13.8 billion light-years due to expansion)

FATE OF THE UNIVERSE:
With dark energy (Λ > 0): universe expands forever, accelerating.
Far future: stars burn out, black holes evaporate (Hawking radiation), cold dark universe.
"Big Freeze" or "Heat Death": maximum entropy, minimum usable energy.

BEYOND THE STANDARD MODEL

PROBLEMS WITH THE STANDARD MODEL:
1. Gravity not included (no quantum theory of gravity).
2. Dark matter not explained.
3. Dark energy not explained.
4. Matter-antimatter asymmetry: why more matter than antimatter?
5. Hierarchy problem: why is Higgs mass so much lighter than Planck mass?
6. Three generations: why three families of quarks and leptons?
7. Neutrino masses: not in original SM, added ad hoc.
8. Fine-tuning: many parameters seem to be precisely tuned.

SUPERSYMMETRY (SUSY):
Every particle has a "superpartner" with spin differing by ½.
Quarks → squarks, electrons → selectrons, photons → photinos, etc.
Solves hierarchy problem: quadratic divergences cancel between particles and superpartners.
Lightest supersymmetric particle (LSP) is a dark matter candidate.
Not yet confirmed at LHC (as of 2024).

STRING THEORY:
Replace point particles with tiny 1D strings (~10⁻³⁵ m, Planck length).
Different vibration modes → different particles.
Naturally includes gravity (graviton = closed string loop).
Requires extra dimensions: superstring theory requires 10 dimensions (6 compactified).
M-theory: 11 dimensions, unifies 5 different string theories.
No experimental predictions yet testable (too high energy).

LOOP QUANTUM GRAVITY (LQG):
Quantize spacetime itself (not particles on spacetime).
Space has discrete structure at Planck scale (10⁻³⁵ m).
No extra dimensions needed. Background independent.
Spin networks and spin foams describe quantum spacetime.
Does not unify gravity with other forces yet.

GRAND UNIFIED THEORIES (GUTs):
At ~10¹⁵ GeV, strong and electroweak forces unify.
Predict proton decay: p → e⁺ + π⁰ (not yet observed, lifetime > 10³⁴ years).
Explain charge quantization.
May explain matter-antimatter asymmetry (baryogenesis).

PLANCK UNITS (Natural units):
Planck length: l_P = √(ℏG/c³) = 1.616 × 10⁻³⁵ m
Planck time: t_P = √(ℏG/c⁵) = 5.391 × 10⁻⁴⁴ s
Planck mass: m_P = √(ℏc/G) = 2.176 × 10⁻⁸ kg = 1.22 × 10¹⁹ GeV/c²
Planck energy: E_P = m_P c² = 1.956 × 10⁹ J
At Planck scale, quantum gravity effects become important.

IMPORTANT NUMBERS IN COSMOLOGY:
Age of universe: 13.787 billion years
Size of observable universe: ~46 billion light-years radius
Number of atoms in observable universe: ~10⁸⁰
Hubble constant H₀: 67.4 km/s/Mpc (Planck 2018)
CMB temperature: 2.72548 K
Baryon-to-photon ratio: η ≈ 6 × 10⁻¹⁰
Dark energy density: ρ_Λ = 5.9 × 10⁻²⁷ kg/m³
