COLLEGE PHYSICS - CONDENSED MATTER AND FLUID MECHANICS
=======================================================

CHAPTER: CONDENSED MATTER PHYSICS

CRYSTAL STRUCTURES:
- Unit cell: smallest repeating unit of crystal.
- Bravais lattices: 14 types in 3D.
- Common structures: Simple cubic (SC), Face-centered cubic (FCC, like Cu, Al), Body-centered cubic (BCC, like Fe, W), Hexagonal close-packed (HCP).
- Coordination number: number of nearest neighbors. SC=6, BCC=8, FCC=12.
- Packing fraction: SC=52%, BCC=68%, FCC=74% (most efficient).

X-RAY DIFFRACTION:
Bragg's law: 2d sin θ = nλ
d = interplanar spacing, θ = glancing angle, λ = X-ray wavelength.
Used to determine crystal structure.
Miller indices (hkl) specify crystal planes.

BAND THEORY OF SOLIDS:
- Nearly free electron model: nearly free electrons modified by periodic potential.
- Tight binding model: atomic orbitals overlap to form bands.
- Band structure: E vs k diagram.
- Effective mass: m* = ℏ²/(d²E/dk²) (can be negative near band edge)
- Holes: empty states in valence band, behave as positive charge carriers.

METALS:
- Partially filled conduction band.
- Fermi sphere in k-space.
- Electrical conductivity: σ = ne²τ/m = ne μ (μ = mobility)
- Drude model: classical, τ = mean free time.
- Bloch theorem: ψ_k(r) = u_k(r)e^(ik·r) (periodic × plane wave)

SEMICONDUCTORS (detailed):
- Intrinsic carrier density: n_i = √(N_c N_v) e^(-E_g/2kT)
- Minority/majority carriers: depend on doping.
- p-n junction built-in potential: V₀ = (kT/e) ln(N_A N_D/n_i²)
- MOSFET: voltage-controlled transistor. Basis of all modern electronics.
- LED: forward-biased p-n junction emits light when electrons recombine with holes.
- Solar cell: reverse of LED; photon creates electron-hole pair.

SUPERCONDUCTIVITY:
- Zero electrical resistance below T_c (critical temperature).
- Meissner effect: perfect diamagnetism (expels all B field).
- BCS theory: Cooper pairs of electrons (mediated by phonons) in singlet state.
- Cooper pair binding energy ~ 1 meV.
- Type I: abrupt transition (Pb, Sn, Hg).
- Type II: mixed state with vortices (Nb, high-T_c cuprates).
- High-T_c superconductors: discovered 1986, T_c up to 135 K.
- Applications: MRI magnets, maglev trains, particle accelerators.

QUANTUM HALL EFFECT:
In 2D electron gas at strong B field and low T.
Integer QHE: Hall conductance quantized in integer multiples of e²/h.
Fractional QHE: fractional multiples due to electron correlations (quasiparticles with fractional charge).

FERROMAGNETISM:
Exchange interaction: parallel spin alignment energetically favorable.
Domains: regions of uniform magnetization.
Curie temperature: T_c above which ferromagnetism vanishes.
Mean field theory: M = N μ tanh(μB_eff/kT)
Spin waves (magnons): collective excitations of spin system.

PHONONS:
Quanta of lattice vibrations.
Acoustic phonons: atoms move in phase (can be longitudinal or transverse).
Optical phonons: adjacent atoms move out of phase (in ionic crystals).
Phonon dispersion: ω vs k.
Heat conduction by phonons dominant in insulators.

CHAPTER: FLUID MECHANICS

FLUID STATICS:
Pressure at depth: P = P₀ + ρgh
Pascal's principle: pressure applied to enclosed fluid is transmitted equally.
Archimedes' principle: buoyant force = weight of displaced fluid. F_b = ρ_fluid V_submerged g.

FLUID DYNAMICS:

Continuity equation (mass conservation):
A₁v₁ = A₂v₂ (for incompressible fluid)
∂ρ/∂t + ∇·(ρv⃗) = 0 (general form)

Bernoulli's equation (energy conservation for ideal fluid):
P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant (along streamline)
Applications: airplane wings, venturimeter, Pitot tube.

Torricelli's theorem: v = √(2gh) (speed of efflux)

VISCOSITY:
- Viscous force: F = ηA dv/dy (η = dynamic viscosity, Pa·s)
- Kinematic viscosity: ν = η/ρ (m²/s)
- Stokes' law: F_drag = 6πηrv (for sphere at low Re)
- Terminal velocity: v_t = 2r²(ρ-ρ_f)g/(9η)

REYNOLDS NUMBER:
Re = ρvL/η = vL/ν (L = characteristic length)
- Re < 2000: laminar (smooth, parallel layers).
- Re > 4000: turbulent (chaotic, eddies).
- 2000-4000: transition region.

NAVIER-STOKES EQUATIONS:
ρ(∂v⃗/∂t + v⃗·∇v⃗) = -∇P + η∇²v⃗ + ρg⃗
General equations of viscous fluid motion.
Extremely difficult to solve. One of the Millennium Prize Problems (existence and smoothness).

SURFACE TENSION (γ):
Force per unit length at liquid surface.
Excess pressure inside bubble: ΔP = 4γ/r (soap bubble, 2 surfaces)
Excess pressure inside droplet: ΔP = 2γ/r (1 surface)
Capillary rise: h = 2γ cos θ/(ρgr) (θ = contact angle)

DIMENSIONAL ANALYSIS IN FLUIDS:
Key dimensionless groups:
- Reynolds number (Re = ρvL/η): inertia/viscosity.
- Mach number (Ma = v/c): flow speed/sound speed.
- Froude number (Fr = v/√(gL)): inertia/gravity.
- Strouhal number: oscillation frequency × L/v.

SOUND WAVES IN FLUIDS:
Speed of sound: v = √(B/ρ) (B = bulk modulus)
For ideal gas: v = √(γRT/M) = √(γP/ρ)
At 20°C in air: v ≈ 343 m/s.
Mach cone angle: sin θ = c/v = 1/Ma (for Ma > 1).

TURBULENCE:
- Energy cascade: energy injected at large scales, cascades to smaller scales, dissipated at smallest scales (Kolmogorov microscale).
- Kolmogorov length: η_K = (ν³/ε)^(1/4) (ε = energy dissipation rate)
- Self-similar structure (fractals).
- Not fully understood — one of greatest unsolved problems in physics.

IMPORTANT FORMULAS:
- P = P₀ + ρgh (hydrostatic pressure)
- F_b = ρgV (buoyancy)
- P + ½ρv² + ρgh = const (Bernoulli)
- F = ηA dv/dy (viscous force)
- Re = ρvL/η (Reynolds number)
- v_t = 2r²Δρg/(9η) (terminal velocity)
- ΔP = 4γ/r (soap bubble)
- h = 2γ cos θ/(ρgr) (capillary rise)
- v_sound = √(γRT/M) (ideal gas)
