COLLEGE PHYSICS - ELECTRODYNAMICS AND STATISTICAL MECHANICS
============================================================

CHAPTER: CLASSICAL ELECTRODYNAMICS (Maxwell's Equations)

MAXWELL'S EQUATIONS IN FREE SPACE:
∇·E⃗ = ρ/ε₀                    (Gauss's law for E)
∇·B⃗ = 0                        (No magnetic monopoles)
∇×E⃗ = -∂B⃗/∂t                  (Faraday's law)
∇×B⃗ = μ₀J⃗ + μ₀ε₀∂E⃗/∂t       (Ampere-Maxwell law)

IN MATTER:
∇·D⃗ = ρ_f                     (D = ε₀E⃗ + P⃗ = εE⃗)
∇·B⃗ = 0
∇×E⃗ = -∂B⃗/∂t
∇×H⃗ = J⃗_f + ∂D⃗/∂t           (H = B⃗/μ₀ - M⃗ = B⃗/μ)

ELECTROMAGNETIC WAVE EQUATION:
From Maxwell's equations in vacuum:
∇²E⃗ = μ₀ε₀ ∂²E⃗/∂t²
Speed: c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) = 3×10⁸ m/s
In medium: v = 1/√(με) = c/n

ENERGY AND MOMENTUM IN EM FIELDS:
Energy density: u = ½(ε₀E² + B²/μ₀)
Poynting vector: S⃗ = (1/μ₀) E⃗ × B⃗ (energy flux, W/m²)
Radiation pressure: P = S/c (for absorption), P = 2S/c (for reflection)
EM momentum density: g⃗ = S⃗/c² = ε₀(E⃗×B⃗)

POTENTIAL FORMULATION:
B⃗ = ∇×A⃗ (magnetic vector potential)
E⃗ = -∇φ - ∂A⃗/∂t (φ = scalar potential)
Gauge freedom: can add ∇λ to A, -∂λ/∂t to φ.
Lorenz gauge: ∇·A⃗ + μ₀ε₀∂φ/∂t = 0

RETARDED POTENTIALS:
φ(r,t) = (1/4πε₀) ∫ ρ(r',t_r)/|r-r'| d³r'
t_r = t - |r-r'|/c (retarded time)
Accounts for finite propagation speed of EM fields.

RADIATION FROM ACCELERATING CHARGES:
Larmor formula: P = q²a²/(6πε₀c³) = μ₀q²a²/(6πc)
Radiated power proportional to q² and a².
Applications: synchrotron radiation, bremsstrahlung, antenna theory.

MULTIPOLE EXPANSION:
Far-field radiation:
- Electric dipole: P ∝ ω⁴|p|² (p = dipole moment)
- Magnetic dipole: P ∝ ω⁴|m|²/c²
- Leading order usually electric dipole.

SKIN EFFECT:
In good conductor, EM fields penetrate only to depth:
δ = √(2/μσω) (skin depth)
At high frequencies, current flows only near surface.

CHAPTER: STATISTICAL MECHANICS

FOUNDATIONS:
Goal: derive thermodynamic properties from microscopic behavior.

Microstates vs Macrostates:
- Microstate: complete specification of all particle positions and momenta.
- Macrostate: macroscopic description (P, V, T, N).
- Many microstates correspond to one macrostate.
- Probability ∝ number of microstates (Ω).

BOLTZMANN ENTROPY:
S = k_B ln Ω
k_B = 1.38 × 10⁻²³ J/K (Boltzmann constant)
Entropy is a measure of disorder/multiplicity.

CANONICAL ENSEMBLE:
System at fixed T, V, N in contact with heat reservoir.
Boltzmann factor: P_i = e^(-E_i/kT)/Z
Partition function: Z = Σ_i e^(-E_i/kT)
Free energy: F = -kT ln Z
Average energy: <E> = -∂(ln Z)/∂β (β = 1/kT)
Entropy: S = -∂F/∂T
Pressure: P = -∂F/∂V

GRAND CANONICAL ENSEMBLE:
System at fixed T, V, μ (chemical potential).
Grand partition function: Z_G = Σ e^(-β(E_i - μN_i))
Average particle number: <N> = kT ∂(ln Z_G)/∂μ

EQUIPARTITION THEOREM:
Each quadratic degree of freedom contributes ½kT to average energy.
Total energy: U = (f/2)NkT = (f/2)nRT
Heat capacity: Cᵥ = (f/2)R per mole.

Monatomic ideal gas: f=3 (3 translational), U = 3/2 NkT, Cᵥ = 3/2 R
Diatomic: f=5 (3 trans + 2 rot), U = 5/2 NkT, Cᵥ = 5/2 R (at room T)

IDEAL GAS FROM STATISTICAL MECHANICS:
PV = NkT (derived from Z = V^N/(N!λ³ᴺ))
Chemical potential: μ = kT ln(n/n_Q) (n_Q = quantum concentration)

QUANTUM STATISTICS:
Fermions (half-integer spin): Fermi-Dirac statistics.
f_FD(ε) = 1/(e^((ε-μ)/kT) + 1)
At T=0: sharp cutoff at ε = E_F (Fermi energy).

Bosons (integer spin): Bose-Einstein statistics.
f_BE(ε) = 1/(e^((ε-μ)/kT) - 1)
At low T: Bose-Einstein condensation (all particles in ground state).

Photons (bosons, μ=0):
f_photon(ε) = 1/(e^(ε/kT) - 1)
Planck distribution for blackbody radiation.
u(ω) = ℏω³/(π²c³) × 1/(e^(ℏω/kT) - 1)

BLACKBODY RADIATION:
Stefan-Boltzmann law: P = σT⁴ (σ = 5.67×10⁻⁸ W/m²K⁴)
Wien's displacement law: λ_max T = 2.898 × 10⁻³ m·K
Rayleigh-Jeans (classical, fails at high ω): u(ω) ∝ ω²kT
Planck resolved UV catastrophe with quantization.

DEBYE MODEL OF SOLIDS:
Treats vibrations as phonons (quanta of sound).
Debye temperature: θ_D = ℏω_D/k
At high T: Cᵥ → 3R (Dulong-Petit law).
At low T: Cᵥ ∝ T³ (matches experiment well).

PHASE TRANSITIONS:
First order: discontinuous change in first derivative of G (e.g., melting, boiling).
Second order (continuous): continuous change but discontinuous in second derivative (e.g., ferromagnet at Curie point).
Order parameter: quantity that is zero above Tc and non-zero below.

ISING MODEL (simplest model of magnetism):
H = -J Σ s_i s_j - B Σ s_i (s_i = ±1)
1D: no phase transition.
2D: phase transition at kT_c/J = 2/ln(1+√2) ≈ 2.269 (Onsager solution).
Mean-field theory: overestimates T_c.

THERMODYNAMIC POTENTIALS:
Internal energy: U = TS - PV + μN, dU = TdS - PdV + μdN
Helmholtz: F = U - TS, dF = -SdT - PdV + μdN
Gibbs: G = U - TS + PV, dG = -SdT + VdP + μdN
Enthalpy: H = U + PV, dH = TdS + VdP + μdN

Maxwell relations (from exactness of differentials):
(∂T/∂V)_S = -(∂P/∂S)_V
(∂T/∂P)_S = (∂V/∂S)_P
(∂S/∂V)_T = (∂P/∂T)_V [most useful]
(∂S/∂P)_T = -(∂V/∂T)_P

IMPORTANT FORMULAS:
- S = k ln Ω
- P_i = e^(-βE_i)/Z
- Z = Σ e^(-βE_i)
- F = -kT ln Z
- <E> = -∂ ln Z/∂β
- U = (f/2)NkT (equipartition)
- Planck: u(ω) = ℏω³/(π²c³(e^(ℏω/kT)-1))
- Stefan-Boltzmann: P = σT⁴
- Wien: λ_max T = 2.898 × 10⁻³ m·K
