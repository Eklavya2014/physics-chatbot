THE LANTHANIDES (4f block, Z = 57–71)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

LANTHANIDE OVERVIEW
--------------------
Also called "Rare Earth Elements" (REEs) — though most are not actually rare in Earth's crust.
The 4f orbitals fill across this series (La has 4f⁰ to Lu with 4f¹⁴).
Lanthanide Contraction: Atomic and ionic radii decrease steadily across the series despite increasing Z.
  Reason: 4f electrons shield nuclear charge poorly (diffuse orbitals; poor shielding of one another) → increasing Zeff → contraction of outer orbitals.
  Consequence: 5d/6s orbitals contract → 3rd row TMs (Hf to Au) have very similar radii to 2nd row (Zr to Ag) → affects their chemistry (e.g. Zr and Hf nearly identical in chemistry, very hard to separate).

General Properties:
  Electron config: [Xe] 4fⁿ 5d⁰⁻¹ 6s². 4f electrons are CORE-LIKE (shielded by 5s² 5p⁶).
  Dominant OS: +3 (Ln³⁺) for ALL lanthanides. Some show +2 (Eu²⁺, Sm²⁺, Yb²⁺) or +4 (Ce⁴⁺, Tb⁴⁺, Pr⁴⁺).
  Valency: 3 (dominant).
  Similar chemistry: All form Ln³⁺ ions; compounds very similar → difficult to separate (historically by fractional crystallisation; now by HPLC or solvent extraction).
  Magnetic properties: Unpaired 4f electrons → paramagnetic (some very strongly so). Dy, Tb → single-molecule magnets.
  Luminescence: Sharp f-f electronic transitions → characteristic emission colours. Used in phosphors (TV screens, LEDs, lighting).
  Reactivity: All are reactive metals; react with water (slowly), air (burn when ignited), acids vigorously.

ELEMENT 57: LANTHANUM (La) — [Xe] 5d¹ 6s²
  Valency: 3. OS: +3. 4f⁰ — not technically a lanthanide (no 4f electrons) but group leader.
  Most reactive lanthanide; soft, silvery-white metal.
  La₂O₃: High refractive index glass additive; camera lenses.
  LaNi₅: Hydrogen storage (metal hydride batteries — NiMH).
  La in FCC (fluid catalytic cracking) catalysts: Stabilises zeolites at high temperature.
  Lanthanum hexaboride (LaB₆): Electron emitter for electron guns (SEM, TEM).

ELEMENT 58: CERIUM (Ce) — [Xe] 4f¹ 5d¹ 6s²
  Valency: 3, 4. OS: +3 (Ce³⁺), +4 (Ce⁴⁺ — strong oxidant; EO° = +1.72V vs SHE).
  Most abundant lanthanide. Only lanthanide with a stable +4 state in water.
  CeO₂ (ceria): Catalytic converter washcoat (oxygen storage; Ce³⁺ ↔ Ce⁴⁺ redox); polishing powder (glass, semiconductors); fuel cell electrolyte additive; UV absorber (sunscreen).
  Ce(SO₄)₂: Strong oxidising agent (analytical chemistry).
  Ce mischmetal alloy: ~50% Ce + La + Nd → used in lighter flints; iron and steel additive.
  Ce in phosphors: Yellow phosphor Ce:YAG in white LEDs (blue LED + yellow Ce phosphor → white light).

ELEMENT 59: PRASEODYMIUM (Pr) — [Xe] 4f³ 6s²
  Valency: 3, 4. OS: +3 (most stable), +4 (PrO₂ — less stable than CeO₂).
  Used with Nd in magnets (Pr-Nd-Fe-B); green/yellow glass and enamels (Pr oxide); Pr:YAG laser.
  Didymium glass (Pr + Nd): Glassblowers' protective goggles (absorbs yellow sodium emission).

ELEMENT 60: NEODYMIUM (Nd) — [Xe] 4f⁴ 6s²
  Valency: 3. OS: +3 only.
  Nd₂Fe₁₄B (NdFeB magnets): Strongest permanent magnets known.
    Applications: Electric motors (EVs, wind turbines, washing machines), hard drives, headphones, MRI machines, speakers, magnetic resonance imaging.
    Coercivity: Extremely high → resists demagnetisation. Energy product up to 60 MGOe.
  Nd:YAG laser: 1064 nm pulsed laser; cutting metals; surgical (ophthalmology, dentistry); rangefinding; welding.
  Nd in glass: Makes glass appear different colours under fluorescent vs daylight (alexandrite effect).
  Critical raw material: EV transition and wind power → massive demand for Nd.

ELEMENT 61: PROMETHIUM (Pm) — [Xe] 4f⁵ 6s²
  Valency: 3. All isotopes radioactive (only radioactive lanthanide with no stable isotopes).
  Most stable: ¹⁴⁵Pm (t½ = 17.7 years). Occurs only in trace amounts in uranium ores (from spontaneous fission).
  Atomic batteries (β-emitter): ¹⁴⁷Pm powered pacemakers (historically). Luminous paint (formerly). Thickness gauges. Rarely used due to radioactivity and short half-life.

ELEMENT 62: SAMARIUM (Sm) — [Xe] 4f⁶ 6s²
  Valency: 2, 3. OS: +2 (Sm²⁺ — reducing agent, less common), +3 (dominant).
  SmCo₅ / Sm₂Co₁₇ magnets: Second strongest permanent magnets (after NdFeB); better high-temperature performance (to 350°C vs ~200°C for NdFeB) → aerospace, military.
  ¹⁵³Sm-EDTMP: Radioactive; bone metastasis pain palliation.
  SmI₂ (samarium diiodide): Powerful single-electron reducing agent; key reagent in organic synthesis (ketyl radicals; Barbier reactions).

ELEMENT 63: EUROPIUM (Eu) — [Xe] 4f⁷ 6s²
  Valency: 2, 3. OS: +2 (Eu²⁺ — half-filled 4f, extra stable; ionic radius ≈ Sr²⁺), +3 (most stable in oxidising conditions).
  Eu³⁺ phosphors: Red phosphor in TV screens (Y₂O₃:Eu³⁺); fluorescent lamps.
  Eu²⁺ phosphors: Blue-green emission (SrAl₂O₄:Eu²⁺ → glow-in-the-dark materials — strontium aluminate).
  Banknote security: Eu luminescence in banknotes (UV fluorescence verification of authenticity — Euro notes).
  MRI contrast agent research: Eu complexes (paramagnetic).

ELEMENT 64: GADOLINIUM (Gd) — [Xe] 4f⁷ 5d¹ 6s²
  Valency: 3. OS: +3 only (half-filled 4f = extra stability → no +2/+4).
  Highest magnetic moment of any element (7 unpaired 4f electrons + 1 5d unpaired = 8 total for the [Xe] 4f⁷ 5d¹ configuration... actually Gd³⁺ has 7 unpaired → f⁷ half-filled).
  Gd-DTPA (gadolinium DTPA, Magnevist): MRI contrast agent (most widely used). Paramagnetic Gd³⁺ shortens T1 relaxation → bright on T1-weighted images → blood vessels, tumours, inflammation.
    NSF (Nephrogenic Systemic Fibrosis): Gd retention in severe renal failure — now using macrocyclic chelates (gadobutrol, gadoteridol) which are safer.
  Gd in neutron absorption: Gd has highest thermal neutron capture cross-section of any stable nuclide (49,000 barns for ¹⁵⁷Gd) → used in nuclear reactor control; BNCT adjunct.
  Gd₃Ga₅O₁₂ (GGG): Magnetic bubble memory; substrate for microwave devices.

ELEMENT 65: TERBIUM (Tb) — [Xe] 4f⁹ 6s²
  Valency: 3, 4. OS: +3 (dominant), +4 (less stable than Ce⁴⁺).
  Tb³⁺ green phosphor: Y₃Al₅O₁₂:Tb³⁺ (YAG:Tb) → green emission in tri-phosphor lamps; trichromatic white LEDs.
  Tb in NdFeB magnets: TbDyFe (terfenol-D) → giant magnetostrictive material (changes shape in magnetic field → precision actuators, sonar).
  Terbium fluorescence: Long-lived green luminescence → FRET acceptor in time-resolved assays.

ELEMENT 66: DYSPROSIUM (Dy) — [Xe] 4f¹⁰ 6s²
  Valency: 3. OS: +3.
  High magnetic moment. Dy³⁺ added to NdFeB magnets → maintains coercivity at high temperature (crucial for EV motors, wind turbines operating at elevated temperatures).
  Terfenol-D (Tb-Dy-Fe): Magnetostrictive actuator.
  Dy in single-molecule magnets (SMMs): Dy₂O₃ clusters → largest blocking temperature.
  Critical raw material for EV motors.

ELEMENT 67: HOLMIUM (Ho) — [Xe] 4f¹¹ 6s²
  Valency: 3. OS: +3. Highest magnetic moment of any naturally occurring element.
  Ho:YAG laser: 2100 nm pulsed laser; minimal water absorption → surgical applications (kidney stones, prostate, joints).
  Holmium magnetic poles (for creating extremely strong magnetic fields).
  ¹⁶⁶Ho radiotherapy: Bone metastases; liver cancer (SIR-Spheres Ho-166 microspheres).

ELEMENT 68: ERBIUM (Er) — [Xe] 4f¹² 6s²
  Valency: 3. OS: +3.
  Er:YAG laser: 2940 nm (maximal water absorption) → ablation of hard/soft tissue; skin resurfacing; dentistry; no thermal damage.
  Er-doped fibre amplifier (EDFA): 1550 nm emission → amplifies signals in optical fibre communications (key enabler of internet infrastructure). Er³⁺ excited by 980 nm pump laser → stimulated emission at 1550 nm.
  Er³⁺ pink-green glass (Er₂O₃).

ELEMENT 69: THULIUM (Tm) — [Xe] 4f¹³ 6s²
  Valency: 3; rarely 2. OS: +3 (dominant), +2 (Tm²⁺ in reducing conditions).
  Rarest stable lanthanide (after Pm which has no stable isotopes).
  Tm:YAG laser: 2013 nm; ophthalmology (photocoagulation).
  ¹⁷⁰Tm: Portable X-ray source (γ emitter, t½=128.6 days).

ELEMENT 70: YTTERBIUM (Yb) — [Xe] 4f¹⁴ 6s²
  Valency: 2, 3. OS: +2 (Yb²⁺ — 4f¹⁴ full = extra stable; strong reducing agent), +3 (most stable).
  Yb:YAG laser: 1030 nm; kilowatt-class industrial cutting/welding.
  Yb in stress gauges; catalysis (Lewis acid Yb triflate).
  ¹⁷⁴Yb: Optical atomic clock (most accurate clock ever made; uncertainty ~1 second in 300 billion years).

ELEMENT 71: LUTETIUM (Lu) — [Xe] 4f¹⁴ 5d¹ 6s²
  Valency: 3. OS: +3. Heaviest lanthanide; 4f¹⁴ complete.
  Densest lanthanide; hardest. Smallest ionic radius (Ln³⁺ contraction).
  Lu₂O₃:Eu: PET detector scintillator (LYSO = Lu₂(1-x)Y₂xSiO₅:Ce — key material in modern PET scanners).
  ¹⁷⁷Lu radiotherapy: ¹⁷⁷Lu-DOTATATE (Lutathera) → first-line for somatostatin receptor-positive NETs (FDA approved 2018). ¹⁷⁷Lu-PSMA-617 (Pluvicto) → metastatic prostate cancer (FDA 2022). Beta emitter; short tissue range.

THE ACTINIDES (5f block, Z = 89–103)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ACTINIDE OVERVIEW
------------------
All actinides are radioactive (all isotopes). Beyond Bi (Z=83), no stable isotopes exist.
5f electrons fill across this series. Unlike lanthanides, 5f electrons ARE chemically accessible.
Greater variety of oxidation states than lanthanides (5f, 6d, 7s all available for bonding).
Early actinides (Th, Pa, U, Np, Pu): Variable OS from +2 to +6 (U → +6 dominant; UO₂²⁺ stable).
Late actinides (Am-Lr): More lanthanide-like; +3 dominant.
All are toxic (radiological + chemical toxicity).

ELEMENT 89: ACTINIUM (Ac) — [Rn] 6d¹ 7s²
  OS: +3. t½(²²⁷Ac) = 21.77 years (alpha + beta emitter).
  Group leader; very rare natural element (from ²³⁵U decay chain); most from nuclear reactors.
  ²²⁵Ac radiotherapy: Highly promising for targeted alpha therapy (TAT) in cancer (²²⁵Ac-PSMA → prostate cancer; 4 alpha particles released per decay chain → very cytotoxic). Being developed rapidly.

ELEMENT 90: THORIUM (Th) — [Rn] 6d² 7s²
  OS: +4 (only). t½(²³²Th) = 14.05 billion years.
  Most stable thorium isotope; can be used as nuclear fuel.
  Thorium fuel cycle: ²³²Th + n → ²³³Th → ²³³Pa → ²³³U (fissile → can sustain chain reaction).
  Liquid Fluoride Thorium Reactor (LFTR): Proposed reactor type using molten ThF₄ + LiF. Advantages: abundant Th; less long-lived waste; harder to proliferate.
  ThO₂: Very high mp (3300°C); used in gas mantles (Welsbach mantle for white light — Th₀.₉₉Ce₀.₀₁O₂).

ELEMENT 91: PROTACTINIUM (Pa) — [Rn] 5f² 6d¹ 7s²
  OS: +4, +5 (PaO₂⁺ stable). Rare; very radioactive. t½(²³¹Pa) = 32,760 years.
  Only 1.3 mg total ever isolated. Used in ²³¹Pa/²³¹Pa geochemistry (ocean circulation tracers).

ELEMENT 92: URANIUM (U) — [Rn] 5f³ 6d¹ 7s²
  OS: +3, +4, +5, +6. Most stable/common: +6 (UO₂²⁺ = uranyl ion, linear, very stable).
  t½(²³⁸U) = 4.47 billion years; t½(²³⁵U) = 703.8 million years.
  Natural U: 99.27% ²³⁸U + 0.72% ²³⁵U + 0.0054% ²³⁴U. Only ²³⁵U fissile.
  Enrichment: UF₆ gaseous centrifuge → increase ²³⁵U to 3-5% (reactor fuel) or 90%+ (weapons-grade).
  Nuclear power: UO₂ pellets in Zircaloy cladding → fuel rods → nuclear reactor.
  Uranium glass (vaseline glass): Contains UO₂ → yellow-green; fluoresces brilliant green under UV.
  DU (Depleted Uranium): Waste from enrichment; mostly ²³⁸U; very dense (19.1 g/cm³) → armour-piercing ammunition; counterweights; radiation shielding. Toxic metal.
  UO₂²⁺ (uranyl): Yellow fluorescent; water-soluble; environmental contaminant from mining.
  U chemistry: Wide range of organometallic compounds; important in nuclear fuel cycle; PUREX process for reprocessing.

ELEMENT 93: NEPTUNIUM (Np) — [Rn] 5f⁴ 6d¹ 7s²
  OS: +3, +4, +5 (NpO₂⁺ most stable in solution), +6, +7. t½(²³⁷Np) = 2.14 million years.
  First transuranium element made artificially (McMillan & Abelson, 1940; Berkeley Cyclotron).
  Produced in nuclear reactors from ²³⁸U + n → ²³⁹U → ²³⁹Np → ²³⁹Pu.
  ²³⁷Np decays to ²³³Pa → ²³³U. Np-Pu nuclear battery research.

ELEMENT 94: PLUTONIUM (Pu) — [Rn] 5f⁶ 7s²
  OS: +3, +4, +5, +6 (PuO₂²⁺), +7. Most stable in acidic solution: Pu⁴⁺.
  t½(²³⁹Pu) = 24,110 years (most important isotope); t½(²⁴⁴Pu) = 80 million years.
  ²³⁹Pu: Fissile; produced in nuclear reactors from ²³⁸U. Critical mass ~5 kg.
  Nuclear weapons: Fat Man bomb (Nagasaki) used ²³⁹Pu implosion design.
  Plutonium-238 (²³⁸Pu, t½=87.7 yr): Intense alpha emitter → heat → RTG (radioisotope thermoelectric generator → powers spacecraft (Voyager, Cassini, Curiosity, New Horizons, Perseverance).
  Extremely toxic (alpha emitter if inhaled/ingested; bone seeker).
  Complicated metallurgy: 6 allotropic phases between RT and mp; density ranges from 16.0–19.8 g/cm³ depending on phase.
  PUREX process: Tributyl phosphate extraction → separates U + Pu from fission products in nuclear reprocessing.

ELEMENT 95: AMERICIUM (Am) — [Rn] 5f⁷ 7s²
  OS: +2, +3 (most stable), +4, +5, +6. t½(²⁴¹Am) = 432.2 years.
  ²⁴¹Am: Alpha emitter → ionises air in ionisation-chamber smoke detectors (most common household application of radioactivity). 1 µg of Am in each detector.
  ²⁴³Am: Longer-lived (t½=7370 yr); used as target to make heavier elements.
  Am in radiography; Cm production.

ELEMENT 96: CURIUM (Cm) — [Rn] 5f⁷ 6d¹ 7s²
  OS: +3 (dominant), +4. t½(²⁴⁷Cm) = 15.6 million years; t½(²⁴²Cm) = 162.8 days.
  Named after Pierre and Marie Curie.
  ²⁴²Cm: Alpha emitter; very intense heat source → RTG; Mars Perseverance rover (Alpha Particle X-ray Spectrometer, APXS, uses ²⁴⁴Cm X-ray source for elemental analysis of rock/soil).

ELEMENTS 97–103: BERKELIUM (Bk), CALIFORNIUM (Cf), EINSTEINIUM (Es), FERMIUM (Fm), MENDELEVIUM (Md), NOBELIUM (No), LAWRENCIUM (Lr)
  All synthesised at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (mostly) by bombardment of actinide targets.
  All radioactive; trace amounts (except Cf).
  ²⁵²Cf (Californium-252): t½=2.65 yr; spontaneous fission source → intense neutron source.
    Uses: Neutron activation analysis; nuclear reactor startup; oil well logging; cancer therapy (intracavitary); portable neutron source.
  All have +3 dominant OS (increasingly lanthanide-like).
  Lr (Z=103): Last actinide; [Rn] 5f¹⁴ 7s² 7p¹.

Island of Stability:
  Predicted region of superheavy nuclides with enhanced nuclear stability.
  Centred around Z=114 (Fl), N=184 (closed shell).
  ²⁹⁸Fl (Z=114, N=184): Most sought-after "island of stability" nuclide.
  Current ²⁸⁹Fl (N=175) has t½=1.9 seconds — still far from N=184.
  If reached: Might have t½ of minutes to years → allow chemical study.
  Theoretical basis: Nuclear shell model; complete proton and neutron shells → extra binding energy (analogous to noble gas electronic stability).

Naming Conventions for Superheavy Elements:
  Named after: scientists (Curium, Einsteinium, Fermium, Meitnerium, Roentgenium, Seaborgium, Nobelium, Rutherfordium, Bohrium, Copernicium, Oganesson), places (Californium, Berkelium, Darmstadtium, Hassium, Dubnium, Moscovium, Livermorium, Tennessine), or properties.
  Naming rights: Awarded by IUPAC to discoverers after verification. Discovery = synthesis of ≥1 atom confirmed by two independent groups.
  Priority disputes: USA (LBNL) vs Soviet Union (JINR Dubna) in Cold War era; now resolved for most.

Superheavy Chemistry:
  Relativistic effects dominate: Electrons near heavy nuclei travel at significant fraction of speed of light → mass increases → orbitals contract/expand → changes chemistry from periodic table predictions.
  Fl (114): Experiments show Fl adsorbs on gold surfaces (chemical properties measured) → behaves more like a noble gas than expected for Group 14 element. Volatile.
  Cn (112): Experiments suggest may be a gas at RT (highly volatile) due to relativistic 7s orbital stabilisation.
  Hassium (108): HsO₄ produced and shown to be volatile → like OsO₄ → confirms Group 8 behaviour.
  Chemistry can only be done on single atoms — "one atom at a time" chemistry using automated rapid separation systems.
