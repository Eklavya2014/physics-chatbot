POLYMER CHEMISTRY, MATERIALS SCIENCE AND INDUSTRIAL CHEMISTRY
==============================================================

PART 1: POLYMER CHEMISTRY

BASIC CONCEPTS:
Polymer: large molecule made of many repeating units (monomers).
Degree of polymerization (n): number of monomer units.
Molecular weight: Mn (number average), Mw (weight average), Mz (z-average).
PDI (Polydispersity Index) = Mw/Mn. Monodisperse = 1. Real polymers > 1.
Tacticity: stereoregularity of polymer chain.
Isotactic: all substituents on same side. Syndiotactic: alternating. Atactic: random.
Isotactic polypropylene: crystalline, high melting point. Atactic: amorphous, low Tm.

ADDITION POLYMERIZATION:

Free Radical Mechanism:
Initiation: I → 2R• (initiator, e.g., benzoyl peroxide, AIBN).
             R• + CH₂=CH₂ → R-CH₂-CH₂•
Propagation: R-CH₂-CH₂• + nCH₂=CH₂ → R-(CH₂CH₂)n-CH₂CH₂•
Termination: Combination: 2R'• → R'-R'. Disproportionation: 2R'• → R'H + R'=.
Chain transfer: R'• + monomer → dead chain + new radical.
Rate ∝ [M][I]^½. Degree of polymerization decreases with temperature.
Temperature effect: faster at high T but lower MW.

Important free radical polymers:
Polyethylene (LDPE): low density, branched, soft, flexible. Food wrap.
Polypropylene (PP): isotactic form, rigid, high melting, food containers.
PVC (polyvinyl chloride): rigid pipes, floor tiles; plasticized: soft, flexible.
Polystyrene (PS): clear, rigid; foamed = Styrofoam (insulation, cups).
PTFE (Teflon): polytetrafluoroethylene, non-stick, chemically inert.
PMMA (Plexiglass/Lucite): transparent, optical quality.
Polyacrylonitrile: textile fiber (Orlon, Acrilon). Also precursor to carbon fiber.

Cationic Polymerization:
Lewis acid initiator (BF₃, AlCl₃, SnCl₄). Low temperature.
Carbocation intermediate. Only isobutylene, vinyl ethers, styrene (stable carbocations).
Fast propagation, difficult to control.

Anionic Polymerization:
Organolithium or organosodium initiator. Very clean, controlled.
No termination if no impurities → living polymerization.
Narrow PDI → nearly monodisperse. Block copolymers made by sequential addition.

CONDENSATION POLYMERIZATION:

Nylon-6,6 (Polyamide):
Monomers: hexanedioic acid (adipic acid) + hexane-1,6-diamine.
Reaction: -COOH + H₂N- → -CO-NH- + H₂O (amide bond).
Properties: strong, tough, low friction. Fibers, gears, nylon stockings.

Nylon-6 (Polycaprolactam):
Monomer: caprolactam (cyclic amide), ring-opening polymerization.
Same properties as Nylon-6,6.

Polyester (PET — Polyethylene Terephthalate):
Monomers: terephthalic acid + ethylene glycol (or methyl ester + transesterification).
Reaction: -COOH + HO- → -COO- + H₂O (ester bond).
Properties: transparent, strong fibers and films. Beverage bottles, clothing (Dacron, Terylene).
Biodegradable polyesters: PLA (polylactic acid), PHB, PHBV.

Polyurethane:
Diisocyanate + diol.
Reaction: -N=C=O + HO- → -NH-CO-O- (no small molecule released!).
Foams (mattresses, insulation), adhesives, elastomers.

Polycarbonate:
Bisphenol A + phosgene (COCl₂).
Transparent, strong, high impact resistance. DVDs, eyeglass lenses, bullet-resistant glass.

Epoxy resin:
Bisphenol A diglycidyl ether + amine hardener. Cross-linked.
Strong adhesive, coatings, composite matrices.

COPOLYMERS:
Random copolymer: A and B randomly distributed.
Alternating copolymer: -A-B-A-B-.
Block copolymer: -AAAA-BBBB-.
Graft copolymer: backbone of A with B side chains.

Important copolymers:
ABS (acrylonitrile-butadiene-styrene): engineering plastic (LEGO, car dashboards).
SBR (styrene-butadiene rubber): synthetic rubber for tires.
EVA (ethylene-vinyl acetate): flexible, used in footwear, foam.

NATURAL POLYMERS:
Natural rubber: cis-1,4-polyisoprene. Elastic.
Vulcanization (Goodyear, 1839): cross-linking with S → stronger, less sticky, maintains elasticity over temperature range.
Gutta-percha: trans-1,4-polyisoprene. Hard, non-elastic.
Cellulose: β-1,4-polyglucose. Cotton (cellulose), paper, cellophane.
Cellulose derivatives: cellulose acetate (rayon, photographic film), nitrocellulose (explosives, film).
Silk: β-sheet protein. Antiparallel β-sheet structure.
Wool: α-helix protein (keratin).

POLYMER PROPERTIES:
Glass transition temperature (Tg): below Tg, polymer is glassy/brittle. Above, rubbery.
Crystalline melting temperature (Tm): for semicrystalline polymers.
Amorphous polymers: no Tm, only Tg.
Semicrystalline polymers: both Tg and Tm.
Crystallinity increases with: tacticity, symmetry of repeat unit, intermolecular forces.

PART 2: INDUSTRIAL CHEMISTRY

IMPORTANT INDUSTRIAL PROCESSES:

NITROGEN INDUSTRY:
Haber-Bosch Process (NH₃):
N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ (ΔH = -92 kJ/mol)
Conditions: 400-500°C, 150-300 atm, Fe catalyst (with K₂O, Al₂O₃ promoters).
Le Chatelier: high pressure favors NH₃ (fewer moles of gas).
High T: unfavorable thermodynamically but needed for kinetics.
Compromise: 400-500°C gives acceptable rate with ~15% conversion.
~170 million tons/year. Used for fertilizers (80%), explosives.

Ostwald Process (HNO₃):
4NH₃ + 5O₂ → 4NO + 6H₂O (900°C, Pt-Rh catalyst)
2NO + O₂ → 2NO₂
3NO₂ + H₂O → 2HNO₃ + NO
Used for fertilizers, explosives (TNT, dynamite), dyes.

SULFUR INDUSTRY:
Contact Process (H₂SO₄):
S + O₂ → SO₂ (burning sulfur or roasting FeS₂)
2SO₂ + O₂ → 2SO₃ (V₂O₅ catalyst, 450°C) [equilibrium, need ~99% conversion]
SO₃ + H₂SO₄ → H₂S₂O₇ (oleum, prevents SO₃ mist)
H₂S₂O₇ + H₂O → 2H₂SO₄
Largest volume industrial chemical. Used in fertilizers (ammonium sulfate, superphosphate), petroleum refining, metal processing, paper.

CHLOR-ALKALI PROCESS:
2NaCl + 2H₂O → Cl₂ + H₂ + 2NaOH (electrolysis of brine)
Membrane cell: Nafion membrane separates compartments.
Cathode: 2H₂O + 2e⁻ → H₂ + 2OH⁻
Anode: 2Cl⁻ → Cl₂ + 2e⁻
Products: Cl₂ (PVC, bleach, HCl), H₂ (energy, NH₃), NaOH (paper, soap, aluminum extraction).

PETROLEUM REFINING:
Fractional distillation → separation by boiling point.
Fractions (from bottom to top, decreasing bp):
Fuel oil/bitumen (>340°C), diesel/gasoil (250-340°C), kerosene/jet fuel (170-250°C), gasoline (40-200°C), naphtha, LPG/gas (<40°C).

Cracking: breaking large alkanes into smaller, more valuable fractions (especially gasoline).
Thermal cracking: 450-750°C, high P. Free radical mechanism.
Fluid catalytic cracking (FCC): zeolite catalyst, ~500°C, 1 atm. Carbocation mechanism.
Hydrocracking: catalyst + H₂. More saturated products.

Reforming: converting straight-chain alkanes to branched alkanes and aromatics for higher octane.
Catalytic reforming (Pt/Re/Al₂O₃, 500°C, 35 atm): dehydrogenation, isomerization, cyclization.
n-heptane: octane number 0. Isooctane (2,2,4-trimethylpentane): octane number 100.

Alkylation: isobutane + butene → isooctane (acid catalyst HF or H₂SO₄).
Isomerization: straight-chain → branched chain (AlCl₃ or Pt catalyst).
Desulfurization: HDS (hydrodesulfurization) removes S as H₂S using CoMo/Al₂O₃ catalyst.

POLYMER INDUSTRY:
Ethylene (ethene): steam cracking of naphtha. Key olefin.
CH₃CH₂CH₂CH₃ → CH₂=CH₂ + 2CH₄ (at 800°C, steam)
Also propylene, butadiene from steam cracking.
Ethylene → LDPE, HDPE, PVC, polystyrene, polyethylene terephthalate, polyvinyl acetate.

PART 3: GREEN AND SUSTAINABLE CHEMISTRY

ATOM ECONOMY:
Defined by Barry Trost (1991): AE = MW(desired product)/ΣMW(all products) × 100%.
High AE = less waste. Synthesis design should aim for high AE.
Diels-Alder: AE = 100% (all atoms incorporated into product, no byproduct).
Wittig reaction: AE = low (Ph₃P=O byproduct = large MW waste).
Grignard: moderate AE.

SOLVENT CONSIDERATIONS:
Traditional solvents: often volatile, flammable, toxic (CH₂Cl₂, THF, DMF, toluene).
Green alternatives:
Water: safest solvent for many reactions.
Supercritical CO₂: non-toxic, recyclable, tunable properties.
Ionic liquids: low vapor pressure, reusable, tunable.
PEG (polyethylene glycol): recyclable, non-volatile.
Solvent-free reactions: mechanochemistry (grinding), microwave heating.

CATALYSIS IN GREEN CHEMISTRY:
Biocatalysis: enzymes as catalysts. High selectivity, mild conditions, biodegradable.
Whole-cell biotransformations.
Asymmetric catalysis: chiral catalysts produce one enantiomer preferentially.
Organocatalysis: small organic molecules as catalysts (proline, BINOL).
Heterogeneous catalysis: easily separated from product, recyclable.

E-FACTOR EXAMPLES:
Oil refining: ~0.1 (very efficient, byproducts used as fuel).
Bulk chemicals: 1-5.
Fine chemicals: 5-50.
Pharmaceuticals: 25-100+ (many protection/deprotection steps, low yields).

RENEWABLE FEEDSTOCKS:
Biomass-derived chemicals: ethanol (fermentation), lactic acid, succinic acid.
Biodiesel: transesterification of vegetable oils with methanol (acid/base catalyst). FAME + glycerol.
Bioethanol: fermentation of sugars/starch by yeast (Saccharomyces cerevisiae).
C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₂H₅OH + 2CO₂ (glucose fermentation).
Cellulosic ethanol: enzymatic hydrolysis of cellulose (lignocellulosic biomass) before fermentation.

BIO-BASED POLYMERS:
PLA (polylactic acid): from lactic acid (fermentation of sugars). Compostable.
Bio-PET: some petroleum components replaced with bio-based materials.
Starch-based plastics: blends for packaging.
PHB/PHBV: bacterial polyesters, truly biodegradable in soil/water.

WATER TREATMENT:
Primary treatment: physical removal of solids (screens, settling).
Secondary treatment: biological oxidation of dissolved organics (activated sludge).
BOD reduced >90%.
Tertiary treatment: nutrient removal (N, P), disinfection (chlorination, UV, ozone).
Reverse osmosis: removes dissolved salts (desalination, ultra-pure water).
Coagulation: alum Al₂(SO₄)₃ → Al(OH)₃ (floc) adsorbs suspended particles.
Disinfection byproducts (DBPs): chlorine + organic matter → trihalomethanes (carcinogenic?).
Alternative disinfectants: ozone, UV radiation, chloramine.
