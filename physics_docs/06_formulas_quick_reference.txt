CLASS 10 PHYSICS - MASTER FORMULA SHEET AND QUICK REFERENCE
============================================================

PHYSICAL CONSTANTS
- Speed of light in vacuum: c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s
- Charge on electron: e = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
- Planck's constant: h = 6.63 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s
- Near point of normal human eye: 25 cm
- Far point of normal human eye: infinity
- Frequency of AC in India: 50 Hz
- Domestic voltage in India: 220 V

CHAPTER 1: LIGHT - REFLECTION AND REFRACTION

Mirror Formula: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f
Relation: f = R/2 (or R = 2f)
Magnification (mirror): m = -v/u = h'/h
Snell's Law: n₁ sin i = n₂ sin r
Refractive index: n = c/v = sin i / sin r
Lens Formula: 1/v - 1/u = 1/f
Magnification (lens): m = v/u = h'/h
Power of lens: P = 1/f(in meters), unit = Dioptre (D)
Combined power: P = P₁ + P₂ + P₃

Sign Convention for Mirrors and Lenses:
- Distances measured from pole/optical centre.
- Direction of incident light: positive (+)
- Against incident light: negative (-)
- Concave mirror: f = negative, R = negative
- Convex mirror: f = positive, R = positive
- Convex lens: f = positive
- Concave lens: f = negative

Refractive Indices:
- Air: ~1
- Water: 1.33
- Glass: 1.5
- Diamond: 2.42

CHAPTER 2: HUMAN EYE AND COLOURFUL WORLD

Near point (normal eye) = 25 cm
Far point (normal eye) = infinity

Defects and Corrections:
- Myopia: concave lens (negative power)
- Hypermetropia: convex lens (positive power)
- Presbyopia: bifocal lens

Spectrum order: VIBGYOR (Violet to Red, increasing wavelength)
Scattering: shorter wavelengths scatter more (∝ 1/λ⁴)
- Blue sky: blue light scattered most
- Red sunrise/sunset: other wavelengths scattered away

CHAPTER 3: ELECTRICITY

Electric Current: I = Q/t (unit: Ampere, A)
Potential Difference: V = W/Q (unit: Volt, V)
Ohm's Law: V = IR
Resistance: R = V/I (unit: Ohm, Ω)
Resistivity: R = ρl/A → ρ = RA/l (unit: Ω·m)

Resistors in Series: R_s = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + ...
Resistors in Parallel: 1/R_p = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃ + ...

Electric Power:
P = VI = I²R = V²/R (unit: Watt, W)

Electrical Energy:
E = Pt = VIt = I²Rt = V²t/R (unit: Joule, J)
Commercial unit: 1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J

Joule's Heating Law:
H = I²Rt = VIt = V²t/R (unit: Joule, J)

CHAPTER 4: MAGNETIC EFFECTS OF ELECTRIC CURRENT

Force on current-carrying conductor:
F = BIl sin θ
For perpendicular conductor: F = BIl

Rules:
- Right-Hand Thumb Rule: direction of B around a current wire
- Fleming's Left-Hand Rule: direction of force on conductor (Motor)
- Fleming's Right-Hand Rule: direction of induced current (Generator)

KEY DEVICES SUMMARY:
- Electromagnet: Solenoid + soft iron core
- Electric Motor: Electrical energy → Mechanical energy (uses commutator)
- Electric Generator: Mechanical energy → Electrical energy (uses slip rings for AC)

Electromagnetic Induction:
- Changing magnetic flux induces EMF.
- More turns, faster change, stronger field → more induced EMF.

AC in India: 220 V, 50 Hz (frequency)
Household wiring:
- Live wire: Red (220 V)
- Neutral wire: Black (0 V)
- Earth wire: Green (safety)

CHAPTER 5: SOURCES OF ENERGY

Energy Conversions:
- Thermal plant: Chemical (fuel) → Heat → Kinetic (steam) → Electrical
- Hydro plant: Potential (water) → Kinetic → Electrical
- Solar cell: Light → Electrical (direct)
- Wind turbine: Kinetic (wind) → Electrical
- Nuclear: Nuclear (fission/fusion) → Heat → Kinetic → Electrical

Biogas composition: ~55-70% methane (CH₄)

Solar constant: ~1400 W/m² (at earth's surface)

Nuclear reactions:
- Fission: Heavy nucleus splits (e.g., U-235)
- Fusion: Light nuclei combine (e.g., H + H → He)
- E = mc² (Einstein's mass-energy equivalence)

IMPORTANT DEFINITIONS

Reflection: Bouncing back of light from a surface.
Refraction: Bending of light as it passes from one medium to another.
Dispersion: Splitting of white light into constituent colors by a prism.
Scattering: Redirection of light by particles in different directions.
Total Internal Reflection: Complete reflection of light within a denser medium when angle exceeds critical angle.
Power of accommodation: Ability of eye to adjust focal length to see objects at different distances.
Myopia: Cannot see distant objects clearly (corrected by concave lens).
Hypermetropia: Cannot see nearby objects clearly (corrected by convex lens).
Resistance: Opposition to flow of electric current.
Resistivity: Inherent property of a material to resist current flow.
Ohm's Law: V = IR (at constant temperature).
Electromagnetic Induction: Production of EMF due to changing magnetic flux.
Electric Motor: Converts electrical energy to mechanical energy.
Electric Generator: Converts mechanical energy to electrical energy.
Renewable Energy: Energy from sources that are naturally replenished (solar, wind, hydro, biogas).
Nuclear Fission: Splitting of heavy nucleus to release energy.
Nuclear Fusion: Combining of light nuclei to release energy.
Fossil Fuels: Non-renewable fuels formed from ancient organisms (coal, petroleum, natural gas).
Biogas: Gas (mainly methane) produced by anaerobic decomposition of organic waste.
Tyndall Effect: Scattering of light by colloidal particles making the beam visible.
Rayleigh Scattering: Scattering of light by fine particles (inversely proportional to λ⁴).

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
1. In mirror formula, sign convention must be applied correctly (u is always negative for real objects).
2. Lens formula is 1/v - 1/u = 1/f (note the minus, unlike mirror formula).
3. Magnification for mirrors is m = -v/u; for lenses m = +v/u.
4. In parallel circuits: current divides, voltage is same across all.
5. In series circuits: current is same, voltage divides.
6. Fuse is always in series, voltmeter always in parallel, ammeter always in series.
7. Concave lens always forms virtual, erect, diminished image.
8. Convex mirror always forms virtual, erect, diminished image.
9. Myopia = concave lens; Hypermetropia = convex lens (easy to confuse!).
10. Motor uses Left-hand rule; Generator uses Right-hand rule.
