CLASS 11 PHYSICS - THERMODYNAMICS, WAVES AND OSCILLATIONS
==========================================================

CHAPTER: THERMAL PROPERTIES OF MATTER

TEMPERATURE AND HEAT:
- Temperature: measure of average kinetic energy of molecules.
- Heat: energy transferred due to temperature difference.
- Scales: Celsius (°C), Kelvin (K), Fahrenheit (°F)
- K = °C + 273.15
- °F = (9/5)°C + 32

THERMAL EXPANSION:
- Linear expansion: ΔL = αLΔT (α = coefficient of linear expansion)
- Area expansion: ΔA = βAΔT (β = 2α)
- Volume expansion: ΔV = γVΔT (γ = 3α)

Anomalous expansion of water: water expands on cooling from 4°C to 0°C.
Density of water is maximum at 4°C.

SPECIFIC HEAT CAPACITY:
Q = mcΔT
c = specific heat capacity (J/kg·K)
Specific heat of water = 4186 J/kg·K (highest of common substances)

Molar heat capacity: C = Mc (M = molar mass)

CALORIMETRY:
Heat lost = Heat gained (in an isolated system)
m₁c₁(T₁ - T_f) = m₂c₂(T_f - T₂)

LATENT HEAT:
Heat absorbed/released during phase change (no temperature change).
Q = mL
- Latent heat of fusion of ice: L_f = 3.36 × 10⁵ J/kg
- Latent heat of vaporization of water: L_v = 2.26 × 10⁶ J/kg

HEAT TRANSFER:
1. Conduction: Heat transfer through solids by molecular collisions.
   Q/t = kA(T₁-T₂)/d (k = thermal conductivity, A = area, d = thickness)
   Good conductors: metals. Poor conductors (insulators): wood, rubber, air.

2. Convection: Heat transfer through fluids (liquid/gas) by actual movement of fluid.
   - Natural convection: due to density difference (hot air rises).
   - Forced convection: fan, pump.

3. Radiation: Heat transfer through electromagnetic waves (no medium needed).
   Stefan-Boltzmann law: P = σAεT⁴
   σ = 5.67 × 10⁻⁸ W/m²·K⁴ (Stefan's constant)
   ε = emissivity (0 for perfect reflector, 1 for perfect blackbody)
   Net radiation: P_net = σAε(T⁴ - T_s⁴)

Newton's Law of Cooling: Rate of cooling ∝ temperature difference with surroundings.
dT/dt = -k(T - T_s)

CHAPTER: THERMODYNAMICS

THERMODYNAMIC TERMS:
- System: defined region under study.
- Surroundings: everything outside the system.
- Thermal equilibrium: same temperature throughout.
- State variables: P, V, T, n (describe the state of a system).
- Process: change from one state to another.

ZEROTH LAW: If A is in thermal equilibrium with C, and B is in thermal equilibrium with C, then A and B are in thermal equilibrium. This defines temperature.

FIRST LAW OF THERMODYNAMICS:
ΔU = Q - W
Q = heat added to system, W = work done BY system, ΔU = change in internal energy.
This is conservation of energy.
W = ∫PdV = PΔV (for constant pressure)

THERMODYNAMIC PROCESSES:
1. Isothermal (constant T): PV = constant (Boyle's law), ΔU = 0, Q = W
2. Adiabatic (Q=0): PVγ = constant, ΔU = -W, Q = 0
3. Isochoric (constant V): W = 0, ΔU = Q = nCᵥΔT
4. Isobaric (constant P): W = PΔV = nRΔT, Q = nCₚΔT

HEAT CAPACITIES OF IDEAL GAS:
- Cᵥ = heat capacity at constant volume = (f/2)R (f = degrees of freedom)
- Cₚ = heat capacity at constant pressure = Cᵥ + R
- γ = Cₚ/Cᵥ = 1 + 2/f
  - Monatomic (He, Ar): f=3, γ = 5/3 ≈ 1.67
  - Diatomic (N₂, O₂): f=5, γ = 7/5 = 1.4
  - Triatomic: f=6, γ = 4/3 ≈ 1.33

SECOND LAW OF THERMODYNAMICS:
- Kelvin-Planck: No heat engine can convert all heat into work.
- Clausius: Heat cannot spontaneously flow from cold to hot body.
- Entropy of universe always increases for irreversible processes.

HEAT ENGINES:
Efficiency: η = W/Q_H = 1 - Q_C/Q_H = 1 - T_C/T_H (Carnot efficiency)
- Carnot engine is the most efficient possible engine between T_H and T_C.
- No real engine can have efficiency equal to or greater than Carnot efficiency.

REFRIGERATORS:
COP = Q_C/W = Q_C/(Q_H - Q_C) = T_C/(T_H - T_C)

CHAPTER: KINETIC THEORY OF GASES

ASSUMPTIONS:
1. Gas consists of large number of molecules.
2. Molecules are point masses (volume negligible).
3. No intermolecular forces except during collision.
4. Collisions are perfectly elastic.
5. Molecules move randomly in all directions.

IDEAL GAS EQUATION:
PV = nRT = NkT
R = 8.314 J/mol·K (universal gas constant)
k = 1.38 × 10⁻²³ J/K (Boltzmann constant)
k = R/Nₐ (Nₐ = 6.022 × 10²³ = Avogadro's number)

KINETIC INTERPRETATION OF PRESSURE:
P = (1/3)ρv²_rms = (1/3)(nm)v²_rms
n = number density, m = mass of one molecule

RMS SPEED:
v_rms = √(3RT/M) = √(3kT/m)
Average speed: v_avg = √(8RT/πM)
Most probable speed: v_mp = √(2RT/M)
Relation: v_mp < v_avg < v_rms

KINETIC ENERGY:
Average KE per molecule = (3/2)kT
Total internal energy = U = (f/2)nRT

MEAN FREE PATH:
Average distance between collisions.
λ = kT/(√2 πd²P)
d = diameter of molecule

CHAPTER: OSCILLATIONS (SHM)

SIMPLE HARMONIC MOTION (SHM):
Motion in which restoring force is proportional to and opposite to displacement.
F = -kx (Hooke's law)
ma = -kx → a = -(k/m)x = -ω²x

GENERAL EQUATION: x = A sin(ωt + φ)
- A = amplitude (maximum displacement)
- ω = angular frequency = 2π/T = 2πf
- φ = initial phase
- T = 2π√(m/k) = period
- f = 1/T = frequency (Hz)

VELOCITY: v = Aω cos(ωt + φ) = ω√(A²-x²)
ACCELERATION: a = -Aω² sin(ωt + φ) = -ω²x

ENERGY IN SHM:
- KE = ½mω²(A²-x²) = ½mω²A²cos²(ωt+φ)
- PE = ½mω²x² = ½kx² = ½mω²A²sin²(ωt+φ)
- Total energy = ½mω²A² = ½kA² (constant, independent of time)

SIMPLE PENDULUM:
T = 2π√(L/g) (valid for small angles, < 15°)
- Independent of mass and amplitude.
- Increases with length, decreases with g.

SPRING-MASS SYSTEM:
T = 2π√(m/k)
- Springs in series: 1/k_eff = 1/k₁ + 1/k₂
- Springs in parallel: k_eff = k₁ + k₂

DAMPED OSCILLATIONS:
Amplitude decreases exponentially due to resistive forces.
x = Ae^(-bt/2m) cos(ω't + φ)

RESONANCE:
When driving frequency = natural frequency, amplitude becomes maximum.

CHAPTER: WAVES

WAVE PROPERTIES:
- Wavelength (λ): distance between two consecutive crests/troughs.
- Frequency (f): number of oscillations per second.
- Amplitude (A): maximum displacement.
- Wave speed: v = fλ
- Period: T = 1/f

TYPES OF WAVES:
1. Transverse waves: displacement perpendicular to wave direction (light, string waves).
2. Longitudinal waves: displacement parallel to wave direction (sound).

WAVE EQUATION:
y(x,t) = A sin(kx - ωt + φ)
k = 2π/λ (wave number), ω = 2πf (angular frequency)
Wave speed: v = ω/k = fλ

SPEED OF WAVES:
- String: v = √(T/μ) (T = tension, μ = linear mass density)
- Sound in medium: v = √(B/ρ) (B = bulk modulus, ρ = density)
- Sound in ideal gas: v = √(γP/ρ) = √(γRT/M)
- Speed of sound in air at 0°C ≈ 332 m/s; at room temperature ≈ 343 m/s.
- Speed increases with temperature: v ∝ √T

SUPERPOSITION AND INTERFERENCE:
When two waves overlap, displacements add algebraically.
- Constructive interference: path difference = nλ (n = 0,1,2,...)
- Destructive interference: path difference = (n+½)λ

STANDING WAVES:
Formed by superposition of two identical waves travelling in opposite directions.
y = 2A sin(kx) cos(ωt)
- Nodes: points of zero displacement (x = 0, λ/2, λ, ...)
- Antinodes: points of maximum displacement (x = λ/4, 3λ/4, ...)

HARMONICS IN STRINGS (fixed at both ends):
Fundamental (1st harmonic): f₁ = v/2L
nth harmonic: fₙ = nv/2L = n×f₁
All harmonics present.

HARMONICS IN PIPES:
Open pipe (open at both ends):
f₁ = v/2L, all harmonics present.

Closed pipe (closed at one end):
f₁ = v/4L, only odd harmonics present.
f₁ : f₃ : f₅ = 1 : 3 : 5

BEATS:
When two waves of slightly different frequencies superpose.
Beat frequency = |f₁ - f₂|
Beat period = 1/|f₁ - f₂|

DOPPLER EFFECT:
Apparent change in frequency due to relative motion between source and observer.
f' = f × (v ± v_o)/(v ∓ v_s)
- Upper signs: observer/source approaching.
- Lower signs: observer/source receding.
v = speed of sound, v_o = observer speed, v_s = source speed.
