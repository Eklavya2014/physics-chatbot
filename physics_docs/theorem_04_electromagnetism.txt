ELECTROMAGNETISM - MAXWELL'S EQUATIONS AND THEOREMS
=====================================================

MAXWELL'S EQUATIONS - THE FOUNDATION OF ELECTROMAGNETISM

James Clerk Maxwell unified electricity, magnetism, and optics into a single theory (1865).
These four equations describe ALL classical electromagnetic phenomena.

IN VACUUM (differential form):
∇·E⃗ = ρ/ε₀          (1. Gauss's Law for E)
∇·B⃗ = 0              (2. Gauss's Law for B - no magnetic monopoles)
∇×E⃗ = -∂B⃗/∂t        (3. Faraday's Law)
∇×B⃗ = μ₀J⃗ + μ₀ε₀∂E⃗/∂t  (4. Ampere-Maxwell Law)

IN VACUUM (integral form):
∮E⃗·dA⃗ = Q_enclosed/ε₀           (1. Gauss's Law)
∮B⃗·dA⃗ = 0                        (2. No magnetic monopoles)
∮E⃗·dL⃗ = -dΦ_B/dt                (3. Faraday's Law)
∮B⃗·dL⃗ = μ₀I + μ₀ε₀ dΦ_E/dt     (4. Ampere-Maxwell Law)

PHYSICAL MEANING OF EACH EQUATION:
1. Gauss's Law for E: Electric field lines originate from positive charges and end on negative charges. Net flux through closed surface = enclosed charge/ε₀.
2. Gauss's Law for B: Magnetic field lines always form closed loops — there are no magnetic monopoles (no isolated N or S poles exist).
3. Faraday's Law: A changing magnetic field creates a curling electric field. The EMF induced equals the rate of change of magnetic flux (with negative sign from Lenz's law).
4. Ampere-Maxwell Law: Magnetic fields are created by electric currents AND by changing electric fields. Maxwell added the displacement current term (ε₀∂E/∂t) which was the key to predicting electromagnetic waves.

MAXWELL'S DISPLACEMENT CURRENT - THE CRUCIAL ADDITION:
Before Maxwell, Ampere's law was: ∇×B⃗ = μ₀J⃗
Problem: This violated charge conservation (∇·J⃗ + ∂ρ/∂t = 0).
Maxwell added displacement current: J_d = ε₀∂E⃗/∂t
Physical meaning: A changing electric field acts like a current in producing magnetic fields.
This completed the equations and predicted electromagnetic waves.

ELECTROMAGNETIC WAVES FROM MAXWELL'S EQUATIONS:
Take curl of Faraday's law, substitute Ampere-Maxwell law:
∇²E⃗ = μ₀ε₀ ∂²E⃗/∂t²
This is a wave equation with speed: c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀)
Substituting μ₀ = 4π × 10⁻⁷ H/m and ε₀ = 8.85 × 10⁻¹² F/m:
c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s — exactly the measured speed of light!
Maxwell concluded: LIGHT IS AN ELECTROMAGNETIC WAVE.

IN MATTER:
Replace ε₀ → ε = ε₀ε_r and μ₀ → μ = μ₀μ_r
∇·D⃗ = ρ_f where D⃗ = ε₀E⃗ + P⃗ = εE⃗ (P = polarization)
∇·B⃗ = 0
∇×E⃗ = -∂B⃗/∂t
∇×H⃗ = J_f + ∂D⃗/∂t where H⃗ = B⃗/μ₀ - M⃗ = B⃗/μ (M = magnetization)
Wave speed in medium: v = c/n = 1/√(με)

IMPORTANT ELECTROMAGNETIC THEOREMS

GAUSS'S LAW (DETAILED):
∮E⃗·dA⃗ = Q_enclosed/ε₀
Applications:
Spherical symmetry (point charge, sphere):
E = Q/(4πε₀r²) outside (r > R)
E = Qr/(4πε₀R³) inside uniform sphere

Cylindrical symmetry (infinite line charge):
E = λ/(2πε₀r) [λ = linear charge density]

Planar symmetry (infinite sheet):
E = σ/(2ε₀) [σ = surface charge density]
Between parallel plates: E = σ/ε₀ (fields add)

FARADAY'S LAW (DETAILED):
ε = -dΦ_B/dt where Φ_B = ∫B⃗·dA⃗
Lenz's Law: Induced current opposes change in flux (negative sign).
Motional EMF: ε = BvL (for rod moving in field)
For rotating loop: ε = NBAω sin(ωt) → AC generator
Self-inductance: ε = -L dI/dt, L = NΦ/I
Mutual inductance: ε₂ = -M dI₁/dt

AMPERE'S LAW (DETAILED):
∮B⃗·dL⃗ = μ₀I_enclosed
Applications:
Long straight wire: B = μ₀I/(2πr)
Solenoid (n turns/length): B = μ₀nI (inside), B ≈ 0 (outside)
Toroid (N turns, radius r): B = μ₀NI/(2πr) (inside), B = 0 (outside)

BIOT-SAVART LAW:
dB⃗ = (μ₀/4π) I dL⃗ × r̂/r²
Circular loop at center: B = μ₀I/(2R)
On axis of loop: B = μ₀IR²/(2(R²+z²)^(3/2))

POYNTING'S THEOREM (Energy in EM fields):
Energy density: u = ½ε₀E² + B²/(2μ₀)
Poynting vector: S⃗ = (1/μ₀) E⃗ × B⃗ [W/m² - energy flux]
∂u/∂t + ∇·S⃗ = -J⃗·E⃗ (energy conservation — Poynting's theorem)
Energy flows perpendicular to both E and B.
Radiation pressure: P = S/c (absorbing), P = 2S/c (reflecting)

UNIQUENESS THEOREM:
A solution to Maxwell's equations satisfying given boundary conditions is unique.
This means we only need to find ONE solution — it's the only one.

SUPERPOSITION PRINCIPLE:
Maxwell's equations are linear → fields from multiple sources simply add.
E_total = E₁ + E₂ + E₃ + ...

BOUNDARY CONDITIONS AT INTERFACES:
Normal components:
ε₁E₁ₙ = ε₂E₂ₙ (D normal is continuous if no surface charge)
B₁ₙ = B₂ₙ (B normal always continuous)
Tangential components:
E₁ₜ = E₂ₜ (E tangential always continuous)
H₁ₜ - H₂ₜ = K⃗ × n̂ (H tangential discontinuous by surface current K)
These determine reflection and refraction of EM waves.

SNELL'S LAW FROM MAXWELL'S EQUATIONS:
Applying boundary conditions at interface:
n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂ (Snell's law)
n = c/v = √(ε_r μ_r) ≈ √ε_r (for non-magnetic materials)
Fresnel equations give reflection and transmission amplitudes.
Brewster's angle: tan θ_B = n₂/n₁ (reflected wave completely polarized)
Total internal reflection: sin θ_c = n₂/n₁ (for n₁ > n₂)

RETARDED POTENTIALS:
EM fields propagate at speed c — effects are not instantaneous.
φ(r,t) = (1/4πε₀)∫ρ(r',t_r)/|r-r'| d³r', t_r = t - |r-r'|/c
A⃗(r,t) = (μ₀/4π)∫J⃗(r',t_r)/|r-r'| d³r'
The retarded time t_r accounts for finite propagation speed.

RADIATION:
Accelerating charges radiate EM waves.
Larmor formula: P = q²a²/(6πε₀c³) = μ₀q²a²/(6πc)
Power ∝ q² × a² (charge squared times acceleration squared)
Applications:
- Antenna: oscillating charges radiate radio waves.
- Synchrotron radiation: relativistic electrons in magnetic fields.
- Bremsstrahlung: electrons decelerated in matter produce X-rays.
- Cherenkov radiation: particle faster than light in medium.

ELECTROMAGNETIC SPECTRUM (with frequencies and uses):
Radio waves: f < 3 GHz, λ > 10 cm. AM/FM radio, TV, WiFi, radar.
Microwaves: 3 GHz - 300 GHz, λ = 1mm - 10 cm. Microwave ovens, 5G, satellite.
Infrared: 300 GHz - 430 THz, λ = 700nm - 1mm. Heat, night vision, remote controls.
Visible: 430 - 750 THz, λ = 400 - 700 nm. Human vision. ROY G BIV.
Ultraviolet: 750 THz - 30 PHz, λ = 10 - 400 nm. Tanning, sterilization.
X-rays: 30 PHz - 30 EHz, λ = 0.01 - 10 nm. Medical imaging, security.
Gamma rays: f > 30 EHz, λ < 0.01 nm. Nuclear reactions, cancer treatment.

GAUGE INVARIANCE:
EM fields unchanged by gauge transformation:
A⃗ → A⃗ + ∇λ, φ → φ - ∂λ/∂t
Lorenz gauge: ∇·A⃗ + (1/c²)∂φ/∂t = 0 → wave equations for potentials.
Coulomb gauge: ∇·A⃗ = 0 → useful in quantum optics.
Gauge invariance is fundamental — leads to charge conservation.

IMPORTANT FORMULAS SUMMARY:
- F = q(E⃗ + v⃗×B⃗) (Lorentz force)
- E = kq/r² = q/(4πε₀r²) (Coulomb's law)
- B = μ₀I/(2πr) (straight wire)
- B = μ₀nI (solenoid)
- ε = -dΦ_B/dt (Faraday's law)
- u = ½ε₀E² + B²/(2μ₀) (energy density)
- S⃗ = E⃗×B⃗/μ₀ (Poynting vector)
- c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) = 3×10⁸ m/s
- P = q²a²/(6πε₀c³) (Larmor radiation)
