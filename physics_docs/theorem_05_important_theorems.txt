IMPORTANT PHYSICS THEOREMS AND CONSERVATION LAWS
==================================================

NOETHER'S THEOREM (Emmy Noether, 1915)
"The most beautiful theorem in physics"

Statement: Every continuous symmetry of a physical system corresponds to a conserved quantity.

Symmetry → Conservation Law:
Time translation symmetry → Conservation of Energy
Spatial translation symmetry → Conservation of Linear Momentum
Rotational symmetry → Conservation of Angular Momentum
Gauge symmetry (EM) → Conservation of Electric Charge
Gauge symmetry (QCD) → Conservation of Color Charge

This theorem explains WHY conservation laws exist — they are consequences of symmetry.
It is the deepest result in theoretical physics.

Example: The laws of physics are the same today as yesterday (time translation symmetry) → energy is conserved. If the laws changed over time, energy would not be conserved.

---

CONSERVATION OF ENERGY:
The total energy of an isolated system remains constant.
E_total = KE + PE + thermal + chemical + nuclear + ... = constant
Work-Energy Theorem: W_net = ΔKE
For conservative forces: KE + PE = constant (mechanical energy conservation)
First Law of Thermodynamics: ΔU = Q - W
E = mc² (rest energy) — mass itself is a form of energy

---

CONSERVATION OF LINEAR MOMENTUM:
Total momentum of isolated system is conserved: Σp⃗ = constant (if F_ext = 0)
p⃗ = mv⃗ (non-relativistic), p⃗ = γmv⃗ (relativistic)
Impulse-momentum theorem: J⃗ = FΔt = Δp⃗
Applications: collisions, rocket propulsion, recoil

Rocket equation (Tsiolkovsky): Δv = v_e ln(m₀/m_f)
v_e = exhaust velocity, m₀ = initial mass, m_f = final mass

---

CONSERVATION OF ANGULAR MOMENTUM:
L⃗ = r⃗ × p⃗ = Iω⃗ = constant (if τ_ext = 0)
Applications:
- Figure skater: pulls arms in → I decreases → ω increases (L = Iω = constant)
- Gyroscope precession: τ = dL/dt → precession ω_p = τ/L = mgr/Iω
- Planetary orbits: Kepler's second law is conservation of angular momentum
- Atomic physics: orbital angular momentum quantized in units of ℏ

Spin angular momentum: S = ℏ√(s(s+1)), S_z = m_s ℏ
Total: J⃗ = L⃗ + S⃗ (vector addition of orbital + spin)

NEWTON'S LAWS OF MOTION

Newton's First Law (Law of Inertia):
A body remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net external force.
Defines inertial reference frames.

Newton's Second Law:
F⃗ = ma⃗ = dp⃗/dt (rate of change of momentum)
Valid in inertial frames only.
Relativistic: F⃗ = dp⃗/dt = d(γmv⃗)/dt

Newton's Third Law:
For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
F⃗_AB = -F⃗_BA
Forces always come in pairs, acting on DIFFERENT bodies.
This is why rockets work in vacuum — exhaust gas pushed back → rocket pushed forward.

Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation:
F = Gm₁m₂/r² (attractive, along line joining masses)
G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²
g = GM_E/R_E² = 9.8 m/s² (surface gravity)

---

KEPLER'S LAWS OF PLANETARY MOTION

Kepler's First Law: Planets move in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one focus.
Eccentricity e: e=0 (circle), 0<e<1 (ellipse), e=1 (parabola), e>1 (hyperbola)
Perihelion: closest point. Aphelion: farthest point.

Kepler's Second Law: A line from Sun to planet sweeps equal areas in equal times.
This is conservation of angular momentum: L = mr²ω = constant
When closer to Sun: moves faster (r smaller, ω larger).
At perihelion: v_max. At aphelion: v_min.
v_p r_p = v_a r_a (conservation of angular momentum)

Kepler's Third Law: T² ∝ r³ (square of period proportional to cube of semi-major axis)
T² = (4π²/GM) r³
For planets orbiting Sun: T²(years) = r³(AU)
Derived directly from Newton's law of gravity.

---

WORK-ENERGY THEOREM:
W_net = ΔKE = ½mv₂² - ½mv₁²
For constant force: W = F⃗·d⃗ = Fd cos θ
For variable force: W = ∫F⃗·dr⃗
Power: P = dW/dt = F⃗·v⃗
Conservative force: work done independent of path, ∮F⃗·dr⃗ = 0
Potential energy defined only for conservative forces: W = -ΔU

---

GAUSS'S THEOREM (DIVERGENCE THEOREM):
∮F⃗·dA⃗ = ∫∫∫ ∇·F⃗ dV
Relates surface integral to volume integral.
Used in deriving Gauss's law for EM from Coulomb's law.

STOKES' THEOREM:
∮F⃗·dL⃗ = ∫∫ (∇×F⃗)·dA⃗
Relates line integral to surface integral.
Used in deriving Faraday's law and Ampere's law in differential form.

WAVE THEOREMS AND PRINCIPLES

SUPERPOSITION PRINCIPLE:
When two or more waves overlap, the resultant displacement equals the algebraic sum.
Constructive interference: amplitudes add (path difference = nλ)
Destructive interference: amplitudes cancel (path difference = (n+½)λ)
Beats: f_beat = |f₁ - f₂|
Standing waves: nodes (zero displacement) and antinodes (maximum displacement)

HUYGENS' PRINCIPLE:
Every point on a wavefront acts as a source of secondary wavelets.
New wavefront = envelope of all secondary wavelets.
Explains: diffraction, refraction, reflection.

DOPPLER EFFECT:
f' = f(v ± v_observer)/(v ∓ v_source)
For light (relativistic Doppler): f' = f√((1-β)/(1+β)) for recession
Redshift z = (f_emit - f_obs)/f_obs = Δλ/λ
Hubble's law: v = H₀d (recession velocity proportional to distance)

PRINCIPLE OF SUPERPOSITION FOR FIELDS:
Total field = sum of fields from all sources.
E_total = ΣE_i (electric field)
B_total = ΣB_i (magnetic field)
Valid because Maxwell's equations are linear.

FLUID MECHANICS THEOREMS

ARCHIMEDES' PRINCIPLE:
Buoyant force = weight of fluid displaced.
F_b = ρ_fluid × V_submerged × g
Object floats if density < fluid density.
Object sinks if density > fluid density.
Neutral buoyancy if density = fluid density.

PASCAL'S PRINCIPLE:
Pressure applied to enclosed fluid is transmitted equally in all directions.
P = F/A (pressure)
Hydraulic press: F₁/A₁ = F₂/A₂ → F₂ = F₁(A₂/A₁)

BERNOULLI'S THEOREM:
For steady, incompressible, non-viscous flow along streamline:
P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant
High velocity → low pressure (explains lift on wings, Venturi effect)
Continuity equation: A₁v₁ = A₂v₂ (mass conservation)
Torricelli's theorem: v = √(2gh) (efflux velocity)

---

OPTICAL THEOREMS

FERMAT'S PRINCIPLE:
Light travels the path that takes the least time.
Explains: reflection (angle of incidence = angle of reflection), refraction (Snell's law), lens optics.
Equivalent to Huygens' principle.

MALUS'S LAW:
I = I₀ cos²θ (intensity after polarizer at angle θ to polarization direction)
Unpolarized light through polarizer → I = I₀/2

RAYLEIGH SCATTERING:
Scattering intensity ∝ 1/λ⁴ (strongly wavelength dependent)
Blue light (shorter λ) scattered much more than red.
Explains: blue sky, red sunset, white clouds.

---

QUANTUM THEOREMS

HEISENBERG UNCERTAINTY PRINCIPLE: (detailed in QM section)
Δx·Δp ≥ ℏ/2

PAULI EXCLUSION PRINCIPLE:
No two identical fermions can have the same quantum state.
Consequence of antisymmetric wavefunctions.
Explains: periodic table, white dwarfs, neutron stars, stability of matter.

EHRENFEST'S THEOREM:
⟨F⟩ = m d²⟨x⟩/dt² (expectation values obey classical equations)
Quantum mechanics reduces to classical mechanics for expectation values.
Explains why quantum particles can behave classically on large scales.

VIRIAL THEOREM:
For bound systems: ⟨KE⟩ = -½⟨PE⟩
For gravity (1/r potential): ⟨KE⟩ = -½⟨PE⟩
Total energy = ½⟨PE⟩ (negative — bound system)
For harmonic oscillator: ⟨KE⟩ = ⟨PE⟩

BELL'S THEOREM:
No local hidden variable theory can reproduce all predictions of quantum mechanics.
Any theory with local realism disagrees with QM for entangled particles.
Experimental tests confirm QM — nature is fundamentally non-local.

NUCLEAR PHYSICS THEOREMS

RADIOACTIVE DECAY LAW:
N(t) = N₀ e^(-λt) (exponential decay)
Half-life: T₁/₂ = ln2/λ = 0.693/λ
Mean lifetime: τ = 1/λ
Activity: A = λN = A₀e^(-λt)

Q-VALUE:
Q = (mass of reactants - mass of products) × c²
Q > 0: exothermic (energy released)
Q < 0: endothermic (energy absorbed, threshold required)

BINDING ENERGY PER NUCLEON:
BE/A peaks at iron-56 (most stable nucleus)
Nuclei lighter than Fe: energy released by fusion
Nuclei heavier than Fe: energy released by fission
This explains why both fusion and fission release energy.
