1. THE MHC (MAJOR HISTOCOMPATIBILITY COMPLEX)
----------------------------------------------
The MHC (called HLA — Human Leukocyte Antigen — in humans) is the most polymorphic
region of the human genome. Located on chromosome 6p21. ~4 Mb; >200 genes.
MHC class I and II proteins present peptides to T cells — central to adaptive immunity.

MHC Class I:
  Present on ALL nucleated cells + platelets (not RBCs).
  Structure: Alpha chain (encoded by MHC) + beta-2-microglobulin (chromosome 15).
  Peptide binding: 8–10 amino acid peptides from intracellular proteins (endogenous antigen).
  Presents to: CD8+ cytotoxic T cells.
  Function: "Self-surveillance" → virus-infected or cancerous cells express abnormal peptides → CD8+ kill.
  Genes: HLA-A, HLA-B, HLA-C.

MHC Class II:
  Present on antigen-presenting cells (APCs): Dendritic cells, macrophages, B cells, thymic epithelium.
  Structure: Alpha + Beta chain (both encoded by MHC).
  Peptide binding: 13–25 amino acid peptides from extracellular proteins (exogenous antigen).
  Presents to: CD4+ helper T cells.
  Genes: HLA-DR, HLA-DQ, HLA-DP.

HLA Polymorphism:
  HLA-B: >7,000 known alleles; HLA-A: >6,500; HLA-DRB1: >3,500. Most polymorphic human genes.
  Most polymorphism in peptide-binding groove → different antigens presented.
  Co-dominantly expressed: Both maternal and paternal alleles expressed → up to 6 class I + 12 class II specificities.
  Linkage disequilibrium: Alleles at HLA loci inherited in haplotype blocks (e.g., HLA-A3-B7-DR15).

2. ANTIGEN PROCESSING & PRESENTATION
---------------------------------------
MHC Class I Pathway (endogenous/cytosolic):
  Intracellular proteins → ubiquitin-tagged → 26S proteasome → peptides (7–30 aa) →
  TAP1/TAP2 (Transporter associated with Antigen Processing) → ER →
  Tapasin + Calnexin + Calreticulin + ERp57 → loading complex → optimal peptide loaded →
  beta-2-microglobulin + HLA heavy chain + peptide complex → Golgi → cell surface.
  Immunoproteasome: Inducible by IFN-gamma; altered cleavage specificity → better antigenic peptides.

Cross-presentation:
  DCs can load exogenous antigens onto MHC class I → prime CD8+ T cells against extracellular pathogens.
  Pathways: Retrograde transport from phagosome to cytosol → proteasome → TAP → MHC I.
    Or: Vacuolar pathway (protease + MHC I in endolysosome).
  Critical for: Anti-viral immunity, antitumour responses, vaccines.

MHC Class II Pathway (exogenous/endolysosomal):
  Extracellular proteins → endocytosis → endosomes (acidification) → lysosomal proteases
  (cathepsins B, D, L, S) → peptides (13–25 aa) → MHC II loaded in late endosomal compartment (MIIC).
  CLIP (Class II-associated Invariant chain Peptide): Blocks peptide binding groove until MIIC.
  HLA-DM: Chaperone; removes CLIP; facilitates optimal peptide loading.
  HLA-DO: Inhibits DM; expressed in B cells and thymus.

3. TRANSPLANTATION IMMUNOLOGY
--------------------------------
Rejection Mechanisms:
  Direct recognition: Recipient T cells recognise intact donor MHC (with donor peptides) on graft APCs.
    High precursor frequency T cells → main driver of acute rejection. "Missing self" NK pathway.
  Indirect recognition: Donor antigens shed from graft → processed by recipient APCs → peptides
    presented on recipient MHC → recipient T cell response. Drives chronic rejection.
  Hyperacute rejection: Pre-formed antibodies (ABO, HLA) → complement activation → vascular thrombosis → graft loss within minutes-hours.
  Acute rejection: T cell-mediated (cellular) within days-months. Responds to immunosuppression.
  Chronic rejection: Months-years; intimal hyperplasia; antibody-mediated (ABMR); fibrosis.

HLA Matching:
  More HLA matches → better graft survival. Full 10/10 match ideal (HLA-A, B, C, DRB1, DQ).
  Kidney transplant: DR mismatch most important. Each mismatch → reduced 5-year graft survival.
  Crossmatch: Recipient serum + donor lymphocytes → detect preformed anti-HLA antibodies.
    Positive crossmatch (cytotoxic) → contraindication to transplant.
  Virtual crossmatch: Compare recipient anti-HLA antibodies (specificity) vs donor HLA typing.
  DSAs (Donor-Specific Antibodies): Measured by Luminex; correlated with ABMR risk.

Immunosuppression:
  Induction: High-dose at time of transplant. IL-2 receptor antibodies (basiliximab, daclizumab);
    anti-thymocyte globulin (ATG) depletes T cells.
  Maintenance:
    Calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus/FK506, ciclosporin): Inhibit calcineurin → NFAT dephosphorylation
      fails → IL-2 transcription inhibited → T cell activation blocked.
    mTOR inhibitors (sirolimus, everolimus): Block IL-2 signalling → T cell proliferation.
    Antiproliferatives (mycophenolate mofetil): Blocks IMPDH → ↓ guanosine nucleotides → lymphocyte-specific.
    Corticosteroids: Broad anti-inflammatory; suppress cytokines.
  Rejection treatment: High-dose methylprednisolone; ATG; plasmapheresis (for ABMR); rituximab.

4. NK CELLS & INNATE LYMPHOID CELLS
--------------------------------------
Natural Killer (NK) Cells:
  Innate lymphocytes; no rearranged receptors; recognise "missing self" and "induced self."
  Missing self: Cells that lose MHC I (virus-infected, cancer) → NK cells kill.
    KIRs (Killer Immunoglobulin-like Receptors): Inhibitory KIRs recognise MHC I → inhibit NK.
    NKG2A/CD94: Inhibitory; recognises HLA-E.
    If MHC I lost → inhibitory signals disappear → NK cell activated.
  Induced self: Stress ligands (MICA/B, ULBPs) upregulated on transformed cells → bind NKG2D (activating receptor).
  Activation: NKG2D, NCRs (NKp30/44/46), NKp80, DNAM-1 → cytotoxicity + cytokine production.
  Cytotoxicity: Perforin + granzymes → apoptosis; ADCC via CD16/FcgammaRIII.
  KIR/HLA genetics: KIR gene content highly variable. KIR-HLA combinations influence NK alloreactivity,
    HIV resistance, pregnancy success, HSCT outcomes.

Innate Lymphoid Cells (ILCs):
  Parallel to T helper cell subsets but no antigen-specific receptor.
  ILC1: IFN-gamma; intracellular pathogens; parallel to Th1.
  ILC2: IL-4, IL-5, IL-13; helminths, allergic inflammation; parallel to Th2.
  ILC3: IL-17, IL-22; extracellular bacteria/fungi, gut immunity; parallel to Th17.
  NK cells: Cytotoxic ILC; parallel to CD8+ T cells.
  ILC2 in allergy: Activated by TSLP, IL-25, IL-33 from epithelial cells → Th2 cytokines → eosinophils, IgE.

5. ANTIBODY DIVERSITY GENERATION
-----------------------------------
V(D)J Recombination:
  Immunoglobulin (Ig) and TCR genes assembled from gene segments in lymphocyte development.
  Heavy chain: ~44 VH + ~27 DH + 6 JH segments → combinatorial diversity.
  Light chain (kappa): ~40 Vk + 5 Jk.
  RAG1/RAG2 recombinase: Creates DNA DSBs at RSS (recombination signal sequences);
    Artemis + Ku70/80 + DNA-PKcs: NHEJ repair; but imprecise → junctional diversity (N and P nucleotides).

Somatic Hypermutation (SHM):
  Activated B cells in germinal centres (GCs). AID (Activation-Induced Cytidine Deaminase)
  → deaminates cytosine → uracil in Ig variable regions → error-prone repair → mutations.
  Rate: ~10^-3 mutations/bp/division (10^6× background). Most mutations in CDR (Complementarity Determining Regions).
  Affinity maturation: B cells with higher-affinity BCRs outcompete others for T cell help → selected → memory + plasma cells.
  GC reaction: B cells cycle between dark zone (proliferation + SHM) and light zone (selection by FDC antigen + T helper cells).

Class Switch Recombination (CSR):
  AID-mediated; switches Ig constant region → changes antibody effector function.
  IgM → IgG (most abundant), IgA (mucosal), IgE (allergy/parasites).
  Cytokine signals: IL-4 → IgE/IgG1; IL-10 → IgA; IFN-gamma → IgG3; TGF-beta → IgA/IgG4.

6. COMPLEMENT GENETICS & DISEASE
-----------------------------------
Complement gene polymorphisms → many immune diseases.
  C3 polymorphisms: Slow vs Fast alleles affect complement activation strength.
  C4 copy number variation: C4A and C4B gene duplications. Low C4A → SLE risk.
  Factor H (CFH): Regulates alternative pathway. CFH Y402H variant → AMD (macular degeneration) risk; increased C3b deposition on RPE.
  MASP-2: Component of lectin pathway. Deficiency → recurrent infections.
  C3 deficiency: Recurrent encapsulated bacteria (Streptococcus pneumoniae, Haemophilus influenzae, Neisseria meningitidis).
  Terminal complement (C5-C9) deficiency: Specifically prone to Neisseria meningitidis.
  Paroxysmal nocturnal haemoglobinuria (PNH): PIGA mutation → absent GPI-anchored CD55/CD59 on RBCs
    → uncontrolled complement → haemolysis.
    Eculizumab (anti-C5): Transformed PNH treatment; prevents haemolysis.
    Ravulizumab (long-acting anti-C5): Monthly vs eculizumab's biweekly.

[FUTURE] HLA-matched cell therapy:
  iPSC-derived "universal donor" cell lines: CRISPR knockout HLA class I + II + B2M + CIITA;
  express NK-inhibitory ligands (HLA-E with single peptide) → avoid both T cell and NK cell rejection.
  Being developed for: Universal iPSC-derived cardiomyocytes, islets, NK cells, CAR-T cells.
  Eliminates need for immunosuppression if successful. Early clinical trials expected 2025–2028.
