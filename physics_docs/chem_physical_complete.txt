CHAPTER 1: ATOMIC SPECTRA AND QUANTUM NUMBERS

HYDROGEN SPECTRUM:
When H atoms are excited and emit light, only specific wavelengths are emitted.
Rydberg formula: 1/λ = R_H(1/n₁² - 1/n₂²)
R_H = 1.097 × 10⁷ m⁻¹ (Rydberg constant)
n₁ < n₂ (n₁ = lower level, n₂ = upper level)

Spectral Series:
Lyman: n₁=1 (UV, 91-122 nm). n₂=2,3,4,...
Balmer: n₁=2 (Visible, 365-656 nm). n₂=3,4,5,...
Paschen: n₁=3 (near IR, 820 nm - 1.9 μm). n₂=4,5,...
Brackett: n₁=4 (mid IR). n₂=5,6,...
Pfund: n₁=5 (far IR). n₂=6,7,...

Visible Balmer lines:
Hα (n=3→2): 656 nm (red)
Hβ (n=4→2): 486 nm (blue-green)
Hγ (n=5→2): 434 nm (violet)
Hδ (n=6→2): 410 nm (violet)

IONIZATION ENERGY:
Energy to remove electron from ground state to infinity.
For hydrogen: IE = 13.6 eV = 2.18 × 10⁻¹⁸ J = 1312 kJ/mol.
For hydrogen-like ions: IE = 13.6 Z²/n² eV (Z = atomic number, n = shell).
He⁺: Z=2, n=1: IE = 13.6 × 4 = 54.4 eV.

ELECTRON AFFINITY:
Energy released when neutral atom gains electron.
EA: F(-333) > Cl(-349) > Br(-325) > I(-296) kJ/mol. Cl has highest EA (not F!).
Unusual: O and N have lower EA than S and P (electron repulsion in small atoms).

CHAPTER 2: CHEMICAL BONDING - ADVANCED

MOLECULAR ORBITAL DIAGRAMS:
H₂: σ1s² → Bond order 1.
He₂: σ1s²σ*1s² → Bond order 0 (doesn't exist).
Li₂: ...σ2s² → Bond order 1.
Be₂: ...σ2s²σ*2s² → Bond order 0.
B₂: ...π2p¹π2p¹ → Bond order 1, paramagnetic.
C₂: ...π2p²π2p² → Bond order 2, diamagnetic.
N₂: ...π2p²π2p²σ2p² → Bond order 3 (triple bond, very stable, diamagnetic).
O₂: ...σ2p²π2p²π2p²π*2p¹π*2p¹ → Bond order 2, paramagnetic (2 unpaired e⁻).
F₂: ...π*2p²π*2p² (added to O₂ config) → Bond order 1.
Ne₂: Bond order 0.

Key MO insight: O₂ has 2 unpaired electrons → paramagnetic (attracted to magnet).
This was predicted by MO theory but not by Lewis structure!

RESONANCE STRUCTURES:
Benzene: two Kekulé structures + Dewar structures.
CO₃²⁻: three equivalent resonance structures. All C-O bonds equal.
NO₃⁻: three equivalent resonance structures.
SO₄²⁻: resonance involving d-orbital participation.
Resonance stabilization energy = actual stability - stability without resonance.

DIPOLE MOMENT:
μ = q × d (C·m or Debye, 1D = 3.336 × 10⁻³⁰ C·m)
Vector quantity — from + to - (chemistry convention) or - to + (physics).
Zero dipole: linear symmetric (CO₂), tetrahedral symmetric (CH₄, CCl₄), benzene.
Non-zero: H₂O (1.85D), NH₃ (1.47D), HCl (1.08D), HF (1.91D).
Uses: determines polarity, determines solubility, microwave absorption.

CHAPTER 3: SOLID STATE CHEMISTRY

CRYSTAL SYSTEMS:
7 crystal systems based on unit cell dimensions and angles:
Cubic: a=b=c, α=β=γ=90° (most symmetric)
Tetragonal: a=b≠c, α=β=γ=90°
Orthorhombic: a≠b≠c, α=β=γ=90°
Hexagonal: a=b≠c, α=β=90°, γ=120°
Trigonal: a=b=c, α=β=γ≠90°
Monoclinic: a≠b≠c, α=γ=90°, β≠90°
Triclinic: a≠b≠c, α≠β≠γ≠90° (least symmetric)

CUBIC UNIT CELLS:
Simple Cubic (SC): 1 atom/unit cell. Coordination number = 6. Packing = 52%. Only polonium.
Body-centered Cubic (BCC): 2 atoms/unit cell. CN = 8. Packing = 68%. Fe, Na, K, Mo.
Face-centered Cubic (FCC/CCP): 4 atoms/unit cell. CN = 12. Packing = 74%. Cu, Al, Ag, Au, Ni.

Relationship: a (edge length), r (radius):
SC: a = 2r. BCC: a = 4r/√3. FCC: a = 2√2 r.
Density: ρ = nM/(Nₐ × a³) (n = atoms/cell, M = molar mass, a = edge length)

CLOSE PACKING:
HCP (hexagonal close packed): ABABAB stacking. CN = 12. Same packing as FCC.
CCP (cubic close packed = FCC): ABCABC stacking.
Both have 74% packing efficiency.
Octahedral voids: n per unit cell in CCP. Radius ratio = 0.414.
Tetrahedral voids: 2n per unit cell. Radius ratio = 0.225.

IONIC CRYSTALS:
NaCl structure (rock salt): FCC anion lattice, Na⁺ in all octahedral voids. CN = 6:6.
ZnS (zinc blende): FCC S²⁻, Zn²⁺ in half tetrahedral voids. CN = 4:4.
CsCl: SC with Cs⁺ in body center. CN = 8:8.
Fluorite (CaF₂): FCC Ca²⁺, F⁻ in all tetrahedral voids. CN = 8:4.
Antifluorite: reverse of fluorite. Na₂O structure.

DEFECTS IN CRYSTALS:
Point defects:
Vacancy defect: missing atom/ion from lattice point. Decreases density.
Interstitial defect: extra atom in interstitial position. Increases density.
Impurity defect: foreign atom in lattice.
Frenkel defect: cation moves from lattice to interstitial. No density change. ZnS, AgCl.
Schottky defect: equal cation and anion vacancies. Decreases density. NaCl, KCl, CsCl.
F-centers: anion vacancy filled with electron → colored crystals (e.g., NaCl in Na vapor → yellow).
n-type semiconductor: extra electrons (Si doped with P, As).
p-type semiconductor: electron holes (Si doped with B, Al).

Mg²⁺/Mg: -2.37V
Al³⁺/Al: -1.66V
Zn²⁺/Zn: -0.76V
Fe²⁺/Fe: -0.44V
Ni²⁺/Ni: -0.25V
Sn²⁺/Sn: -0.14V
Pb²⁺/Pb: -0.13V
H⁺/H₂: 0.00V (reference)
Cu²⁺/Cu: +0.34V
Ag⁺/Ag: +0.80V
Hg²⁺/Hg: +0.85V
Au³⁺/Au: +1.50V
F₂/F⁻: +2.87V (strongest oxidizing agent)

MnO₄⁻/Mn²⁺: +1.51V (acidic)
Cr₂O₇²⁻/Cr³⁺: +1.33V (acidic)

GALVANIC CELLS:
Weston standard cell: Cd amalgam|CdSO₄||CdSO₄(sat.)|Hg₂SO₄|Hg. E = 1.0183V.
Concentration cell: E = (RT/nF) ln(C₂/C₁) — potential from concentration difference.

ELECTROLYSIS:
Electrolysis of water: 2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂
Cathode: 2H₂O + 2e⁻ → H₂ + 2OH⁻ (or 2H⁺ + 2e⁻ → H₂)
Anode: 2H₂O → O₂ + 4H⁺ + 4e⁻
Electrolysis of NaCl solution: cathode: H₂, anode: Cl₂, solution: NaOH.

Discharge order at cathode (reduction): ion with higher reduction potential discharged first.
Discharge order at anode (oxidation): Cl⁻ > OH⁻ > SO₄²⁻ > NO₃⁻ (concentration matters too).

Electroplating: object = cathode, metal to deposit = anode.
Refining: impure metal = anode, pure metal = cathode, metal salt solution = electrolyte.

CHAPTER 5: SURFACE CHEMISTRY

ADSORPTION:
Accumulation of substance on surface of another.
Adsorbate: substance adsorbed. Adsorbent: surface on which adsorption occurs.
Physisorption: physical adsorption, van der Waals forces, reversible, multi-layer, low Eₐ.
Chemisorption (activated adsorption): chemical bond formation, irreversible, mono-layer, high Eₐ.
Factors affecting: nature of adsorbent/adsorbate, surface area, temperature, pressure.

FREUNDLICH ADSORPTION ISOTHERM:
x/m = k·P^(1/n) (1/n between 0 and 1)
log(x/m) = log k + (1/n)log P → straight line.

LANGMUIR ADSORPTION ISOTHERM:
Assumes monolayer, fixed adsorption sites, no interaction between adsorbed molecules.
θ = bP/(1+bP) (θ = fraction of surface covered)

CATALYSIS:
Homogeneous catalysis: H⁺ catalysis (esterification, hydrolysis), NO in SO₂ oxidation.
Heterogeneous catalysis: Fe (Haber process), V₂O₅ (Contact process), Ni (hydrogenation), Pt (catalytic converter).
Autocatalysis: product itself acts as catalyst. KMnO₄ + H₂C₂O₄ (oxalic acid) — Mn²⁺ catalyzes.

COLLOIDS:
Dispersion of particles (1-1000 nm) in a medium.
Types: sol (solid in liquid), gel (liquid in solid), emulsion (liquid in liquid), aerosol (solid/liquid in gas), foam (gas in liquid).
Tyndall effect: scattering of light by colloidal particles (distinguishes colloid from true solution).
Brownian motion: random motion of colloidal particles.
Charge on colloids: responsible for stability (same charge → repulsion → no coagulation).
Coagulation: destabilization of colloid by adding electrolyte. Hardy-Schulze rule: higher charge of ion → more effective coagulation.
Gold number: minimum amount of protective colloid (gelatin, starch) to prevent coagulation of 10 mL red gold sol.
Preparation: dispersion (grinding, peptization) or condensation (chemical reaction, change of solvent).

EMULSIONS:
O/W emulsion: oil drops in water (milk, cold cream). O/W emulsifier: soap, proteins.
W/O emulsion: water drops in oil (butter, cold cream). W/O emulsifier: heavy metal soaps.
Demulsification: centrifugation, adding electrolyte, heating.

CHAPTER 6: NUCLEAR CHEMISTRY

RADIOACTIVITY:
Spontaneous disintegration of unstable nuclei.
Types:
α decay: ²³⁸U → ²³⁴Th + ⁴He (A-4, Z-2)
β⁻ decay: ¹⁴C → ¹⁴N + e⁻ + ν̄ (A same, Z+1) [n → p + e⁻ + ν̄]
β⁺ decay: ²²Na → ²²Ne + e⁺ + ν (A same, Z-1) [p → n + e⁺ + ν]
γ emission: no change in A or Z, just energy release.
Electron capture: ⁷Be + e⁻ → ⁷Li + ν (A same, Z-1) [p + e⁻ → n + ν]

Radioactive Decay Law: N = N₀e^(-λt)
Half-life: t₁/₂ = 0.693/λ
Radiocarbon dating: ¹⁴C t₁/₂ = 5730 years. Age = (t₁/₂/0.693) × ln(A₀/A).

NUCLEAR REACTIONS:
Q-value: Q = [m_reactants - m_products]c²
Positive Q: exothermic (energy released). Negative Q: endothermic.

Fission: ²³⁵U + n → ⁹⁰Kr + ¹⁴³Ba + 3n + 200 MeV.
Chain reaction: controlled (reactor) or uncontrolled (bomb).
Fusion: ²H + ³H → ⁴He + n + 17.6 MeV. Requires ~10⁸ K.
Mass-energy: E = mc², 1 amu = 931.5 MeV.

APPLICATIONS:
Medical: ¹³¹I (thyroid cancer), ⁶⁰Co (radiation therapy), ⁹⁹ᵐTc (medical imaging).
Dating: ¹⁴C (organic materials, up to 50,000 years), ²³⁸U (rocks, millions of years).
Power generation: nuclear reactors.
Food irradiation: ⁶⁰Co γ-rays kill bacteria.

NUCLEAR STABILITY:
Stable nuclei have N/Z ratio close to 1 for light elements, up to 1.5 for heavy elements.
Magic numbers: 2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126 (especially stable — like noble gas configuration).
Even-even nuclei most stable (even protons AND neutrons).
Odd-odd nuclei least stable.
Belt of stability in N vs Z plot.
